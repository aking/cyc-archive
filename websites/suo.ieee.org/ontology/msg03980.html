| Arabesque, en [ah na-ra-BESK].
| In arabesque, that is, in an arabesque position.
| As, for example, in pirouette en arabesque.
|
| Arabesque, à deux bras [a-ra-BESK a duh brah].
| Arabesque with two arms.  This arabesque is taken
| in profile with the extended leg nearest the audience.
| Both arms are extended forward with the arm on the side
| of the supporting leg held slightly higher.  The head
| may be held in profile or turned to the audience.
|
| Gail Grant,
|'Technical Manual And Dictionary Of Classical Ballet',
| Third Revised Edition, Dover, New York, NY, 1982.
