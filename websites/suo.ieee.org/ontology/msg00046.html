To Whom It May Concern Or Not (TWIMCON),

Comments that I have received, both on and off List,
from members of the SUO group, make me feel that it
is necessary to address the following questions,
which seem to be explicit or implicit in the
remarks that were made to me:

1.  What is my stake in the SUO project?
2.  What's all this fuss about history?
3.  Why so many irrelevent links?

I will address first things first, and save the rest
for other notes, other times, except insofar as some
aspects of them may bear upon the immediate business.

My stake is this:  I believe that Society deserves to be served
by software that can help it to solve -- if not yet solve, then
at least to cope with and to deal with to some degree, the many
critical problems that it faces on everyday and long-run bases.

In trying to design the needed software, my sense of aesthetics,
basic pride of craftsmanship, and overriding duty of work ethic
bears on me in a number of rather acute ways:  I would not want
to build software that I myself would never use, on account of
a substandard construction or due to an utter superfluousness
in regard to the potential for applications.

Accordingly, I would not want to be a party to building or to canonizing
a sort of software that is "State Of The Art" (SOTA) 1960, any more than
I want to go on using logical systems that are SOTA 1860, as most of the
formulations of logic in current use, CG partly excepted, presently are.

That is the general part of my "statement of interest" (SOI).
I believe that I can safely leave the specifics until later,
and so I will give it a rest, for now.

Sincerely Yours,

Jon Awbrey
