pat hayes wrote:
> 
> > [Frank Farance, to Bob Spillers:]
> >
> > It is contradiction to state
> > "wider participants will produce lower adoption"
> > and then "lower participation gets wider adoption".
> 
> Well, just a technical niggle, but its not a contradiction.
> It's just saying that participation and adoption will have
> a Galois relationship.  For example, suppose that participation
> in attempting to construct the SUO produces a strong impression,
> among many of those who participate, that the complexity of
> conforming to such an oppressively complex and relatively
> useless standard isnt worth the effort of compliance.
>
> In other words, suppose that participation in the
> process itself constitutes a rather sharp education
> in the philosophical complexity of ontological practice,
> sharp enough to overcome the rather naive, starry-eyed
> optimism which seems to motivate many SUO adherents.
>
> Then this kind of Galois relationship might indeed hold.
> (One might draw an analogy with the relationship between
> gung-ho patriotism at the beginning of WW1 and the amount
> of enthusiasm for continued participation in trench warfare
> among the veterans.)
>
