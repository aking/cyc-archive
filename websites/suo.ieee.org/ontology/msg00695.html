Jon-

KIF 3.0 does contain elaborate mechanisms for quotation (and 
quasi-quotation), but these were removed from the SUO-KIF core 
deliberately as they were felt to be too exotic for everyday use, and 
they greatly complicate the semantics.  However, there is still a 
proposal (details being thrashed out) for a 'meta-extension' to 
SUO-KIF which will include both string quotation and structural 
quotation, providing the ability to define recursive operations on 
the language's own syntax.  It will also have a truth-predicate which 
comes as close to the Goedelian cliff as one ever wants to get. I 
believe this will provide you with all the meta-syntactic power you 
could hope to get.

LISP 1.5 allows a lot of self-description; but it doesnt give you any 
quantifiers. Recursion and self-description is a pretty heady 
mixture, but adding quantification to self-description is a bit like 
mixing diesel oil and weedkiller.

>I will comment on its logical incapacities,
>from the standpoint of practical efficiency,
>a lack that is hardly any special fault of it,
>since it merely shares in the degraded condition
>of the logical languages that a certain tradition
>has seen fit to devolve "on us", but another time.

Well, if you have any better ideas, please do share them with us as 
soon as you can manage it.  There are known limitations on deductive 
efficiency for any reasonably expressive languages, as Im sure you 
know, which cannot be blamed on mere traditional stubbornness.
