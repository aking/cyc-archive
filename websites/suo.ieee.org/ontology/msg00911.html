>So what do we do about it?

Well, Im not sure what I can do about it, but Niles is at 
Teknowledge, and they ought to have access to some software that can 
manipulate onotlogies in an organized way. At least that is what Adam 
was insisting at the KIF meetings. (Cyc uses 'microtheories' a lot, 
which also provides some of this kind of structuring ability.)

Another way to go might be to follow the DAML+OIL initiative and use 
web URIs to refer to ontologies on web pages, the idea being that 
such a reference 'includes' all the axioms from that page (and their 
associated vocabulary) into your ontology. DAML+OIL is still a rather 
restricted language compared to KIF, but we could utilise this idea: 
no deep reason why KIF identifiers couldnt be URI's.
