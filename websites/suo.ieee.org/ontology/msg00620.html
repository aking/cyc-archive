I don't see that these implications can be drawn from my comments, and my
reasoning follows.

> 
>  - it is alright if the SUO is inconsistent and patchy

I think the final SUO standard would be a failure if it were contradictory
and/or patchy.  However, it seems to me to be an inescapable fact of life
that the first few drafts of an initial working paper are going to contain
some inconsistencies and gaps.  My hope is that we can iron out the
contradictions and fill in the gaps through many iterations of
comment/response.

>  - it is alright if we can't answer the question "why does it 
> look like this"

But we can explain why the current version of the merged ontology looks the
way it does.  I have distributed on several occasions the methodology that I
followed in putting together the first two versions of the ontology.
Changes to these versions will be a product of iterations of
comment/response, and there will always be the list archive to explain why
any particular change or addition was made to the ontology.  Aside from all
of this, it seems to be that the main justification of any engineering
artifact, including an ontology, is a pragmatic one.  If it allows you to
accomplish something in less time or at a reduced cost, then that's why the
engineering artifact looks the way it does.

>  - it is alright if we have no philosophical foundation at all

I'm not sure what you mean by a "philosophical foundation".  If you mean a
metaphysics/epistemology that catalogues the primitive entities in the
world, analyzes complex concepts in terms of these primitive entities, and
shows how we can mentally apprehend these entities and concepts, that's
certainly something that would be nice to have.  However, it is unreasonable
to expect that we will be able to develop such a metaphysics/epistemology in
this context.  There is no agreement within the philosophical community on
any proposition within metaphysics or epistemology, and I think it would be
the height of hubris to claim that we're going to make progress where Plato,
Descartes, Wittgenstein, et al failed.
