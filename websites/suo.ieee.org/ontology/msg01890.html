MW: We are talking about informal material here so a theory would be
a bit over the top. Excluded simply means "not dealt with here" with no
commitment as to whether it will or will not be dealt with elsewhere.

Included means it is dealt with here and it would be axiomatized.

If it is referred to, I would expect either a reference to where it is
axiomatised, or a "to be done" acknowledgement.

However, I would accept that this much is a high standard to expect of 
an initial document (I can ask though).

MW: I am really reflecting engineering design practice of developing a 
specification before building the product. If we are going to develop
the SUO in anything like a reasonable time we will have to build it in 
modules, where the modules are developed in parallel.

So hypothetically taking what you have done as an example, there should 
have been a short proposal outlining what you were going to do and its
boundaries/interfaces. Others might do the same in other areas, and thus
reduce the risk of duplicating the work of others, whilst being aware of 
its general shape.

I know I might seem over-critical, but this is actually because what you 
have done is sufficiently close to what I think we ought to be doing
that I think the gaps are worth drawing attention to.
