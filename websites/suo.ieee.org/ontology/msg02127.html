Graham,

Yes, I have also been recommending controlled languages for
a long time.

>While what you say is true, It does not relate to what I was
>suggesting for the SUO.  

>Also, I believe your statements exaggerate the extent to
>which a carefully thought out and appropriately restricted and modified
>version of Standard English cannot be developed for programming, database
>development and more generalised computer - human dialogue, etc. 

I believe that such tools can be very useful, but I want to
distinguish them from the kind of hype that keeps appearing
in press releases.  That kind of hype is one of the greatest
obstacles to getting people to take controlled languages
seriously.

>I feel you are quoting hyped up "press release"
>overstatements to exaggerate up your position. Who would believe a newspaper

>or other journal article with that sort of language anyway? 

Unfortunately, such hype tends to be generated at least as
often by the developers of the tools as by the journalists.
In those two quotations I cited, the journalists may have
written the words, but they were seriously misled by the
people they interviewed.

John Sowa
