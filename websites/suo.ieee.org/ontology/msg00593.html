Martin, John, etc.,
.              This looks like solid progress.
MK> Delighted you think so
.    The ideas of having "generic relationship concept" ("relationship type"
in normal data modelling parlance), and "relationship instance" (just
"relationship" in normal data modelling parlance) make good sense, and fit
in well with the normal data modelling terminology many people use. It does,
however, mean that the traditional "entity - relationship diagram (ERD)" now
becomes an "entity - relationship type diagram".

MK>  I believe the most natural interpretation of most ERD diagramming
conventions each graphic element coresponds with something at the type
level.  This a typical context in which this is so obvious, that nobody
bothers to qualify the word relationship (or the word entity) with the word
type.  So ERD always has meant "entity type - relationship type diagram",
but we did not bother to say so, and I suggest can still leave it implied by
the context..

GH>  I understand perfectly where you are coming from. However, It is pretty
well exactly this sort of assumed obviousness that led to this thread. What
we need to come up with for the SUO standard, as with any international
standard, is language that says EVERYTHING explicitly (or refers the reader
to another standard, or possibly other reference document, that does).

MK>> I agree a formal SUO must not use a term to mean two different things.
Therefore if we choose to define Relationship as an SUO term, we must choose
a particular meaning for it.  I would like this meaning to be "in the real
world".  In the Enterprise Ontology NL version, we actually wrote sentences
with the word Relationship which I interpret as statements about
relationship instances, but (by what I call sleight of hand), these
sentences by implication define the properties of the relationship type 

GH>>	This sounds OK to me for now. If it runs into problems later, there
may be a need to extend it. 

GH>>	Incidentally, did you find yourselves restricting your vocabulary in
any ways for the Enterprise Ontology NL version (ie. making it a de facto
"restricted English" version)?
