Jim, 
	.	So long as you have his stated "willingness to allow SUO to
do this" in writing, you already have that release implicitly. 

	.	A problem is that that would provide shared copyright. I
seem to recall we wanted to make the SUO available without charge to users,
or something along that line. 

	.	We would therefore also require his acknowledgement that he
realises that, and agrees to it. Again, this could be implicit already if he
stated his "willingness to allow SUO to do this" after that issue was
raised. If so I would still recommend advising him of intention to proceed
along those lines, and inviting him to advise if he has any objections. This
should be naturally done by snail mail - registered post, or other secure
transmission technology, so that receipt by him can be confirmed. 

	.	A general approach in such circumstances is to provide a
deadline for response, and provide an additional but unstated time buffer as
a concession to allow for out of course delays for any reply transmission.
