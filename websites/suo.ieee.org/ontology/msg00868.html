Pat Hayes said:

Your current methodology takes the upper level as 'fixed'
(modifiable, but at every stage it is the skeleton) and fits lower
levels into it, which I think is likely to be the wrong way around at
this stage; I would rather see a collection of 'useful' mid-level
ontologies first, and then an organized attempt to design a coherent
upper level into which they could be fitted.

I AGREE WHOLEHEARTEDLY. I have said this before, but I'll repeat again
since it was brought up again. We verysuccessfully use this approch,
which we called middle-out in building the Enterprise Ontology.  If we
start with things that we all know we want in the ontology first, it may
be easier to reach agreement than it will be to agree on upper level things
first.
