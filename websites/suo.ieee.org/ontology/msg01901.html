I agree with John and Chris below.

As Deputy Convenor of ISO TC184/SC4/WG10 and current head of UK 
delegation to ISO TC184/SC4 I am probably one of the more
experienced standards developers here. So I will give my 2 cents
worth on when a vote on a document is justified. I think there
are just two (good) reasons:

1. A document has reached a level of maturity and completeness
where a formal review is required to assess consensus and to 
identify issues against the document that require resolution
before it could be issued as a standard.

2. A member of the standards community has the opportunity to
obtain funding to do some standardisation work, and some 
recognition of what he is proposing to do is supportive in 
helping him to get that funding.

I have certainly seen votes on other occasions, and as I see
it the value has been nill (well actually negative because of 
the unnecessary energy consumed - we have a case in point here).
On the other hand the only real damage done has been the
unnecessary energy consumed. In the end everything important
will have to go through a series of type (1) votes.

In the current situation (1) clearly doesn't apply with Ian's 
document. If (2) does then he should let some of the more
prominent members here know, either openly on this list, or
offlist if he is shy about these things. I would certainly be
happy to give my support in these circumstances to something
suitably worded.

I would encourage everyone here to remember that we are probably
all learning how to do this, and mistakes are innevitable. On the
other hand I don't think we should pretend that mistakes haven't 
been made. It would be nice to find a way to draw a line under 
what has passed and move on.
