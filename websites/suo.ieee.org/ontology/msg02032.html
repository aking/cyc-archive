This makes good sense to me.
What I hear Matthew indicating is a preference akin to
a preference for a normalized database.
http://luna.pepperdine.edu/gsbm/class/ckettemborough/DBNorm.html 


See, e.g., the "Non-Normalized Relation" example on
http://www.albany.edu/acc/gangolly/ssa6.html


Normalization gives you a lot of good things,
such as no insertion, deletion, or update anomalies.
http://www.microsoft.com/TechNet/Access/technote/ac101.asp

In general, with the lower arity relations and normalization, you 
tend to get "one fact in one place".

That said, I do personally view ternary relations (at least) as
desirable.
