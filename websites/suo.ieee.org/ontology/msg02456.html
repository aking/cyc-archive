Josiane,

Once again I find myself torn.  I personally believe that the
problem-solving aspect of inquiry, the propositional attitude
appropriate to it, the intellectual virtues demanded by it,
whether of the artificial agents that we envision or even
of ourselves as we approach this work, not to mention all
of the empirical givens that we might gather about it,
are all very pertinent to the long-term goals of the
SUO working group.  And yet there has been all of
this "ack-ack" -- a negative "acknowledgement"? --
about "too much Peirce" in recent days, and so
I will make an extra effort to be concise.

If the topic turns too specifically "Peircerei",
and if even I fail to see any wider application --
which is seldom, I must admit! -- then you might
think of joining the Arisbe list and we could
discuss it more at length there:

http://stderr.org/cgi-bin/mailman/listinfo/arisbe

My only problem with the SemiCom List is that it appears
to be closed to external search engines, which I regard
not only as a bit of a block to inquiry but also as an
obstacle to finding old correspondence even a few weeks
later.

But, for the moment, I will contiunue to operate on the
assumption that the topic of semiotics, in general, the
insights of pragmatic semiotics -- which adds the maxim
of pragmatism that is the grandmother of the principle of
"operational definition" -- in particular, and all of the
trying and erring lessons that AI and cognitive science
have learned since the days of the "state space approach"
and the "General Problem Solver" (GPS, the Elder), that
all of this is very relevant to our efforts to achieve
a comptutationally implemented conceptual framework
for intercomm and interops among diverse belief
systems and putative knowledge bases.

To specifics.  The paragraph that you quote is
a very tricky one.  If taken at its ebb it can
lead on to a form of nominalistic reductionism,
which is why I deliberately mention the analogy
to perspective drawing (Desargues vs. Descartes),
as if to say, watch out! a 2-dim representation
of a 3-dim reality can be useful if read right,
but it is never the whole picture.

I have to do a bit of work around the house today --
to plant a lilac bush and a plum tree in the yard --
and so it may be a while before I can get together
a sampler of links on the "Sign Relations" thread.
You can find some by searching the SUO archive,
using either <find in page> or the FAST engine,
which allows you to specify a URL and different
types of filters and keys:

http://suo.ieee.org/email

Talk to you later,
