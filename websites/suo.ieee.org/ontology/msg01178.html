GH>	I have actually raised the issue of the intensity of discussion, and

GH>	More recently we have had some pretty intense discussions about some
of the technical aspects. My perspective in hindsight is that much, but NOT
ALL, of this discussion grew out of misunderstandings between what one

we don't have a structure for co-ordinating contributions on such matters in

individually. I am sure this has drained the group's resources
unnecessarily. Having said that, I have to admit I am not an expert in
developing this sort of structure, though I do have a gut feel that there

GH>	Ah! I think the difference is that we have more flexibility here
than the average car purchase. To take the analogy further I suggest we are
more akin to a Formula 1 racing car team.  
*	We have the opportunity to modify the car quite significantly, as
participants identify shortcomings, using the team resources. 
*	We can also throw away, or completely restructure, the car, or
significant parts of it, the moment we find some major design flaw or
potential major beneficial alternative that vastly improves the quality
(taking the holistic approach: eg. scope, flexibility, performance, user
friendliness, etc). With commercially produced cars these are called new
models. With commercial off the shelf (COTS) software these are called new
versions. In both cases, some are minor upgrades, while others are complete
re-engineerings of the product; 
*	In our case, the effort expended is not wasted, since we learn
lessons from it, hopefully one of our project documentation items will be
some form of record of the changes made and the reasonings involved, so that
any subsequent revisiting of an issue is informed by the previous experience
rather than needing to rediscover the issues involved all over again;
