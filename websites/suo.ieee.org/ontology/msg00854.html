> I don't know what Fred has in mind (or what his gnostic emails are 
> supposed to mean), but one technical objection to nth-domain is that 
> it uses integers, and integers are not formalized in the KIF core; 
> and, more substantially, integers cannot be fully defined in any 
> compact logic with an RE proof predicate (because of Godel's second 
> theorem.). So to rely on a non-axiomatisable concept to define the 
> basic syntactic entities of the core language seems like a bad idea, 
> rather like shooting oneself in the foot; particularly as there is 
> absolutely no need to do so. For example, we could introduce separate 
> predicates for 1st-domain, 2nd-domain, etc. - we will almost 
> certainly never need more than, say, ten of them - or we could adopt 
> an entirely different sorting declaration in which the 'domains' are 
> all declared at once. I will submit a detailed proposal along these 
> lines in a few days.
