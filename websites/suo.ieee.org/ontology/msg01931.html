Draft of motion for adoption by general (unanimous) consent

Motion:  Request for adoption by general (unanimous) consent.

Resolved: Any voting member of the IEEE SUO Working Group may post a document to the SUO web page as an "Expert contribution1".  The member who posts a document is presumptively the technical editor2 of the document and by posting agrees to perform these duties or alternatively name another member (either voting or candidate member3) who agrees to perform the duties of technical editor.  No vote will be required to post a document or name an editor.

The statement  "All documents posted are deemed to have equal status and no document has any preferred position over any other document." will precede the list of documents.  No separate category of documents may be created without an explicit vote of the members.
 

1   This is a name suggested by John Sowa and I think it is an appropriate one.  It seems the IEEE SUO web page (suo.ieee.org) was changed today (3/27/01) to eliminate the heading "Document being worked on by this Working Group" - the heading where the Teknowledge Merged Ontology was previously listed.  The Teknowledge Merged Ontology is now listed (along with David Whitten's structural ontology) under "Candidate Content to be merged into a standard".  This is probably not a good choice as there may be submissions by members who do not wish their contribution to be merged with other material. The material submitted might not be content (in the sense of nodes, relations, axioms, etc.) but architecture, structural considerations, tutorial materials, tools, etc.    My main concern is not the name; it is that there be no difference in status of any documents that are or will be listed.

2  I think it would be appropriate to list the duties of a technical editor in a footnote to the motion.  Some obvious duties occur to me (maintaining a current version, logging issues, facilitating the resolution of issues etc.), but I would appreciate suggestions.

3  I have tried to accommodate John Sowa's (and others) suggestions about the types of membership and their privileges regarding document submissions.  The motion requires a voting member to submit a document, either a voting member or a candidate member may serve as editor and non-members may apply and wait for membership or  alternatively persuade a voting member to make the submission.  This might eliminate the contribution as editor of people who are subscribers to the list, but do not desire voting privileges.  I can accept either form, but invite comments if anyone feels strongly either way.

4  Regarding filters to screen out "crank" submissions, I have adopted Matthew's suggestions.  Since one has to be a voting member for their submission to merit any consideration this would seem to serve as an effective screen for "crank" submissions.  It is hard to see how any real harm could result from a submission that is silly, out of the scope of the group, etc.  If the submission's language is obscene, contains spam or is otherwise offensive a simple vote on whether or not it should be removed is appropriate and can be done quickly.  I accept Matthew's view that considering the limited membership that may submit, it is better to deal with exceptions than to apply a review process to all documents.  (Of course, most of the world would consider all of the members of this group as cranks ;-)

I have tried to accommodate the suggestions that have been made.  If I have omitted some or you think of others please comment (particularly on the duties of a technical editor).  I will wait a few days for other comments (not everyone reads the list every day).  If there are none, I will move for adoption by general consent.

Thanks for your advice and counsel.

Bob
