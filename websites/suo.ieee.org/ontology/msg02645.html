Communication is always so difficult.
There does not seem to be anything else
for it but to keep on trying until you
get to know a person better.  I was only
speaking abstractly about different ways
that people can choose to operate, and
so it is purely a strategic matter that
needs to be reflected on after the fact.

Now I take a point of view that may not be the
most popular one here, and I always seem to be
annoying people with continual pesky questions
and my undischarged childhood curiousity about
"Where do ontolgies come from, anyway?"  There
are many people who behave as if they believed,
though I am sure that they would not say so if
they paused to reflect for even a moment, that
my question of "ontologogenesis" has an answer
that goes just a liitle bit like the following:

| Prophet P ascends Mountain M
| and receives Ontology (Big O)
| on the metaphorical "tablets"
| of revelant Sacred Source SS.

Although I admit the abstract possibility of this oracular answer,
I think that one has less frequent recourse to it in modern times,
and so I am led to inquire into the possibility of other paths to
the ontological revelation.  And this is the greater issue that I
must strive to keep in mind as we contemplate various examples of
problem-solving action among finite and mortal, fallibly informed
creatures, such as I guess myself to be from all present evidence.

So, let us ask ourselves:

| What is the problem to which an ontology is the solution?
| What is the question to which an ontology is the answer?
| What is the start state and what is the aim of it all?
| What is the ontology problem space that one transits?

If I keep these questions before us I will feel that
I have shouldered my duty to the sense of this quest.
