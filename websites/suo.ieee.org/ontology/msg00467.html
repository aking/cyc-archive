Jim, Pat, et al.,

I sympathize with Pat's concerns, but I also agree that
Ian Niles has done some useful work that can help to focus
the discussions on the SUO list.

I am happy that Jim has dropped the term "base document"
in favor of a less committal term, such as "working paper".

Therefore, I am willing to vote YES on a proposal with
provisions such as the following:

 1. The merged ontology by Ian Niles should be considered
    as a working paper intended to focus the attention
    of the SUO group on series of critical issues in ontology.

 2. For some period of time, say 90 days or so, all SUO
    participants will be urged to study that document, to
    become familiar with its content, and to send relevant
    comments, questions, and suggestions (pro or con) to
    the SUO list.

 3. At the end of that period, the original document should
    have been transformed into a new document that can be
    put to another vote to (1) continue developing as a working
    paper, (2) upgrade to a higher status, such as a base
    document, or (3) downgrade to a lower status as a shelved
    or mothballed document for the SUO archives.

John Sowa
