Dear Matthew,

Well, no, I am suggesting that the phrase "Reducing X to Y"
is extremely ambiguous, even a bit polysemiously perverse --
I can think of eleven or twelve purely formal possibilities
without hardly trying -- and you have just added another one,
or, at any rate, a very apt rephrasing of a familiar option, so,
unless, people are willing to formally specify exactly what notions
of "analysis", "decomposition", "reduction", and so on -- or whatever
converse notions of "synthesis", "composition", "extension", and so on,
that they have in mind when they deign to talk this way, then all of our
continuing animadversions about the question of the title are likely to
go on being as utterly inutile in the future as they have been, as yet,
inutterably futile.  It is not like we could not say what we intend,
and it is not like we could not evaluate, more or less roughly,
the relevance of each kind of reduction to a particular aim,
so I just keep wondering why everybody keeps avoiding it
like it were a Plague On All Our Houses to do so.

Many Regards,

Jon Awbrey
