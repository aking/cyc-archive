Matthew, 
	.	Excellent. It's highly appropriate to raise this issue. It
helps us to map out the components of what we need to produce. 

	.	I would suggest we also need, under 3., "Ontology Basic
Concepts and Principles". 3. should also possibly be in the form of a
structural description for users wishing to understand what they are dealing
with, rather than a methodology for development. Alternatively, perhaps we
should have both? 

	.	Here's my suggestion, based on your start. 

1	Basics and Architecture 
A.	Introduction 
B.	Site Guide / Architecture 

2	Ontology Concepts and Structure 
A.	Ontology Basic Concepts and Principles 
B.	Ontology Structure 
C.	Definitions of Terms 
D.	How to Use the SUO 

3	Ontology Parts
	A. - N. 	A series of parts, each probably quite small
(smaller than the Ian Niles document) that can be used in groups together
for particular purposes.

4	Languages 
A.	Language Requirements
B.	SUO-KIF Language Reference and Usage Manual 
C.	possible future SUO-CE Language Reference and Usage Manual 
D.	+ others? 

5	Methodology for ontology development (refer also 2A)
A.	Ontology Structuring Principles
B.	Ontology Integration Principles and Methodology
C.	Ontology Mapping Principles and Methodology
