Jim, 
	.	As so often happens with new initiatives, one only
identifies some of the shortcomings once the system is put in place. I now
realise I have a major amount of correspondence in a category not covered by
the new structure: 
*	SUO logic issues. 

	.	These issues are language independent, but are not content
either, and have consumed a lot of the correspondence to date. 

	.	Extrapolating from some of Jon Awbrey's recent scepticism,
it occurs to me that probably the bulk of correspondence generally thought
of as KIF related fits this category, as well as a significant portion of
that thought of as content. In fact my SUO-KIF file is a fair bit smaller in
comparison, and generally only contains e-mails with the word "KIF" in the
title or relating to David Whitten's work, etc. 

	.	At present, I see this area grouped as a mixture of SUO-KIF
and Ontology, for want of a separate place to post it. Can we do something
about this, like have an additional category for the logic issues? 

	.	What do others think?
