For those voting NO, there have been many good points put forward, most of
which I agree with. However, I'm not sure that warrants a no vote.  It is too
much to presume that the NO voters should provide an alternative. However, it
would be helpful if they could characterize the sort of document that they
might vote YES to. E.g

* would it have to be a certain size?
* would it have to include/exclude anything specific?
* should it includ many/few axioms?

In short, we have to start from SOMEWHERE. We all applaud Ian's hard work in
proposing such a somewhere.  If its not the right place, can we have guidance
as to what a better place might look like?

Also, keep in mind James's definition of what a NO vote means.
Are the no voters really reccommending that the group stop working on this
document?
