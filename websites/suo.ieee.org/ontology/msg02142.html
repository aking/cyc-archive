Hi Pat,

Here is some more feedback on ontological namespaces.

As I mentioned before, the Set Ontology that I am working on is part of the
metalevel. It functions as part of the foundations and as a base category
for other categories such as Classification and Hypergraph. In tandem, I am
developing the Classification Ontology and the Category Theory Ontology. I
am using these three ontologies synergistically to figure out how to
represent the basic categorical and information flow intuitions with KIF
terminology. The Set Ontology (and the Classification Ontology) feels like
its getting fairly mature. So I thought I would mention how I am thinking
about namespaces vis-a-vis the Set Ontology.

It seems to break up into about 5 sub-namespaces: sets, functions, limits,
colimits, and topos terminology. So I am thinking about partitioning the Set
terminology into these 5 parts, with the following namespace prefixes for an
arbitrary term t (the separating symbol could be any one of '#' or '$' or
':' etc.):
    set$t or set.set$t
    set.ftn$t
    set.lim$t
    set.col$t
    set.top$t
consisting of a namespace prefix 'set' for the entire ontology and namespace
prefixes '' or 'set', 'ftn', 'lim', 'col' and 'top' for the sub-namespaces.
I am allowing two prefixes for the sets sub-namespace, since the ontology is
named after this. This seems to look good in external references, such as in
the Classification Ontology. Internally, the 'set' prefix is not needed, and
within any sub-namespace its own prefix is not needed. Although, I so far
have not formalized how to specify the structure, it could look something
like this:
    (ontology set
    ...
        (part ftn ...)
        (part lim ...)
        (part col ...)
        (part top ...)
    ...)
