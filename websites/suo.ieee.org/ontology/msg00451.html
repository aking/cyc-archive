To be precise, the Base Document, if accepted, will be Working Draft 1.  Working Draft N is a baseline for determining Working Draft N+1.  Between Working Draft N and Working Draft N+1, the Working Group (WG) receives proposals for changes (including substantial changes) for the next Working Draft.  Based upon the opinions/votes of the WG determines what *edits* should be made to the next Working Draft.

The basic operation of a WG developing a standard is:

        Working Draft Process (roughly)
        Step 1.  Current Working Draft (WDn)
        Step 2.  Receive proposals for changes (working papers and presentations with *specific* edits defined)
        Step 3.  Committee decides which proposals to adopt.
        Step 4.  Apply edits to WDn to create WDn+1.
        Step 5.  n = n+1
        Step 6.  Go to step 1.

This process ends when the committee starts getting the sense of stability (possibly a year, two, or three from now) and the committee moves to Sponsor Ballot.  Sponsor Ballot has some similarity to our current voting, except that there is more formality to it.  The major steps for Sponsor Ballot are roughly:

        Sponsor Ballot Process (roughly)
        Step 1.  Draft Standard #n (DSn)
        Step 2.  Send DSn out for ballot/recirculation
        Step 3.  Receive ballot comments.
        Step 4.  Resolve negative comments first, then positive comments second (this formal process is called Ballot Resolution).
        Step 5.  Apply edits to DSn to create DSn+1.
        Step 6.  If there was at least 75% approval from at least 75% of the respondents, then go to Step 8.
        Step 7.  Go to step 1.
        Step 8.  Submit document to RevCom for publishing as a Standard.

I would expect many drafts of the document over the next 1-3 years.  As I've said in prior E-mail, the final standard can look very different from a Base Document.  It all depends on the number and type of proposals received and adopted by the committee (see steps #2 and #3 in the Working Draft process above).

In the end, the committee will need to approve the document for Sponsor Ballot (75% and 75%), so it is impossible to exit the standards process without significant consensus.

Hope this helps.
