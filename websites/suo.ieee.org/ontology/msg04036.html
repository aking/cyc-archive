Ascii renditions of math symbols:

|¯|  =  |^|  =  intersection (set-theoretic),
                greatest lower bound (glb) in a partial order

|_|          =  union (set-theoretic)
                least upper bound (lub) in a partial order

]¯[  =  ]^[  =  greek capital pi,
                used for extended products

]_[          =  flipt capital pi,
                used for coproducts (sums, disjoint unions) in category theory

c            =  "contained as a subset of", set-theoretic inclusion

The uberbar ¯ is not strictly legit and doesn't always translate.

I use bang-bang bars like !a! for generic emphasis and greek letters, !S! = sigma,
and scrip bars for script, fraktur, and gothic letters, $L$ = script L, and so on.

|   Figure 1.  Manifold X with Charts (U_i, q_i) and (U_j, q_j)

U_i and U_j are little patches of X that are mapped into the
local coordinate systems or the reference frames E_i and E_j.
X may be some really strange space, but the E's are familiar
spaces that one already knows how to handle well enough --
the E was originally for "Euclidean" and manifolds were
often called "locally Euclidean spaces", but this has
all been generalized to the max, and we are fixing to
try and develop the logical or qualitative analogues.

The pair (U_i, q_i), consisting of patch U_i and a map q_i, is called a "chart",
and a particular manifold structure for the X is specified by a big collection
of charts, called an "atlas".

Intuitively, I think of the chart as giving the "point of view" (POV) of an agent,
and diverse charts give the diverse POV's of diverse agents or sometimes the same
agent at diverse times.

The transition maps between charts are a simple idea with a complex name.
Eventually, we will name them like this:  q_ij or t_ij or maybe !t!_ij --
I forget right now what I usually use -- anyway:

t_ij  =  (q_j) o (q_i)^(-1)

is the mapping that "backs up" along q_i from E_i to U_i c X,
and then follows up with the mapping q_j from U_j c X to E_j,
in short, it is a map from a patch of E_i to a patch of E_j.

In order for there to be any sense of communication at all between
one agent and the next, or any one agent with its past and future
selves, there has to be some sort of constraint on the "transits",
or transition maps.  Different choices for these constraints --
computable, differentiable, etc. -- will give rise to the
study of different species of manifolds.
