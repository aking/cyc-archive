Chris,

I agree with the following paragraph:

>.... It seems to me that the position you
>outline - which regards roles (at least for parts of artifacts) as some kind

>of time-indexed relation, and does not reify them, is different from mine
>and Matthew's which does re-ify them. There seems to me no inconsistency in

>following our route of re-ifying them (and as you note, there are even
>linguistic practices that support this view).

But I disagree with the following point:

>It seems to me that we have two different approaches here - and that we
>might well need to decide which one we take in the SUO.

When there are two different approaches, it is reasonable to
use one if it is appropriate for some application, and it may
be equally reasonable to use the other if it is better for some
other application.

But there is no reason why the SUO must take a hard-and-fast
stand in favor of one or the other.  In my KR book, I adopt
both approaches.  For every role, such as Child or Author,
there is a corresponding dyadic relation HasChild or HasAuthor.

There is a formal translation between the two approaches, and
anything stated in one can be automatically translated to the
other.  There is no reason why the SUO can't support both.

John Sowa
