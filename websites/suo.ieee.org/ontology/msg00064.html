Pat,

One dimension at a time. I see the (revised) Heraclitus distinction and a
(revised) Peirce distinction as the two basic independent distinctions,
which provide a framework for the structural aspect of a SUO. I see these
categories as timeless. Perhaps a third distinction, a  relativized
Whitehead distinction anchoring a theory with axioms from linear logic, can
distinguish successfully between continuants and occurrents, and hence
objects and processes with states.
