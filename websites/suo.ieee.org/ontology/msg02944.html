People seem to think that the mere listing, naming,
or numbering of logical expressions are operations
that have no logical consequences, as if they fell
outside the jurisdiction of logical laws, and must
be innocent of any offense against reason, however
they operate, and no matter how they are conducted.
That would amount to a "purely syntactic indexing",
executable willy nilly, or with "logical impunity".
But semiotics is the ground over which logic walks,
so a fault in semiotics makes for a quake in logic.
