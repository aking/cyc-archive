Jim & All,

Let me try to state my perspective yet another way.
The way that you pose the problem of focus is like
looking through the wrong end of a telescope, and
that way of trying to see the object of the group
is serving to make us miss a potential approach
that we have never really had in view before,
namely, to use these under-utilized technes
all around us to improve the power of our
methods of inquiry themselves, sans which
we are not that much better fueled than
the presocratics when it comes to the
species of ontologies that we will be
able to chew out of our own fat in
the space of our own lifetimes.

So instead of straining our naked eyes to focus on
this or that all too admittedly heavenly objective,
I think that we need to start acquiring or building
a few telescopes -- "intelliscopes", if you will --
by way of improving the magnifying and the resolving
powers of our not quite voluminous enough brain pans.

This conversion in our point of view would require
that we view the sorts of objectives that you are
pointing out to us as being the ends of our aims,
not their starting blocks, as the very termini
of a process of inquiry, the engines of which
we currently lack, if not the very wheels to
ease it along its way.

Running On Empty In Motor City,

Jon Awbrey
