I did not find any insensitivity in your original reply above, in wanting to
further explore the topic of semiosis and its relevance to SUO, with me and
others. To further explore this topic, I started a thread titled "What is
the role of semiotics in SUO?".

In response you wrote a message entitled "Lo, The Veil Of Transparency Is
Over All!", the tone of which greatly surprised and disappointed me -- gone
was the friendliness shown in your original reply, and the cheerfulness
shown in other postings I've seen from you, replaced by attitudes that
seemed very negative to me personally, and which were not justified by
anything I wrote.

Also, your message on the veil of transparency attributed a belief to me
that you understood at the time I did not claim as my own, as shown in the
following exchange  posted later:

JA: Notice what you just said, by way of stating this POV:
JA:
JA: "ontologies are supposed to be about things that exist"
JA:
JA: You have just told me what (you believe)
JA: an ontology is "supposed" to be "about" --
JA: you have not told me what an ontology is --
JA: unless what it is reduces to the question
JA: of what it is "supposed" to be "about".
JA: Is that not an interesting observation?

PJ: It is a mistaken observation.
PJ: I merely tried to summarize three points of view.
PJ: I did not say that I personally suppose an ontology
PJ: can only be about things that exist.

JA: I understood that part -- hence the hedge:  "by way of stating
JA: this POV" -- but staking out the nuances of your estimable
JA: propositional attitude and distributing it throughout the whole
JA: discussion would have led me to use an order of circumlocution
JA: that is beyond my powers and level of comfort.

I felt it was a serious mistake on your part to knowingly say that I
believed something which you understood I did not say I believed. The reason
you gave for doing this amounted to a difficulty in writing on your part.
(This was also surprising, given the strong literary faculties that you have
frequently demonstrated.)

Anyway, enough said. I appreciate your apology. I hope that we can start
fresh, move forward and put all of this behind us.
