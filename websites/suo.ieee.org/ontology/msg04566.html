TG  = Tom Gollier
WVQ = W.V. Quine

TG: Nice quote.  We, or at least I, see
    the Peircean "object" reflected for
    a moment in:

WVQ: The fundamental-seeming philosophical question, How much of our science
     is merely contributed by language and how much is a genuine reflection
     of reality?

TG: only to have it disappear in:

WVQ: some conceptual scheme peculiar to our own special language.

TG: I think Peirce was more interested in
    a "conceptual scheme" peculiar to that
    alluded to "reality".

Hi Tom,

This begins one of the places where "Quine appropriated Pierre Duhem's thesis" --
as Jules Vuillemin puts it -- and I'll be copying out a few more before I try
to start thinking about it all again.  Let's dub that the "epoche of the text".
But what strikes my eye through the bracken this time around is the notion that
we "impose upon the world some conceptual scheme".  I wonder if the world even
notices our imposition?  And what would it do if it did?

Back to the grinstone ...

Jon
