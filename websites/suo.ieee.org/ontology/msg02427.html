At 8:51 AM +0200 10/5/01, Robert Meersman wrote:
>At 09-05-01 20:28, John F. Sowa wrote:
>>The enormous volume of messages to the SUO list has forced many
>>people to unsubscribe to SUO because they cannot afford the time
>>to wade through all the messages.  Various people have suggested
>>solutions such as setting up multiple mailing lists, but that hasn't
>>seemed to work.
>
>### John, I concur. I have also stopped contributing and even to 
>some extent reading the messages. There also seems to be a matter 
>that nobody is willing to mention explicitly? Since I subscribed on 
>Jan. 16, I received 2256 messages from SUO. Of these, 452 were 
>generated by one person. Including the replies to those, I counted 
>not less than 773 messages, or about 1/3 of the list's traffic 
>devoted to creatively meandering text with extremely well hidden 
>content. Lately there are indications that the trend is increasing. 
>These messages and some of the replies had occasional entertaining 
>value as lexical puzzles, but the net contribution so far to the 
>matter of (upper or other) ontologies in my opinion has been 
>infinitesimal.
>
>### This may result in a discouraging effect on other (and new) list 
>members from making contributions to it. That certainly applies to 
>me. We would either need a new list or a new style of contribution 
>management for serious practitioners and theorists who want to stay 
>focused on the real and important issues related to UO. Some of us 
>are into this research as a job, and have things at stake.
>

I AGREE TOTALLY with Robert. I really GOT SICK of this traffic, which 
is leading nowhere. I am convinced we need a much stronger management 
effort to implement the working solutions we proposed, and to 
discourage people from abusing of this list.
