Jack,

Can you forward me the subscription information for the OCA (?) List?

This is stuff that I have been thinking about for as long as I can remember,
and I do not think that I can sum up right away all of the different things
that I have believed at one time or another with respect to these questions,
but I tend to toss them all under the heads of various "integration tasks" --
like integrating eternal logic (Parmenides) and secular change (Heraclitus),
or integrating the dynamic with the symbolic aspects of intelligent systems,
or integrating the qualitative facets and the quantitative factors of stuff,
and so on.

When I applied to enter my current Systems Engineering programme,
I wrote out a Prospectus of what I thought would be compelling and
yet reasonable for me to do, in the direction that I imagined myself
suited to explore, and some of these bits of blue sky are what I have
been presenting in the various and sun-dry fora of your acquaintance
under the "Inquiry Driven Systems" thread.  You are providing me with
a sufficient series of excuses to continuing doing that, and so I will
try not to disapoint.  The rest of what I know or think is in my Diss,
my "Theme One" program, and the "Diff Log & Dyn Sys" paper that I will
send your way as soon as I dig my way out of these snow drifts of real
and virtual snow hereabouts.

As I see it, the biggest obstacle to our even being able to think clearly
about these causal issues is that we have not yet developed for logic --
though I think that I, with a boost from Peirce, have at least begun! --
what would form the analogous extension to the way that differential and
integral calculus extend analytic geometry.  So that is the block in the
way of inquiry at which I am occupying myself with merrily chipping away.

And Let The Chips Fly Where They May!

Jon Awbrey
