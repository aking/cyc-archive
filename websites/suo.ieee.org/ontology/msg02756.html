Robert Grayson Spillers wrote:
> 
> Ian / Pat C,
> In your respective ontologies, where would one place a CD containg
> software that tests the truth value of a set of propositions that
> describe the process of refining gasoline?
> 
> Bob
> 

  I would have "InformationBearingObject" as a direct 
subclass of "Physical Object", and the CD would be 
a subclass of both InformationBearingObject and "Solid Object".
(A liquid "InformationBearingObject" could be a solution
of DNA, for example.)
   The program contained on the CD would be a subclass of
Script, which would be a subclass of Mental Object.  There
might be intermediate classes, I haven't yet filled in that
part of the ontology.
   The process of refining gasoline would be a subclass of
Industrial Process, a subclass of Human activity, which is
a subclass of physical process.
   Where the intention is closely the same, I prefer to use 
CYC names for the concepts in my own ontology, just to make
cross-reference easier.
