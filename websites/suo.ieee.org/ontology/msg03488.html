MW: Jon's paper presents some foundations of logic which he thinks
are important to set out as the basis for what is to follow. This
is  important, because as you will already have seen, logic, and
some of the stuff immediately around it, is not as cut and dried
as we might like to think. There are choices to be made, and these
should be made explicitly. As you may conclude from his exchange
with CM, there is nothing earth shattering here.

MW: Jon's main contention that this is all leading to is that the
"big problem" we really face is ontology development, rather than
a specific ontology, and that we need amongst other things to 
specify and develop tools for this purpose. This would support an
"enquiry" process which would support the interaction between
ontology developers to help them resolve issues and move forwards.
He correctly identifies semiotics (for practical purpose the
relationships between terms and meanings) as a key element in this.

MW: The main reason I am bothering to mediate here is that I have
enough experience from my base standards community to know that he
is right about these things. If we think we are going to create
anything significant by hand coding KIF files, we don't understand
the problem, but that is where we are right now.
