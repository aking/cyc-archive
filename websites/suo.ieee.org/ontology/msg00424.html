To Matthew & John, and all the rest, 
	.					Based on the principles set
out by Jim, the rationale here sounds pretty well like a YES, not a NO, to
me. 

	.	Basically, the sentiment seems to be that we should continue
with the structure Ian has assembled. I may have missed something (I haven't
read all the discussion on the voting), but I haven't seen anyone arguing
for a stop to Ian's work, nor any offer of any alternative proposals. 

	.	I addressed possible alternatives yesterday. If anyone
wishes to propose one of them, or add an extra option, then please let's
hear it in an e-mail explicitly labelled accordingly. 

	.	I find it quite amazing that, in a group claiming and aiming
to provide an unambiguous structure for expression of the nature of the
world, and for aiding unambiguous communication, so many people should
CONTRADICT THEMSELVES. I respectfully suggest participants take some time
out and gather their thoughts. 



Jim,
	.	I recall you requested a straw poll at first. I suggest the
outcome needs to be determined by taking into account the reasonings given
for people's votes. 

	.	I suggest the air should be allowed to clear before this
matter is readdressed. It is clearly a mess at present.
