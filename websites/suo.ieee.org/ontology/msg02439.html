We have already been over this ground several times,
but perhaps it is time for yet another quick review:

A person who claims that everything of type X can be reduced,
according to some notion of reduction Y, to things of type Z,
is required to define all of these things and to prove it so.

Paradigmatic examples:

1.  X = non-neg integers 0, 1, 2, 3, ...
    Y = sum of numbers other than itself and 0.
    Z = {1}

    Hence, 3 is reducible to sums of 1's.
    Proof: 3 = 1+1+1.

2.  X = positive integers 1, 2, 3, ...
    Y = product of numbers other than itself and 1.
    Z = primes

    Hence, 3 is irreducible under the notion Y,
    since its only factorizations are 1x3 and 3x1,
    which are excluded as trivial cases under notion Y.
    This means that we can put 3 in Z as being one of the
    primitive components from which other numbers are built
    according to the mode of synthesis that corresponds to Y.

With respect to the two most common notions of reduction for relations
that I know about, what I eventually called "compositive reduction"
and "projective reduction", I satisfied all of these requirements,
giving explicit definitions and the requisite examples or proofs
to demonstrate what I asserted.  So my lessons are done here.

But now you say, for the set X = {finite valence relations},
that there is yet another method or notion of reduction Y,
according to which X reduces to Z = {2-adic relations}.

Now, as far as I am concerned, there are many perfectly useful
ways of analying relations where the method of analysis Y itself
invokes one or another 3-adic relations to carry out the process.
The case of projective reduction that I gave above is one of these.
But, from your point of view, I am just guessing, I should think
that it would amount to a somewhat Pyrrhic victory if you are
forced to invoke 3-adic relations within the definition of Y.
So far, you have not yet defined the notion of reduction Y
that you claim to have in mind, nor given a proof that it
works for the X in question.  I said that I would help
you do this if it were at all possible, but for the
moment you are not giving me anything to go on.
I am not the one to say where this is going --
we are following your gnosis, not mine.
