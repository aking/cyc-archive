Adam,

Here was your original:

(forall (?t1 ?t2)
        (<=> (beforeEq (?t1 ?t2)
                       (and (timepoint ?t1) (timepoint ?t2)
                            (or (before ?t1 ?t2) (= ?t1 ?t2))))))

Here is your new one:

(forall (?t1 ?t2)
   (<=>
     (beforeEq ?t1 ?t2)
     (and
       (timepoint ?t1)
       (timepoint ?t2)
       (or
         (before ?t1 ?t2)
         (= ?t1 ?t2)))))

And here is how I usually pretty-print it --
prettiness is in the eye of the beholder --
assuming that I am reading it right yet --
I only use square brackets here because
whoever designed New Courier was not
a Lisp programmer.

[ forall [ ?t1 ?t2 ]
  [ <=>
    [ beforeEq ?t1 ?t2 ]
    [ and
      [ timepoint ?t1 ]
      [ timepoint ?t2 ]
      [ or
        [ before ?t1 ?t2]
        [ = ?t1 ?t2]
      ]
    ]
  ]
]

That checks.

Jon
