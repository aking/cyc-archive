Graham,

I personally avoid the use of that pretentious prefix "meta-"
as much as I possibly can.  The reasons for my druthers are
too involved to take up right now, and in any case, sadder
budweiser experience teaches me that it's a waste of breath
to go into, especially among a crew of Lotus Eaters who are
so addicted to their met-ampheta-mental upper ontologesics.

One diagnostic criterion, though, check to see if the domain in question
is closed under the application of the "meta-" operator, that is to say,
ask yourself, "Self, is MetaX c X?"  A lot nonsense meta-melts away,
that is to say, sublimates, when faced with that question -- which
is why those who love nonsense, even more than I, so seldom ask it.

There is a whole chunk of my dissertation that tries to tackle this.
I will post the link -- why not?  Someday some bold researcher will
do the job of discovering what I am beginning to suspect already,
that apart from a cursory surf-by, no website ever got read by
anybody, except, on occasion, possibly its author.  Cretanly,
ninety percent of the discussions that we have had here
would have been rendered redundandant if anybody had
read, say, ten percent of the tutorial material on
Sowa's site, or any one of 10^4 equivalent loci.

Cheers, Anyway,

Jon Awbrey
