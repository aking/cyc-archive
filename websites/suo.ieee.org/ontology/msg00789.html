May I suggest that the subject of this discussion be "Similar to CYC" or
"Compared to CYC".  It is more reflective of my meaning and it is easy to
see that "No better than CYC" could be offensive.  I wanted to highlight the
waste of resource involved in creating an equilivent ontology.  Although I
would prefer an ontology constructed differently than CYC, I do not wish to
disparage CYC.  It is a significant accomplishment. 

Many people have criticized CYC and almost everyone would have done it
differently and ( I suspect) different from each other.   Considering the
methods used and the purpose of the ontology, CYC is well constructed and on
a scale different than other ontologies.  It works for CYC - and their
customers. 

My point is that an ontology created using similar methods will, if done
well, produce similar (but different) results.  My prior note discusses the
methods I believe will produce a superior result.  Cyc is sympathetic and
has offered their co-operation. They have invested over 15 years and many
tens of millions of dollars to develop their ontologies.  However, they are
willing to adopt a standard ontology developed in a more principled way
(their caveats are in my previous note). 

It seems unlikely to me that a volunteer effort using the same methods will
produce something superior in two to three years.  One might augment or
improve CYC and this would be worthwhile, but it is not what I think most of
the contributors to this list have in mind. 

Bob
