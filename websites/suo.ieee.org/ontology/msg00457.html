> 
> For those voting NO, there have been many good points put 
> forward, most of
> which I agree with. However, I'm not sure that warrants a no

MW: I think that the real issue is that any vote is premature. I would be
happy to accept that we have a useful working paper, but not more than that.

> It is too
> much to presume that the NO voters should provide an 
> alternative. However, it
> would be helpful if they could characterize the sort of 
> document that they
> might vote YES to. E.g
> 
> * would it have to be a certain size?

MW: Not particularly, but I think it does need to be significantly more
complete technically.

> * would it have to include/exclude anything specific?

MW: I think amongst other things it needs to have a description of the
approach taken in its development. The current document reminds me of what
happens when a programmer is asked to write a requirements specification,
and decides it is easier just to write the code directly.

> * should it includ many/few axioms?

MW: This is less important. I am looking for a firm foundation that means
amongst other things that multiple people could develop parts of the SUO
independently, yet consistently. I just see one person doing something
ground up. This can be a useful experiment, but we have to be prepared to
throw away the results in favour of what we learn from them.
> 
> In short, we have to start from SOMEWHERE. We all applaud 
> Ian's hard work in
> proposing such a somewhere.  If its not the right place, can 
> we have guidance
> as to what a better place might look like?
> 
> Also, keep in mind James's definition of what a NO vote means.
> Are the no voters really reccommending that the group stop 
> working on this
> document?

MW: I simply do not accept Jim's definition of what a no vote means. I see
it simply as a tactic to put pressure on getting a yes vote.

> FYI: I have not had time to look at the document yet.
