MW: Yes, the merged ontology was by a narrow margin given that status on 
a vote, not on the basis that it was at an appropriate stage of development 
to work  towards a standard, but that it was necessary at this stage to
put it under control of the group. I think it is fair to say in hindsight
that this was premature, and the technical position at the moment is that
we have a working document but no editor for it, due to the objections
that have been raised, including by some who originally voted for the
merged ontology being accepted as a working document.

MW: It is of course important to keep these procedural issues separate
from becoming a reflection on the work that Ian has done, which is
unquestionably valuable.

MW: Ian and Adam have to decide whether to pursue a vote on the editorship
or to withdraw the Merged Ontology as a working document and submit it as
an Expert Contribution (following the passage of Bob's resolution. The
recent
changes on the web site indicate an intention to do the latter, which shows
grace and statesmanship on their part.

> 
> Note that under this definition, SUO could have more than one working
> document and they would all have equal status, i.e. just 
> being a working
> document would not mean that SUO had selected that document 
> as the final
> standard.

MW: I would in any case expect a number of separate but related standards
to come out of the work here. E.g. Architecture, methodology, content, 
langauge (but being done elsewhere).
> 
> What is "a sufficient number", and are there any limits on 
> when a working
> document may be proposed? These questions are further discussed below.

MW: My personal opinion is that we should set a relatively high hurdle to
jump before accepting something as a working document. I ahve had this 
imposed on me in SC4, and whilst I didn't like it, I have to admit that
the work benefited.
