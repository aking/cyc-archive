Pat, 
	.	If it were Maths, Physics, simple Chemistry, or Engineering,
your comment would apply to me also. 

	.	The problem is that I learnt those terminologies at school
(Engineering is just an extension of physics), but not the logic stuff.
Actually, I did even learn some basic logic in a brief Philosophy course at
Uni, but it didn't get into as esoteric stuff as this. I find the SUO-KIF,
etc. is explained in a way that misses some of the steps out, so that I have
to guess the intermediate steps, making comprehension very laboured and
imprecise. 

	.	This is why I wish someone would try explaining the
terminology in a way that is intended for the uninitiated. I don't mean
those who don't know how to think logically, as probably ¾ the population
can do that. What I mean is, for example, explaining the terminology in a
way that could be explained to a judge in a court, or a builder. 

	.	As to your suggestion "then someone could probably find some
software that could generate it automatically", I agree, and it would
necessarily be some restricted, and possibly modified, version of English
with strictly defined meanings of words, and only limited allowable sentence
structures. That's exactly what languages like ACE are about. 

	.	As John Sowa and others have pointed out previously, if they
are well done, they are actually easier to read than normal English. 

	.	I think Robert Meersman summed it up pretty well below.
