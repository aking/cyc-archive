### Perhaps this particular discussion should be carried on after everybody
has had a chance to read the absolutely seminal paper "Towards a Logical
Reconstruction of Relational Database Theory" by pur sang AI person Ray
Reiter already in 1978. It is accepted by DB theorists everywhere,
certainly those that dabble in semantics of data, at least as an
ontological-avant-la-lettre point of departure for talking formally about
(relational) database. The paper quite simply but firmly imbeds relations
and all the stuff that floats around them in Database World in first-order
logic or indicates where or why this is difficult.

### After this, the discussion at hand may happen to go away entirely.
Relations à la Sowa, Reiter, ... are mathematical objects and as such part
of the semantical domain (i.e., ontology) for the language L1 in which we
talk about them (viz. as in the message above). Relations à la King are
part of a different language L2, the practice of relational database as
pontificated by Codd et al., and large parts of the relation-concept this
language in fact may be (formally semantically) interpreted in terms of the
above base ontology, but some terms and usage belong to the domain ontology
for L2. The interesting issues here are merely *grin* the (interpretive)
mapping where in L1 and the building and alignment of L2 to L1.
