ELF.  Literature Note 9

o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o

ELF Lovers Everywhere,

So long as we're casting about for any prospective sight of land,
I might mention one of the tactics that I frequently use to keep
my own footing on something that approaches solid ground, to wit,
let's prepend the modifier "computable" to every airy subject in
cite, or site, or sight.

In my frame of reference, words like Abstraction, Intention, Interpretation,
Operation, Reflection, Representation, and so on, that rhyme with Function
and that end in "-ion", are called "-ionized words".  They have as a rule
the peculiar semantic charge to denote a process that prospectively ends
in some product or result, which end is equivocally denoted by that term.

In the context of our present work, one has good reason to prefix this
entire suffix class with the adjective "computable", and that has been
my driving motivation -- see, there's another one -- for quite a few
years now.

In anticipation -- yikes, there everywhere, once you start noticing them --
of the sort of comparative study that will no doubt eventually be forced
on our attention -- yes, it was in my mind, too -- I'll begin to append
a collage of resource materials on the subjects of computable semantics
and computational model theory that I have run across in my own travels.
