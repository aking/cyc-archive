> JA, you are simply mistaken if you think that syntax has any impact

You are starting to say things that you would know better than if you reflected
for just a moment.  Syntax, notation, representational systems make a difference
in what sorts of things folks can think about, both effectively and efficiently.
Calculus depended on, you guessed it, a "calculus", a syntactic system, or two,
being discovered by a couple of guys at a particular point in history.  Before
they could do that, ceratin other obstructions of a "merely representational"
character had to be cleared out of the way.  That is how it happened.

What do you think you have when the parse is done?
Some sort of Zeus Ordained Pure Idea?  Not a bit.
It is yet another form of representation, with
its own syntax, and a nearly endless variety
of different ways to do it, all built on top
of a binary representation that is the end,
or rather the mediate, result of a long
cultural and technological evolution.
And people get paid big bucks to
figure out better ways to do it.

ATP is just a catalyst.

Jon Awbrey
