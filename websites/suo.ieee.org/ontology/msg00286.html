   I hope you don't mind a Biology post. As anyone who has looked at
Biosci will note, the field has split into innumerable smaller and
smaller enclaves. Yes, fractal biosci !
   Anyway, I'm an evolutionary anatomist and I study the point at
which the pelvis closed , giving us the placental mammals. Eutherians.
   There is an association with this closing which is the trait
called oestrus, estrus or "heat" as in "My cat is in heat and keeps
me awake all night." H.sapiens does not have this trait, because of a
small change in our anatomy related to pelvic tilt.
   The important thing is, that oestrus makes the female as anxious
to mate as the male. He does not have to court her; he just has to be
there when she goes into heat.
   The absence of male courtship in most eutherians has resulted in
an almost complete absence of the use of vocalization for
communication; first as a courtship tool and then for other
communication, as in the repetoire of a bird's calls. Vocalization
for courtship expands beyond courtship, in a species like the crow,
to include self-identification, identification of danger,
notification of food, and other functions which have been recorded
and cataloged.
    Humans do not have oestrus. Females do not go into heat. Oestrus
is more a mechanical than an hormonal process so it is either "all
on" or "all off".
    As a result we have taken up the use of vocalization, undoubtedly
first as a tool of courtship and then for general  communication.
    Our vocalization is developed, as in baby birds, during early
infancy. At this point it also shapes the amorphous brain by
establishing a pathway based on : vocalization out / response back.
    The benefits of developing such "hardware" are obvious since it
incorporates the interpretation of the content of responses; the
action, if any, to be taken; and composing vocalizations to reply.
    This pathway, in humans, utilizes the "corpus callosum"; the
connecting structure in our bipartite vertebrate brains.
    Children who have not acquired vocalization do not have the
intellectualization seen in children who develop vocalization
normally.
    Our eutherian inheritance is the large brain; neccessary because
it lacks the vocalization pathway.
    Our inheritance from prior ancestral vertebrates is that pathway,
converting our "excessive" encephalization into a brain which
functions like the elegant little bird-brains.
     Vertebrates before eutherians on the evolutionary tree would not
have needed large brains. Only eutherians have oestrus. Only
eutherians do not need male courtship to mate.
     This is why the dinosaurs could function with the small brains
they had. They did not need more brain mass.   
    "We think because we speak" is an accurate, if grossly
simplified, explanation of our human intelligence.
    Could a.i. use the 2-part pathway? A system would need to isolate
the vocalization (equivalent) function in one device; hearing
(equivalent) in another; and the interpretation in a middle device.
It is somewhat like 2 individuals working together and speaking with
one voice.
    Biosci seems incapable of understanding this relatively simple
explanation of human evolution. There are 3500+ biosci journals with
as many "specialties". The interaction between these is minimal,
since any finding in one are must be recognized and brought to the
attention of others. The systems to do this effectively do not exist.
     Unlike Physics, biosci findings are seldom available rapidly and
full-text online. Individual researchers must be subscribed to the
appropriate service in order recieve copies. Non-service articles
will cost upwards of $100 to non-subscribers. It is an incoherant
mess.
