JA: One way to read these 3-adic sign relations is to recognize that
    the 2-adic relations formed by the sign and interpretant columns
    constitute equivalence relations, and this property allows us to
    graph the pertinent facts as shown in the next couple of Figures ...

JD: Good idea!
    Then why not just have two tables instead of
    the 'join' of them which is much more bulky.

o---------------o---------------o

| "B"           | "u"           |

JD: And the symmetrical for L(B).

This looks wrong, at least, if you meant to form the 2-adic
projection of L(A) on the subspace !O!x!S!, which one would
commonly write as "Proj_OS(L(A))" or as "L(A)_OS" for short,
and the 2-adic projection of L(A) on the subspace !S!x!I!,
which is written "Proj_SI(L(A))" or "L(A)_SI" for short.

Taking the 2-adic projections of L(A) on
!O!x!S!, !O!x!I!, !S!x!I!, respectively,
we obtain the following set of Tables:

L(A)_OS  =  Proj_OS(L(A))
o---------------o---------------o
| Object        | Sign          |
o---------------o---------------o
| A             | "A"           |
| A             | "i"           |
o---------------o---------------o
| B             | "B"           |
| B             | "u"           |
o---------------o---------------o

L(A)_OI  =  Proj_OI(L(A))
o---------------o---------------o
| Object        | Interpretant  |
o---------------o---------------o
| A             | "A"           |
| A             | "i"           |
o---------------o---------------o
| B             | "B"           |
| B             | "u"           |
o---------------o---------------o

L(A)_SI  =  Proj_SI(L(A))
o---------------o---------------o
| Sign          | Interpretant  |
o---------------o---------------o

o---------------o---------------o
