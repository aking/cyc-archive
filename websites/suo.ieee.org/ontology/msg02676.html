Josiane Caron-Pargue wrote:

I am interested in this paradigme too. The way by which concepts can be
defined in terms of other concepts, needs that a medium has to be
considered. Concepts are contextualised through the medium and then
constitute occurrent representations of the socio-cognitive task. I am
presently working in order to do a better modelization than I have already
done with my working group PROVERB. I want to modelize the
decontextualisation effects - i.e. the construction of new concepts
stabilized from occurrent representations. 

In PROVERB group we succeeded to simulate some novice strategies. This work
is not published because the group was dissolved because someone told to one
of the members that Toh was not an interesting problem because it is too
much easy to simulate it! and also than NL was not interesting ! I know by
heart all these arguments.

So now I am completely alone, You may see it on laboratory web site which is
still in construction. I have not yet elaborated my personal page.
