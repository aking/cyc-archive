Meaning of 'on' in the different contexts


        First I will give the way I interpreted these examples:
- the context 'on c': 
It means that there is nothing on the peg; In this case it is difficult to
give another denomination.
But there are several cases where the peg is not empty, namely in 18-19
where a new episod started with the connective 'then' in 18, and another one
is starting in 20

It means that at this time the participant do not consider that disks 3 and

at a time, for setting automatically the goal at the situational level, but

- the context 'on the black one' (le noir, in french) i.e. 'on the colour

- the context 'on the black one' (le noir, in french) i.e. 'on the colour
one' appears as a particular case of the preceding differenciation,

- the context 'on the column c' can be generalized as 'on the column peg'.
The term 'column c' concerns a set of disks more or less organized as
agregates, that the participant intends to move. As the situation of problem
evolves this set of disks is more or less spreading through the three pegs.
The goal is to move the set of disks. It could be understood as the
automatization of the generic goal studied in the modelization I have
started for the trial 1 (and whose markers were 'then I can take again' in
3, trial 1 - cf my mail 26 May 2001 09:40:08)

- the context 'on the column' is a particular case of the preceding
differenciation. It appears for the first time in trial 2 in 20 when the
participant started to put disks 1, 2, and 3 on the disk 4. It appears again
in 26 and 31, which sound as main steps for moving 1-2-3 on disk 4. The term
'on the column' means that the problem is quasi-solved from the
participant's point of view.


- The context 'on the disk b', generalised as 'on the disk peg'. It means
that the position of the disk on that peg is not in accordance with the
progress of the goal. So this position has to be considered as an
intermediary one in order to give a rectification of the strategy.

---------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION

'on' marks the way in which the process of making the action of put
something on another thing was intended. This intention depends on the
structure, possibilities and constraints of the considered objects.
