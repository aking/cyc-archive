Joe,

You might want to look into the ArsDigita Group's concept
of "Open-Source Solutions for Purposeful Communities",
for example, at:

<http://www.arsdigita.com/asj/>

<http://www.arsdigita.com/index.adp>

One of the brains behind the whole thing is apparently
one Philip Greenspun, who is responsible for designing
the web architexture for many such open web communities,
including CogNet, PhotoNet, and sundry others.  Bio data
can be found at:

<http://philip.greenspun.com/>

Philip and his canine co-author, Alex, have also published
a highly-regarded book on such stuff (as webs are made on):

<http://www.arsdigita.com/books/panda/>

Also, have you looked at the IBM Techexplorer platform as
an alternative to Adobe and PostScript?  Seems like I saw
that being used at Perpignan or some other Euro web sites
a lot, so maybe they would know more about it.

Regards,

Jon
