Avast Ye Hearties!

It always seems to come to this.

It is understandable that a hardy band of explorers --
passing through many a strange and wondrous terrain,
cut off in the quest for who knows how long a spell,
isolated from all converse with that larger society
from which they initially journeyed out, and so, by
turns, reduced in their utterants to a peculiar mix
of mind-numbingly abbreviated blandishments that go
to facilitate the mundaner matters of the shipbored
routine spiced with the increasingly marvelous bits
of expressive novelty that they craft to express in
some way the heretofore ineffable sites encountered --
like I say, I know how it happens that special-duty
argots, codes, jargons, pidgins, are most naturally
developed, faute de mieux, under such circumstances.

But when the argosy has come round again, in prospects
of returning to its native shore, when these argonauts,
these hearty hermenauts, sense that it is time to send
out this pidgin in search of drier, sundrier, and dare
to hope for stabler, steadier ground, well, what then?

Aye, there's the rub.

Jon Awbrey
