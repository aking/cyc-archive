Pat,

Before I try to tackle the body of your note, let me propose
that we divide the corpus in question into a couple of parts.
You may think that this is some strategem of mine, designed
to afford me an unfair advantage, but I assure you that it
is offered purely on behalf of the shear possibility that
we already agree on certain points, and that it would be
a waste of our energies, if so, to exercise our wits in
wrangling over issues that are not at issue, however
diverting that might be to the onlooking bystanders.

So, here is my partition of the case:

1.  The indispensable utility of triadic relations in general.

2.  The indispensable utility of triadic relations in treating
    that omnibus topic of the relationships among language and
    thought and "pragmata", for which last position of players
    you may somewhat weakly substitute the tag-team tandemonia
    of "objects and objectives", if you so desire and so elect.
    It's no skin off my gnosis, either way.

Whether this arrangement of the alternatives is agreeable to you,
and whether by good fortune Point One is already moot between us,
just let me know how you choose to take your stand on each score.
Meanwhile, I will go off to watch yet another rerun of Star Trek,
and return to this locus post*haste.

Your Obedient Servant,

Jon Awbrey
