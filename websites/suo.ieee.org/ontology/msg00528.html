Jim et al, 
	.	There is a significant comment, which has been made more
than once, that is not among your list. It may not have been part of any
formal voting comment, but I believe it relates to potential ways forward
(as opposed to ways backward, such as abandonment). 

	.	Basically, the gist of the comments you have listed still
leave us with two real alternatives I can envisage (nothing would stop them
being undertaken in parallel, except that only one seems to actually had any
real energy expended on it). 
*	Further development of Ian Niles's work. Having reviewed the
comments Jim reported and Ian's comments, it seems to me Ian is likely to
make significant worthwhile further progress, and also make modifications
that will accommodate issues people have raised. 
*	Adoption and refinement of an existing Ontology, such as CYC. This
was proposed sometime ago, I forget by whom initially, and necessarily has
considerable technical merit due the solidity of its foundation. The issue
of its solid basis seems to me to be the most significant question mark
hanging over our current approach of Ian's work, essentially starting from
scratch. 

	.	If anyone has any other positive alternatives, then please
let's know what it is. 

	.	Sorry if I seem repetitious, but I haven't seen any
resolution of the issues between these options.
