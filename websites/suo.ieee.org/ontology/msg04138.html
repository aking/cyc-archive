Here is how you build a bridge across a chasm.
You cast a thin line across the gap, a thread
attached to an arrow, perhaps, then use it to
pull a slightly heftier cord across the space,
and then a sturdy rope, and then a succession
of eventually, ultimately load-bearing cables.

So there you have my plans for this thread.

The chasm is one that opened up quite a while back --
it was already wide enough to be a serious concern
by the 1850's, so far as I can tell, and the first
few tentative lines cast across it have since been
torn all to shreds.

To finesse a line across the looming crevasse, between alpha-
and numerical mindsets, we will need to travel light at first,
and not sweat the inessentials.

Sets, functions, relations.
That much gives a foothold.

From my point of view, I think of them this way:

Functions are relations.
Relations have types.
Types are functions.

I will explain that later, when it comes up.

I will be looking for ways to exploit the continuities
that already exist among the sundered domains that are
mentioned in our current subject line.  There are many.

The problem, as I see it, is to reconnect the different senses
of words like "data", "description", "model", "theory", "world"
that are employed by all brands of viewers of the current scene.

John Sowa has pretty picture of this that keeps coming back to me:

http://www.jfsowa.com/figs/mthworld.gif

To my way of reading him, Aristotle provides another good picture
of what we need in his discussion of "analogy, example, paradigm",
where the analogical "model" and the logical "model" are fused in
a way that equips our thinking with a very useful form of "pivot".

But I feel like we may be getting distracted with this quibble over "model".
I'm already used to the way that it's used in six or seven different fields.
And I'm the one who said that ambiguity would be unavoidable, if you recall.
So that is not the problem, not for me.  The problem is keeping clear about
what senses of the word we are using at any given time, and not letting any
one usage prevent us from remembering the purpose of data, models, theories.
