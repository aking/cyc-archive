be a more significant confound, if you are the sort

confoundations put together.  But I will
leave that open for some future lack
of discussion.

Now, the catalogue or the taxonomy that you gave,
which, by the way, I took to be worthy of no less
a thinker than Jorge Luis Borges:

> - What is ontology
> - General discussions about ontology and logic
> - Ontology-driven applications
> - History of ontology
> - Poetry and ontology
> - Ontological aspects of US elections
> - and so on ...

Well, I took that to be in the spirit of
a "metontology", an "ontology of ontologies",
at least, an "ontology of ontology-related crimes",
and since it did not seem to be intended as either
"standard" or "upper", it just seemed to me that
it naturally belonged somewhere else than where
you -- or the damned machine -- chose to put it.

I hope that this serves to clarify both
my apprehensions and my misapprehensions
about the contents and the implications
of your recent note.
