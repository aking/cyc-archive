Matthew,

Well, yeah, except, read this part again,
then tell me what KIF has got to with it:

> Now, I think that there is a rather obvious connection among
> these tasks, since finding better ways to "do logic" might
> just contribute to the job of finding better ways
> to learn and to teach logic.

I am talking about 'better' ways to do logic here.
I have little interest in teaching the old regime.
Personally, now that I have developed better ways
to do certain pieces of logic, I would no more go
back to using all of that "&", "V", "=>" business
in a real logical computation than I would try to
use Roman Numerals to do Differential Equations --
hey, but that's just me -- I obviously don't have
what it takes to succeed in this "Gatesian" world.
So, in the end, I guess I agree -- Heaven help us!

Jon Awbrey
