Matthew --
   Just to get back on one or two points:


> MW: I consider time dependent attributes to be indicative of states of
> something.
> 
   This is a reasonable way to handle such attributes.  I just use
a different terminology -- I like to consider a "state" as the set
of all of the time-variable attributes of an object at a particular 
time.  The term "state" has been used in different senses, such as in
Petri Nets and in computer science to describe an attribute-value
that persists for some period of time.  To avoid debates over
terminology, it may be a good idea to eschew the term "state"
itself and use more specific terms like "instantaneous_state"
or "persistent_state"  (once we decide how to define them).

> The long answer is that the STEP community has developed (I 
> guess) about 20,000 pages of standards on how to represent 
> engineering  data in ISO 10303
> - Standard for the Exchange of Product Model Data, ISO 13584 - Parts
> Libraries, ISO 15531 - Manufacturing Management Data, ISO 15926 Data
> Integration for Process Plant facilities including Oil and 
> Gas, ISO 18876 -
> Integration of Industrial Data for Exchange Access and Sharing.
> 
> These have shown interest in a number of types of relationship between
> "parts" and possible parts, and versions etc.
> 
   I've only been able to look at a small part of the STEP specifications,
and it looks like there are a lot of useful ideas there, such as the
catalog of concepts related to shape.  I haven't found the sections
related to "possible parts".  I will try to find it -- if you have a 
specific reference, it could be very helpful for me.

    Pat Cassidy
