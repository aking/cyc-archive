SUO Working Group,

A misunderstanding has developed between
Phil Jackson and myself for which I have
already sent him a carte blanche apology
off-list.  Since the later stages of the
misunderstanding appear to me to involve
a tangle of factors that I cannot remedy,
traceable perhaps to misapperceptions of
the intentions behind certain manners of
expression to which diverse temperaments
are likely to resort, for which conducts
it would be an act of some hypocrisy for
me to apologize, since I do not know any
way that I might be able to gain control
over their ilk and thus be able to avoid
the occasions of their recurrence in the
future, it is incumbent on me to try and
root out at least the original causes of
the misunderstanding insofar as they lie
within some controlable error on my part.

Therefore I have gone over Phil's initial communique,
and I have found what I think to be my first mistake
in interpreting it, which I will mark below as a way
of exposing my sin against him and his epistle to us.

Okay, what is now rather abundantly clear to me
on my second reading of the following paragraph
is that -- well, not desiring to be attributing
intentions to another, I have to say that it is
only my next hypothesis -- that Phil's comments
about semiosis are not really intended to raise
that subject as a topic for discussion but only
to set it down as one of many possible examples
that might be adduced to illustrate his mediate
concern with a number of more procedural issues,
and so it was only my overriding interest, even
obsession, with this single substantive subject
that led me to run rough shod over the intended
purpose of his note.  This, of course, was very
insensitive, and quite out of keeping with that
brand of regard that has become the hallmark of
our discussions here.  And the instance of such
a behavior, on my part, is something that I can
say that I genuinely regret.  I shall take more
care with that aspect of my conduct from now on.
