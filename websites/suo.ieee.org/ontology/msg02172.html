I got onto this particular thread a little late. So please forgive me if 
I am stating the obvious.

1. My understanding is that the proposal is to have an import `statement'
(import url formula)

2. To allow references within the formula to other ontologies O#ref

Some comments:

1. Programming languages have dealt with this problem with varying 
degrees of success. There is actually not much that is specific to 
ontologies per se here

2. An expression such as O#ref is quite hard to give a semantics for. 
Consider the reasonable version: fun(Arg)#ref. Yes, this will come up in 
practice. Does the O part have an independent interpretation? (op.ed. It 
should if you want to make life easier for yourself)

3. A simple story looks like this:

A micro theory (sic Cyc) is a tuple of predicates and other items.
An item may be named: foo:item

(Yes, this does smack of higher order; but at least its explicit)

Exp#foo refers to the item named foo in the tuple of named items in Exp. 
More generally, you could allow foo to be an expression: foo is 
evaluated in the context of Exp (much like a letrec)

import(url) refers to the contents of the expression located in the url.

So, a full import of an ontology looks like:

import("htttp:.....")#formula

where the interpretation of this is analogous to the LET

LET x1:pred1
        x2:pred2
       ...
      IN formula

which should be familiar.
