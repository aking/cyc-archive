Matthew West wrote:
> 
> Dear David,
> 
> Let me at least explain how I use the word "individual".
> 
> Let me say first what it is not (to me in a formal context).
> 
> It is not a class, and it is not a relation (or relationship).
> 
> It is something that has existence in space time,
> though this may be in some possible world.
> 
> It is something that has a sense of wholeness about it
> (very difficult to define).  It is not just people.
> 
> It includes activities as well as static things.
> 
> Some examples:  me, my car, my planned holiday next year.
> 
> If I compare it to instance, I use instance as a role
> relative to some class.  But classes can be instances
> of other classes, so not all things that are instances
> are individuals.
> 
> Regards
>       Matthew
