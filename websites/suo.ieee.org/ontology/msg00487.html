I support Pat Hayes' (and others) argument about the nonsense of a 
formal vote about Ian Niles' document (I would abstain, if forced to 
vote).

As many people have noted, I believe that the main point of this 
document is that it is a concrete stimulus to focused work. In this 
sense, it is a useful working document, to be hopefully complemented 
by others.

The document has also the merit of being a first courageous attempt 
to integrate different (and disparate) approaches. Unfortunately, it 
has very serious technical drawbacks, which have been pointed out in 
Pat and Matthew's comments, both for what concerns the axioms and 
(even more seriously) the comments.

Hopefully, these drawbacks will be solved by further (painful) work 
of those people who will have the time and energy to collaborate. I 
will do my best to give some feedback, although I am committed to 
work on a top-level ontology proposal funded by an European 
industrial project 
http://www3.eureka.be/Home/projectdb/PrjFormFrame.asp?pr_id=2235, and 
honestly I can't afford following too many discussions and at the 
same time doing concrete work. We are planning to submit a document 
presenting the main choices of our ontology by the time of the IJCAI 
SUO workshop deadline. This is a "natural" deadline, we have already 
agreed on; by that deadline, I hope that a good number of similar 
documents will be submitted to the workshop; *these* could form the 
"base" for further work, along the methodology proposed by Chris 
Partridge.

I also agree with those who have noted that the critical part of this 
merged ontology is (the adaptation of) Sowa's top level, which is the 
glue that in Ian's mind would guarantee the global coherence. I 
believe we need to be patient, and keep on trying to clarify better 
the basic choices and distinctions before trying to use them for an 
integration work.

If we find that some choices are incompatible each other (as *might* 
be for the 3D/4D issue), we may have two groups of people working on 
alternative axiomatizations using the different choices, in the 
spirit of a non-monolithic, modularized development (so that we 
ultimately offer to the IEEE SUO user an "ontological menu'" to 
choose from).

Cheers,

-- Nicola
