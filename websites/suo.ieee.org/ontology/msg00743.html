on while you are still breathing.

>At any rate, I am guessing that you under-estimate my hopes,

Maybe you could tell us how far you are going to try to get to the 
Goedelian limit? Ive never come across anything closer than KIF 3.0

> > LISP 1.5 allows a lot of self-description;
> > but it doesnt give you any quantifiers.
>
>Yeah, but, it's a generic programing language --
>whatever can be done effectively can be done
>in it -- I mean, you could do it all in BASIC --
>that is, if you like that sort of thing.
>The only question is "How easy is it?"

NO!! Absolutely wrong! Ah, now I see why you have used up so much of 
the world's optical fiber capacity with emails about Turing machines. 
Look, the goal here is not to make the computers DO things, but to 
make the computers able to reason ABOUT anything. You of all people 
should understand the difference between a sign's being used to 
manipulate behavior and is being used to, well, signify. Just being a 
generic programming language is peanuts. The hard part is being able 
to SAY what you want, not just make the symbols dance in some pretty 
way.
