JA: Up till now quantification theory has been based on the assumption of
    individual variables ranging over universal collections of perfectly
    determinate elements.  Merely to write down quantified notations like
    "(For All)_(x in X) F(x)" and "(For Some)_(x in X) F(x)" involves a
    subscription to notions of this order, as certified in the membership
    relations that are exhibited in their indices.  Reflected on pragmatic
    insights and constructive principles, however, these ideas begin to
    appear as problematic hypotheses whose warrants to be admitted are
    not beyond question, as projects of exhaustive determination that
    overreach the powers of finite information and control to manage.
    Therefore, it is worth considering how we might opt to shift the
    foundations of quantification theory closer to familiar ground,
    namely, toward the predicates themselves that represent our
    continuing acquaintance with phenomena.

SR: I think we could look on this like an optical system where
    sometimes the image is in focus and other times it's not.
    Just so, sometimes our minds, mechanisms, and dialogues can
    focus sufficiently to justify the Doctrine of the Individual,
    and other times they cannot.  With dialoguing agents usually
    the more the dialogue and interaction, the better the focus;
    and it's just the same between a mind and its extents ...
    the more the interaction with its environment the better
    its focus with that environment.  Yet our formal logic
    does not allow such a focusing mechanism.

SR: .... me thinks we need to find the focus knob for formal logic ....

'scoptical metaphors i like a lot ...

these thoughts go back to my sophomore year, when i was doing a lot of reading
and taking courses in logic, math, physics -- and one day some sort of triple
interaction occurred in my head -- and i can still remember saying to myself:

1.  why not have a time that is multi-dimensional?

2.  why not have logical quanta -- along the lines of the paradigm:
    subject / predicate  =  particle / wave  =  node / mode --
    instead of those old-hat old-tie logical atoms?

well, that was way before i had even heard of
birkhoff and von neumann's quantum logics,
or everett's many-worlds quantum theory,
and if my karma gets me a vertebrate
brain the next time around i plan
to get right on it, but my time
has been too viscous for that.

still, the basic idea continues to haunt my waking thoughts about logic today.
i mean, logic has always been marketed to us poor dumb bumpkins, then and now,
as the magic beans that wud be gud in any ubiverse, whatever, and so now all of
a sudden it chickens out when you ask it:  hey, what about ubiverses where c & h
aint exactly the same grain of pure cain sucker granules that we chew hereabouts?
and there you will come up against the real limits of the logicains's imagination.

another time ...

jon awbrey
