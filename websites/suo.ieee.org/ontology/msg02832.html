Dear Bernard, dear ontologists

we were really close to announcing OntoMap (http://www.ontomap.org) - a
portal for upper-level ontologies.

So, on behalf of OntoText Lab. I would like to invite all of you to visit
OntoMap.
It is free, it is a form of facilitation, it is pragmatic.
Hope you will find it useful. We started developing it, just because we
needed something like this. With the time the idea grew up a bit.

There are few IMPORTANT things to be considered:

- the underlying inference engine and other software is not seriously
tested, there could be bugs and crashes. Please, report bugs at
support@ontomap.org

- there could be some incompatibility problems. It is a known problem that
some of the pages doesn't show up in some Internet Explorer 5.x browsers (it
seems to depend on the Windows Service Packs in a way). There are no such
problems with Netscape.

- we are open for suggestions on improvement and extension of the
functionality of the site

- of course, we are open for comments on the content, it should be the most
natural thing within the SUO-community :-)


You will find more information about the nature of the portal at
http://www.ontomap.org. I will try to give you an idea with few words: you
can think about OntoMap as for Ontolingua server that is more specialised
for upper-level ontologies, a bit more informal, and much more easier for
people unfamiliar with frames. Currently, you can find there Upper Cyc,
EuroWordNet Top Ontology, some 250 top concepts of SENSUS, WordNet 1.7 top
(unique beginners and top nouns), others.


Best Regards,
Atanas
