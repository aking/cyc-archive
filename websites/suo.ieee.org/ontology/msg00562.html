Dear Jim,

A process that I know works well is for a group of about 3 experts
(representing a range of expertise and interests, so say 3 of Pat Hayes,
John Sowa, Nicola Guarino, and I'll include myself, but there are others
also suitable) to meet for 2-3 days with a facilitator once a month for some
period of time (say 6 months). Each meeting reviews and corrects the work of
the previous meeting, and develops new material (about a 50/50 split in
time). After 6 months the material considered in good order is published to
a wider audience (before the new stuff gets so big that anyone drowns in
it). Feedback is elicited from the wider audience, probably through 2-3 day
workshop(s) in different regions (of the world). Repeat as necessary.

I'm not sure that this is practical (mostly funding).

What I know doesn't work is meetings of large groups of people (10 or more)
where the membership changes from meeting to meeting, with this changing
group doing the development work.

To make e-mail work requires some discipline. It is easier on resource, but
because effort is fragmented, continuity becomes difficult. We could make
the e-mail stuff work better than it does.

Regards  
      Matthew
