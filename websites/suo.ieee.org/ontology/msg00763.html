MW: Happy to oblige.

MW: In a 4D world, as I recall, I said that there exists a set for every
possible combination of things (including sets). The good news is that we
don't have to be interested in them. The ones we are interested in have some
significance and we give them a name and generally call them classes. These
are abstract objects and do not exist in (i.e. are independent of) space
time, but do exist in some sense. Relationships (actual or otherwise) are
the same. For every pair (triple, etc) of objects there is a relationship.
It is an abstract object, in that its existance is independent of space
time. Again the good news is that we don't have to be interested in many of
them. Those that we are interested in we put into relations (actual or not
as may be) i.e. we classify them. I rely here in part on the fact that
relations can be derived from sets.

MW: Now that is an exposition of the background. The important question is
what does that mean when I want to say, perhaps, that something was
purchased, say our lawnmower?

MW: There is an activity which we can denote by the constant a. A is a
member of the class Purchase, which is a subtype of activity. At the start
of the activity there is a spatio-temporal extent b that is classified as a
whole lawnmower (whole here indicates the spatio-temporal extent that is the
lawnmower for the whole of its life). More particularly there is a state of
this that is owned by a matching state of the person or company that owns
the lawnmower, in this case Carter's. There is a timeless ownership
relationship between these two spatio-temporal extents (all the time
elements are in the states that are related, so the relationship is always
true and timeless). The purchase activity causes the termination of the
state of the lawnmower that was owned by Carter's, and the start of the
state of the lawnmower that is owned by me. The event(s) are the temporal
boundary of the states and are at a point in time. Whether there is a gap
between the ownership states is arbitrary.

MW: What you will notice is that what I am describing here is somewhat more
precise than what is normally represented. This is why I say that a 4D
ontology is well placed to define and interrelate other ontologies, which is
where my primary interest lies.
