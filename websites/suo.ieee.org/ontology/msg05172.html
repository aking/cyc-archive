ZOO.  Discussion Note 3

o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o

In the "alpha level" of Peirce's logical graphs
and the finite part of Spencer-Brown's calculus,
before it gets all loopy and auto-semantic, the
logical arithmetic that supports the algebra is
isomorphic to a two-partition of "rooted trees".

For various reasons, it is useful to extend the
primary arithmetic to the species of graph that
is usually known as "rooted cacti", and this is 
the focus of the work that I have been doing in
the area.  You may find an organized discussion
of it in the chain of links that I append below.

Jon Awbrey
