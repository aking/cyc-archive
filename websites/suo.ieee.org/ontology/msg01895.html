Dear Chris and John,

I wasn't going to mention this, I've been picky enough on what
is an excellent document, but since John does.

> Chris,
> 
> It's a bad sign when the choice of relation names causes so
> much confusion that you have to release a clarification of the
> clarification that was intended to resolve the confusion.
> 
> Question:  If you need clear names for successor and predecessor
> relations, why not use the terms "successor" and "predecessor"
> or something like "Next" and "Bfor"?
> 
> I admit that "+1" and "-1" are shorter and more consistent
> with LISP.  But for the formal definitions, they seem to
> suggest a compound of "+" or "-" with "1".  (I ran into the
> same problem when I was trying to explain Peano's axioms,
> and I decided to err on the side of long names rather than
> confusing names.)  

MW: I agree with long names to eliminate (OK reduce) ambiguity,
so things like numerical-successor, mathematical-function (examples
only not proposals). I know it takes a bit more to write, but it
(the whole ontology) only has to be written once. Hopefully it will
be read hundreds of times (or more) so we should think first about
the reader (if we want our work to be used and useful).
> 
> For everyday human consumption, the alternate names "+1"
> and "-1" could be added as synonyms in the KIF library
> of handy-dandy useful stuff.
> 

Regards  
      Matthew
