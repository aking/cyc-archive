Dear Robert,

Please do not hesitate, subject to the availability
of your time, to provide us with any sort of tutorial
on those aspects of "Relative Homological Algebra" (RHA)
that you think may be relevant to the work of designing
an overarching conceptual framework for ontologies.

I do not know if it is a related idea, but in my own work
to generalize category theory to the point where it would
be a tool that is better suited to dealing with relations --
where I use the idea of a "formal relational arrow" (FRA) --
I often speak of arrows that are "known by" or "owned by"
a given agent, community, interpreter, observer, or whatever,
meaning that the "system of interpretation" (SOI) in question
contains this arrow as a part of its "resource base" or its
"standard equipment".  What this means, just in very rough
terms, is that each arrow acquires an extra parameter that
tells which agents "know", "own", or "see" it.  These sorts
of arrows can then be generalized to very arbitrary varieties
of relations, and even to those do not have defined arities
but are more like formal languages in their specifications.

Yours Truly,

Jon Awbrey
