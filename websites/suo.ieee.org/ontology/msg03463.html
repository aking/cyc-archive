JA: > But I would like to ask that you not single me out if you

a big deal about it in public, dispute it, and ignore it.  

JS:         Also, the reason you are getting the most attention is you
post the most messages of this nature (I won't use the word research and
more).  If you cut your quantity of postings way down, nobody would care.
 That is one solution, but I'd rather see you relate (directly) your work
to the work of this group, if that is possible. 

JA: > This tactic of saying "pick a document, any document" and
> let's push some standard words around on paper so we'll
> look official does not seem to be a line that anybody
> else buys.  

JS: Good spin but hollow point.  We are chartered to try to develop a
document.   You're going to need to find a way to help with developing
such a document or won't be permitted to post so much. 

JA: I get the sense that this compulsion goes
> back to a sense of a proper timeline for the project.
> If you are on the 2 year plan and others are on the
> 10 year plan, then it is inevitable that there will
> be some friction about what we should be doing at
> this point in time.  Since I am scarcely alone in
> taking the long view, I believe that this issue
> is already one that belongs on the main floor.

JS; Honestly, I think it could easily take 10 years.  IEEE give us 4
years to start, 1 of which is already gone.  But they give another 2
years without question and more years if the end is in sight.  Plus you
can always just submit a new PAR, so the bottom line is IEEE's time
limits aren't real, but they give a little incentive and can be used to
shut down projects that aren't making progress.   I feel we should shoot
for 3 years from now, but be prepared for a long haul.  But my point has
been that your nature of posting will never get us anywhere if they don't
result in standards words on paper, not in 10 or 20 years.  

JA: I feel that this WG will not be able to

> connection as any of its proposers,

then resolve each of your comments?  Or, is there some other way you can
tie your input directly to improving IFF.  You can ignore SUMO if you can
relate your work to IFF.
