Ian,

I agree that the work you have done is a useful beginning.
In fact, it might be a very good way to focus our attention
on something concrete, instead of the aimless commentary
that we have been engaged in for the past several months.

Request:  In order to help us address the "trouble spots"
that require further work, could you please send us some
information about the following points:

 1. What additions have you made (other than translations
    to KIF) beyond the original source files you started with?

 2. What assumptions did you find it necessary to make in
    order to fill in the gaps between the various sources?

 3. Which areas seemed to raise the largest number of questions?
    And where did you need to make modifications in order to
    make the source ontologies fit together?

I still agree with Pat that the current version of the merged
ontology is not yet suitable as a "base document".  But perhaps
we might come closer to something concrete if we jointly
addressed the "trouble spots" that you have uncovered in your
work.  It might help us to make some progress toward a truly
collaborative ontology effort.

John Sowa
