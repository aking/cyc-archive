I regret that I have not been reading SUO at all for some weeks
now - due to other time pressures. However, this message seemed 
worth responding to, even though it is old.  Pat H. discusses the
'gensym fallacy' that the computer knows exactly the same amount
even when the names in the axioms are replaced with arbitrary symbols
such as "G2456734".  We all agree that this is true.

I also agree with Chris Angus that it is beneficial to convey what the
intended meaning is in (if necessary) copious quantities of text with
positive and negative examples. 

A major (if not the only) reason for axiomitizing something is to rule out
unintended interpretations. We can never rule them ALL out, so machines are
always going to be prone to making an inference that we did not intend. However
this will happen LESS, if the humans know from reading the text how they are
suppposed to model things, given the definitions of what terms mean.

Mike Uschold
