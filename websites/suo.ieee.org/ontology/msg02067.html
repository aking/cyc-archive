Title: RE: SUO: Excessive volume of effort for far too few results

Dear John,

Your points about HTML are well made.  However, your "bottom" line borders on being totally unconstructive criticism.  Although not the point on this occasion, HTML is equally "useless" for pretty printing on paper and other tasks that are all relevant to real business practice.  Instead, as with all the activities of this group, given that we are largely vounteers, people need to be able to put things on the table "as is" and the rest of the group needs to be creative in using the offerings.  A collaborative working environment in XML is a wonderful dream but I do not believe anything is available and the perfect solution would rely on linking technology (bi-directional links, links into arbitrary parts of a document) that is not yet in products.  However, I suppose that others are already working towards the necessary capabilities and perhaps something for our purposes exists despite the cash limitations that we would face.  We are frustrated because we have bumbled along with the limitations (however creative Jon Awbrey manages to be) of plain-text e-mail and each one to their own reply and comment style and technique.  On the other hand, surely, in terms of extracting the nuggets, the human effort is as important as the technology and we must seek to co-ordinate this effort as much as is possible.

Cheers,
Tim.
