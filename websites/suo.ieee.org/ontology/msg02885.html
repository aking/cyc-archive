When you say "imaginary color", which aspect of that
is the most important to you, the color or the image?

If you want to talk about the property of a thing that we call its color,
then I would call this property an "object", merely because it is an object
of discussion and thought, and thus an object of the signs that go into this
process of talking and thinking.

If you want to talk about the impression of a color that we have in our minds,
then I would call this mental impression an "image", and I would tend to treat it,
in the first instance, as a type of "sign", one that resides or flits through the
minds of creatures like us -- though, of course, anything at all, even this image,
becomes an object of discussion and thought as soon as anybody talks or thinks
about it, like we are now.

Now, I use the word "referent" in conformity with a whole user community --
and this is an army somewhat larger than the paltry platoon of Peirceans --
so take your pick, and pay the consequences of doing so.
(Do as we say -- and nobody will get hurt?)

> Sign emitter:  Josiane Caron-Pargue

It is a popular option to parameterize signs by the various sorts of agents
that bear them in various sorts of ways, where "agent" can mean a community,
a person, an animal, a machine, a divinity, and so on.  I have just sought
to transmit to you what I consider to be the benefit of my experience, and
the benefit of what my experience has taught me is a quantum of good advice
from Peirce, that this particular way of doing things will ultimately lead to
despair, but then, I did not take Peirce's advice either until after I had gone
out and personally experienced some of this despair.  So it goes.  At any rate,
you may consider the fact that the particular role of transmitter or receiver
is of secondary importance here, since people are always talking to themselves
as they to talk to others, if you catch my drift.

> Purpose:  Illustration of a technique for analyzing certain kinds of discourse
>
> (??)

Purpose is good.  But consider this, for a pragmatic thinker "purpose" is
a synonym for "object", that is, "objective" (aim, business, concern, end,
goal, intention, interest, 'pragma').  All of which raises an interesting
question:  What is the relationship of this "big object", that is to say,
the "big idea", the overall objective that an agent is directed toward,
to the "bit object", in the present case, the property that is being
talked or thought about at the moment in question?

In this connection, one of the reasons why 3-adic relations are so important
is that they are what it takes for you to focus on a "little object" without
losing track of the "big object" of which it forms a part, in other words,
to put your effort into getting from A to B without forgetting that you
were doing this order to get from A to Z.
