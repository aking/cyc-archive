> Let me offer what I think would be a more helpful heading

Okay so to use the paper-clip and worms pictoral form,
your "domains of definition of a relation" are groups
of worms. Doing the worm to marble mental 'transform',
each marble is separated into different hoops, with each hoop
specifying a different 'argument position' of the relation.
Of course, since the hoops are venn diagram hoops, any particular
marble may actually be in more than one hoop at the same 'time'.

Your "source" relation is (when viewed extensionally) a hoop full of
paperclips that all have the same colour (intention) as the hoop.
The associating of the relation with the domains (hoops) 
of its 'arguments', is again a paperclip connecting that
relation's hoop-as-worm with the domain's hoop-as-worm.
It will be generally a shade of the NTH-RELATION or NTH-SUBCLASS
colour, and in general be a different colour from your source
relations's colour.

So you are introducing a "target" relation which is similar
in shape to the source relation except that the colour of the hoops
and paper-clips is different from the source relations's colour as
well.  (Technically,if the source and the target express the same
intention, they are the same colour, and hence the same intentional
relation, so the reduction relation is simply the identity relation.)

Your FOG (form of generalization) is just saying that your reduction
relation is extensionally just a hoop of paperclips itself.
Each paperclip then is binding a particular source-relation-as-worm
and a particular target-relation-as-worm.

> In many situations, we may speak of this relation between
> source and target relations as a relation of "projection",
> but one will most likely be able to think of other brands
> of relations between relations that are not apt to fall,
> nor very aptly to be cast, under this "projective" pall.
> But never mind all that now -- it is best to start with
> the easy examples and the familiar facets of phenomena.

Right any particular reduction relation's colour may be a
shade of the "projection" hue. (I hope this analogy doesn't
cause you to cry)

> 
> In this view, to speak of the "place-j domain" of a relation
> is to speak of what remains to speak of in the relation when
> we choose not to speak of what resides in the other places of
> the relation.  

Right. this "place-j domain" is what I'm envisioning as a 
particular argument hoop of a relation.

> That is to say, if one pictures a k-place relation
> as a k-column relational data table -- "But is it a table already
> filled or a table yet to be filled?" you cleverly ask, and I will
> have to let my silence pass for the promise to think on an answer
> anon -- then one may think of this table as being set, that is to
> say, quite literally defined, by the specification of those very
> k projections onto those very k target domains of which we desire
> to speak.

No.
you are saying that the table (hoop of marbles in my picture)
is defined by the hoops of argument domains that are paperclipped to it.
You are forgetting that the hoop of marbles also has an intensional
definition (colour) of its own. As to how filled the hoop/table is,
it depends on if the hoop/table has an extension. And that fact is
totally off topic (I think) from your current focus of conversation. 

>  And that is "sort of" how the "a la cartesian product"
