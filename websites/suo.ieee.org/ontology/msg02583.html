> >Thanks for your comments. You are correct that the framework

> >prevent such definitions, either. The SUO Dictionary is open for users to
> >contribute material in any form.
> >
> >I see this as an advantage, not a limitation. While I agree that our goal
> >must be to have an ontology stated in a formal, computable language, to
> >supporting automated inference, etc., I think that in the early stages of
> >ontology development people may need some flexibility in how they create
> >their common working document(s), to allow exploration of new ideas.
> >
> >Besides this flexibility, and openness to different notations,
> and different
> >levels of formalization, the other key requirements that I would
> propose for
> >a "SUO Common Ontology Development Architecture (CODA)" are:
> >
> >web-enabled
> >open to input from multiple authors
> >an authors input is easily published on the web, directly by the author
> >readers can view any author's contributions
> >authors can only edit their own contributions
> >teams of people can collaborate to develop consensus
> definitions, or agree
> >to disagree
> >multiple ontology efforts / directions can be pursued in parallel
> >support for namespaces within each ontology effort
> >
