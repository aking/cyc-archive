John, Jon, Pat,

Here are several questions about semantics ranging from general to specific.

1. What are the papers that discuss the relationship between Tarski's
semantics [attached PDF] and Peirce's semiotics
[http://www.clas.ufl.edu/users/jzeman/peirces_theory_of_signs.htm]?
2. More specifically, can we relate (identify in some sense) model-theoretic
interpretations with sign relations:
    interpretation corresponds to interpretant
    constant, function, predicate symbols -- c, f, p -- correspond to signs
    interpretation images -- I[c], I[f], I[p] -- correspond to objects
3. In particular, what is the nature of the interpretation image I[c]? Can
this be a "real world object", notwithstanding what John says below, in the
World part of the diagram
[http://www.bestweb.net/~sowa/ontology/mthworld.gif], or does it have to be
a "surrogate" existing in some formal semantic domain? For example, in the
sentence "John Sowa is less than ten feet tall", can I not define an
interpretation that maps the string "John Sowa" to the actual individual
within the domain of actual individual people, or do I need to interpret it
as some surrogate such as the email address sowa@bestweb.net?
4. Are sign relations completely relative -- can their objects be thought of
as signs in a different sign relation? Can the real world objects in the
diagram above be regarded as signs?

Robert E. Kent
rekent@ontologos.org
