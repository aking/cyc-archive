Note 110

¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤

| If the eye were a living creature, its soul would be its vision;
| for this is the substance in the sense of a formula of the eye.
| But the eye is the matter of vision, and if vision fails
| there is no eye, except in an equivocal sense,
| as for instance a stone or a painted eye.
|
| Aristotle, "On The Soul", in 'Aristotle, Volume 8',
| W.S. Hett (trans.), Heinemann, London, UK, 1936, 1986.

Ontologenesis, Again.  In the Big Inning of the Game of Life, there
was the "wonder of living and worlds of things in view" (WOLAWOTIV).
Sometime after the kick-off-toss-up-start of the game -- and I know
that it genuinely looked like a good idea at the time -- there came
to be set up, as if the passive voice could declaim the blame, this
"instituted domain of language" (IDOL), initially set up all in the
admirable hope of referring to the sacred wonder that was the world --
but, and you always knew that it was coming, however long in coming
it might have been bound to be -- things started to go a little off
kilter after that, due to the propensity of the IDOL setting itself
up as some kind of almighty autonomous object of worship, as if the
third person reflexive could deflect the blame from the worshippers
of the fright thereof.

Not that I want to be taken for an iconoclast or anything, but what
my critique of non-functional reasoning is supposed to be achieving,
in time, in good time, is just this examination of the IDOL that we
call "FOL", not with an eye to its destruction -- okay, maybe a bit
of deconstruction -- but soully with the aim of reconstructing this
icon of the times nearer and dearer, not only to our heart's desire,
but ever more nigh unto its instigmatory and its sole true function
within our hopefully rational orientations to a world of experience.

Now, does that sound like such a bad thing?

Good!  Then let us continue ...

Jon Awbrey
