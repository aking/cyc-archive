Oh, I did not mean to imply that you do not have your reasons
and your sources, and I am fully aware that there are several
long traditions, falling under the banner of "conceptualism"
that speak this way.  It is just that I cannot join in with
them, as I suspect that their future will be as their past,
bringing no end of confusion between signs and objects.

Okay, but writing axioms is a fairly risk-free activity.
I know that it should not be, not in principle, anyway,
but that is just how it most often turns out in practice.
People will generally warp their logic into all sorts of
weird shapes before they will give up on a favored axiom.
So we hardly find much in the experience of writing axioms
that would drive us to shift our comfortable foundations.
