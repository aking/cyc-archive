All,
There are subtleties and potential ambiguities at every turn.

It does appear that relation and relationship have two different senses or
referents (in some cases one, in some cases the other)
when used by different members of this forum.

Here is a rough trial baloon for an agreement to distinguish the
referents for  SUO usage:

A relation of arity n, n>0,  is symbolized as predicate with n arguments.
The extension of a relation of arity 1 is the set of things in the domain
of quantification
for which the predicate is true.  The  extension of a relation of arity n>1
is the
subset of the nfold cartesian product of the domain of quantification for
which
the predicate is true.

A relationship is an instance of a relation.    E.g.,  married(X,Y)
describes a relation.
married(Bill, Hillary) describes a relationship.

I've probably missed some subtleties (or even gotten it backwards), but
I hope this is unambiguous enough to be ammended.

Dear John,

The real problem with relationship and relation is that they are frequently
used both to refer to classes and instances.

For example, when we talk about the lines on entity relationship diagrams,
we call them relationships (perhaps more properly a class of relationship)
yet we also talk about my marriage to my wife as a relationship
(occurance).

In logic I notice that the more experienced tend to use "relation" for what
would be a class, or predicate, and not the occurances of it. It is not
clear (to me) that there is an established terminology for the occurances.
