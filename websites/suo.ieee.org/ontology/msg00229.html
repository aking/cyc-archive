To keep interested parties posted on the state of current collaboration
between the DAML and OIL efforts at developing ontology-markup language
technology for the semantic Web, I include below two brief reports on a
recent meeting between people from these two programmes (in Washington DC
last week).

The announcement of this joint EU/US committee can be found at
http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-rdf-logic/2000Nov/0093.html

Technical discussions concerning this effort can be found at the
www-rdf-logic list at W3C, archived at
http://lists.w3.org/Archives/Public/www-rdf-logic/2000Dec/thread.html (and
similar URLs for the archives of Nov and Oct).

The notes below are from a recent meeting of this group.

Frank van Harmelen.
   ----

After two months of weekly telephone conferences, the first face-to-face
meeting of the Joint EU/US ad hoc Agent Markup Language Committee was
organised in Washington DC on 7-8 December 2000, to coincide with XML 2000,
the major industry event on XML technology.

The day before the face-to-face meeting, Tim Berners-Lee (director of W3C,
and member of the Joint Committee) gave an invited talk at XML2000 outlining
the vision and architecture of the Semantic Web, mentioning DAML and OIL as
core ingredients. A journalist report of Tim's talk can be found at
http://www.xml.com/pub/a/2000/12/xml2000/timbl.html. The slides for his
presentation can be found at
http://www.w3.org/2000/Talks/1206-xml2k-tbl/slide1-0.html

Participants:

    Tim Berners-Lee (W3C, by telephone 1/2 day)
    Dan Connolly (W3C)
    Mike Dean (DAML, committee chair)
    Frank van Harmelen (OIL)
    Jeff Heflin (DAML)
    Jim Hendler (DAML programme head)
    Ian Horrocks (OIL)
    Ora Lassila (W3C)
    Deb McGuinness (DAML)

New DAML language release:

One of the major outcomes of the meeting was the decision to produce a new
release of the DAML-O markup language. This release will be called DAML+OIL,
and will closely follow the proposal made earlier by Ian Horrocks and Peter
Patel-Schneider (see http://www.cs.man.ac.uk/~horrocks/DAML-OIL). This
release is expected before the end of the calendar year.

Ian Horrocks will serve as editor for the DAML+OIL release.

Future evolution of the Working group

  1.We anticipate that W3C will convene a Working Group
    (probably the RDF Schema Working Group) to develop an ontology language
  2.We will package up our then-current language specification
    (more below) and contribute it to the Working Group
  3.We anticipate that W3C will convene a public Interest Group
    (probably www-rdf-logic) to focus longer-term on rules and logic.
  4.We will proceed in parallel with the Interest Group, providing it
    with drafts and experimental results as they become available.
    Many committee members are likely to join the Interest Group.
    Since the W3C Interest Group would be public, membership in the
    Joint Committee can be limited to government funded US and EU Semantic
Web
    participants plus invited experts.
    Other groups and activities may similarly contribute to the
    W3C Interest Group.

The W3C groups probably won't be formed before March 2001. Most of our
activity until then will be focused on preparing our current work for
transition.

Official Joint Committee Minutes 7-8 December 2000 can be found at
http://www.daml.org/committee/minutes/2000-12-07.html
