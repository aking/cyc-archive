IOU: The nice thing about the initial puzzle is that it was rendered
     in a flat enough form that one had a chance of telling on what
     grounds -- how shall I say? -- to address, approach, attack it,
     as Caesar might dixit.  Now the whole thing about these sorts
     of puzzles is that they invite the interpreter to make a few
     natural interpretive assumptions, out of charity, perhaps,
     but more likely out of innate instincts and inured habits,
     and then they trap you in being charitable or innocent to
     a fault.  So I would not even begin to touch a natural
     language specimen, encountered in its natural habitat,
     until I had a nimbler grasp of its natural semiotic
     environies.  So that is what I will do, but later.
