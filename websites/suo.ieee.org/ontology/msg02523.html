> That's just the case that raises the problem.  If C1 and C2 have
> contradictory meanings associated with them and both of them have no
> extension (i.e. both of them are associated with the empty set), then
> there is no actual object which is an instance of C1 and is not an
> instance of C2.

And vice versa.  Which is only to say that C1 and C2 are coextensional.

> Hence, the two classes would not be disjoint, according to the
> standard extensional criterion of disjointness.

Time for a coffee break, Ian!  :-)  Though coextensionality implies
nondisjointness for nonempty classes, since C1 and C2 by assumption have
no instances, ipso facto they have no instances in common.  They are
assuredely disjoint by the extensional criterion.
