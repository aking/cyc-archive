I will chose to interpret "K-lunk" in a way that is charitable --
to myself! -- as "knowledge link", but no, well yes, now that you
mention it, anyway, at the time that I said this I was probably only
conscious of thinking about the contrast between declarative languages --
like Prolog, for one, was initially aimed to be -- where the relation
between problems and solutions is described in "purely logical" terms,
and the task of the underlying programming system is to do all of the
procedural dirty work of actually constructing particular instances
of that relation, typically the ones that correlate with the input
problem instance.  But I have also been concerned for quite some
time now -- I really oughta try reading my own dissertation! --
with a more general sense of "underlying dynamics", not always
specified in procedural code or machine code, but implicit in
the action, behavior, conduct, development, and evolution of
the entire "symbolically computable information" (SCI) system,
even as its use is "embedded", "indexed", or "situated" within
the surrounding "community of inquiry" (COI).

The short answer, I reckon, is that I am aware that this desideratum
of mine is "not the first order of play here", but it is just one of
the obstacles against which or around which I will need to make some
headway in order to have any particular use for the resulting system.

You may consider it a reminiscence, or a lapse of nostalgia, if you wish --
I was just trying to remember some of the reasons why I ever took up with
these silicon-blasted machines in the first place!  But I do, after all,
think that it represents a "non-obviable general obstruction" (NOGO)
that lies before our common advance, whatever riot, rout, ruckus
that its e-gress may apparently be constituting at times.
