Jim,

I have a couple of questions about the "general ontology" (GO) list,
and some comments on the current situation..

1.  At the present time, all postings to the main list get
    dumped on the GO list, so people looking for a quiet place
    actually to discuss ontology per se, which discussions might,
    after all, one day have a bearing on the work of the SUO group,
    will still be burdened by readings from the Bit Ching of people
    who seem to require an engraved invitation before they can deign
    to make their magnanimous contributions to the present effort.
    Okay, I did wax a bit sarcastic toward the end there, but the
    substantive point is clear.

2.  If people then reply to postings on the GO list that have
    come from the main list, and if they reply-to-all by habit,
    or otherwise think that they should reply in the direction
    from whence the posting arrived -- well, then we are back
    to Square One.

3.  Square One is that everybody who makes a contribution to the
    main list probably does so because they honestly believe that
    it has a bearing on what a State of the Art Third Millennium
    Ontology System ought to look like, even if that makes some
    State of the Art 1979 folks uncomfortable with the amount of
    work and the number of different expertises that would really
    be required to avoid cranking out yet another standard that
    all innovative people will have to work against from Day One.
    So perceptions about what is "relevant" and what is not, what
    is "general" and what is "specific", are what they always are,
    personal opinions derived from personal or corporate interests,
    and unless people are prepared to argue and to give reasons why
    this or that potential contribution is "on" or "off" topic, then
    I personally have to regard such aspersions as the abuse of true
    collegiality that they really are.

Jon Awbrey
