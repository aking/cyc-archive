I forget where this bit started, so I will just break in here ...

I wish that those of you who fail to see the cogency of various
logical arguments on this score would try at least the following
experiment:  Amble on down the mountain to the ordinary zen world
of your friendly local neighborhood Data Group, and tell them that
due to the latest round of corporate down-sizing, all relational
data tables will now be restricted to just two columns, not two
plus a key or two plus an index, just two, and that this rule
will be strictly enforced right down to the bare bones level
of assembly language, no funny business with hidden indices
and secret keys tucked away to the various nooks and crannies
in which your average computer geeks know how to squirrel away
all of their best kernels'o'wit if not to say their bits'o'honey.

What will happen?  Most likely, from experience, one of two things:

1.  They will go away mumbling something about "turd normal farm",
    and wondering what brand'o'moron or other rough beast has now
    come round alas to rake over the company.

2.  Having learned, from experience, to be play-along sorts of folks,
    they will say "Sure, Boss! Get right on it!", and go away doing
    precisely what they always do, knowing that none'o'you prophets
    would ever have even the first clue where to look for the nuts.

Just me, talking to no one in particular,

Jon Awbrey
