> liking for the 4-D (Perdurantist) view - at least for the tasks I am
> interested in. However, I think it is always useful to look at other
> solutions and the route they take.
> 
> The possibility space seem to be:
> 
> 1. Every occurrent has a continuant of which it is the life.
> 2. Every continuant has an occurrent which is its life.
> 3. Every occurrent has participant(s)
> 4. Every occurrent is constituted by its participant(s).
> 5. Every continuant participates in some occurrents.
> 6. Every continuant is constituted by its participations in 
> occurrents.
> 
> 6 is there for completeness - but I am not aware of anyone strongly
> advocating this.
> As you indicate there is no compulsion for the Endurantist to 
> accept any of
> these.
> 
> One could also have restricted forms of the theses - e.g. 
> every (occurrent)
> war is constituted by its participants.
> 
> One can also look at whether these is a corresponding 
> Perdurantist thesis. I
> think that 4 is interesting - and intuitively comfortable. The
> spatio-temporal extent of every 'event' is the fusion (sum) 
> of the (relevant
> temporal stage of the) participating bodies. This seem to 
> imply that we
> divide up events using bodies as our guides. So there is no 
> portion of an
> event that does not belong to some participating body.
