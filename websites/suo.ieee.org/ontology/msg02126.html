Graham,

Some comments on your comments:

>GH>We are talking of out Knowledgebase being redeveloped in XML, but I
>have never had any contact with it. I take it XML flows in a reasonably
>smooth way from HTML? 

Yes, both are based on SGML, but the X in XML stands for
"extensible".  One extension of XML is XHTML, which is
compatible with both HTML and XML.
 
>HTML is even more
>anaemic than Word in these aspects, but then I have had to trim my style for

>e-mails anyway. What I haven't tried yet is to see whether using Word as my

>e-mail editor would allow me to send my e-mails in HTML.

The HTML generated by MS Word is horribly bloated.  But for
ordinary e-mail, I would prefer plain text -- it's more
compact, easier to edit, and universally available.

If I need to include diagrams or special formats, I create
HTML files and put them on my web site.  (And I wish that
everyone else would do something similar.  I would much rather
click on a URL to look at somebody's web site than to get
large files stuffed in my input mailbox.) 

GH>I think people around here expect people to silently overcome all
>such problems themselves and not bother anyone. Half the time, the IT
>support response to a crash, etc. is "do it a way that doesn't causse the
>problem", rather than fix the problem.
