Frederick,
   I'm very surprised by this.  The current proposed ontology explicitly 
allows a representation of time.  It includes a notion of temporal 
intervals, specification of clock time and dates, relations between 
temporal intervals, relation of events to metric time etc.  Specifically, 
it includes Allen's temporal ontology.  What do you think is missing?  Do 
you have even an informal specification such as an English sentence that 
you don't think could be represented using the ontology?
   The example I provided earlier today shows a particular example of how 
the current ontology supports temporal information.  Here it is again.

English:  Someone purchased Carter's lawnmower at 12:15, August 12, 
1999.  The lawnmower is a Honda CB250.  The purchase price was 500GBP.

Database tuple: <purchase, Carter's, lawnmower, Honda CB250, GBP, 500, 
12:15 12th August 1999>

SUO-KIF:
   (thereExists (?EV ?L)
     (isa ?EV PurchaseEvent)
     (isa ?L Lawnmower)
     (objectTrasferred ?EV ?L)
     (fromPossessor ?EV ?C)
     (name ?C "Carter")
     (productManufacturer ?L HondaCorp)
     (productModelString ?L "CB250")
     (paymentExchanged ?EV (BritishPoundsFn 500))
     (existsAt ?EV (MinuteFn 15 (HourFn 12 (DayFn 12 (MonthFn 8 (YearFn 
1999))))))
