Just by way of contributing my invisible bit of information,
one who started thinking very deliberately about the problem
of "pragmatics as context-dependent semiotics" some 15 years
ago might be able to suggest that context is just one of the
factors that influences the actual transitions from signs to
interpretant signs in regard to an object, and that therefore
an appropriate conceptual framework in which to begin is the
study of sign relations, both descriptively and normatively,
which latter study would be found to shade off into logic.
