Matthew, your answer below is rather longer than I expected.  To precis my
understanding of your POV.

0. A spatio-temporal object is something in the real world, and can be
represented by a constant.

1. A spatio-temporal extent is something in the real world, and is
different from a spatio-temporal object.

2. An activity is something in the real world.

3. An activity is a spatio-temporal extent

4. A relation is a representation (and therefore not in the real world
unless you are qualified to study advanced topics)

5.  Activities can be modeled as relations.  The example is modelled as
multiple dyadic and triadic relations.

6. Attempting to model a activities as a single n-adic relation may be
adequate for some purposes, but is inadequate for intercommunication
between systems, and by implication for the SUO.

How am I doing so far?

Dear Martin,

I note this is just us. If that is an accident feel free to post this
thread.

I try to be careful to match representation form to what they represent, so
I recognise a spatio-temporal object as something that first exists in the
real world, and second has a consistent representation in the information
world, so as a constant such as a, b, c, fred, etc. A spatio-temporal
analysis of activities in the real world reveals them to be spatio-temporal
extents. They consist of the temporal parts of the objects that are playing
a role in the activity. Thus I expect them to be represented as
spatio-temporal objects, i.e. by a constant, as above.

Actual relationships exist in the same way that actual classes do. They are
abstract objects that are out there that we can take an interest in and use
to organise and think about the world. Actual relationships are what one
thing has to do with another. I note that a relation like:
<purchase, lawnmower, Honda CB250, GBP, 500, 12:15 12th August 1999>
does not represent a relationship, but actually a number of relationships
that each element of this construct has to an object that is not part of,
but may be inferred from this construct.

So in analysis I look for recognition of this. I would analyse the above
into:
<Purchase a>
<Purchaseof a b>
<Datetimeof a 12:15-12th-August-1999>
<lawnmower b>
<HondaCB250 b>
<hasprice HondaCB250 c>
<GBPvaluemap c 500>

Or something similar. So I have extracted and represented the actual
relationships.

Not that there is anything wrong with the original data structure in a
purchasing system, but you do have to extract what it means if you are to
make that available without everyone having to learn the language of every
other system.

Does this provide an answer?
