Hi Francis,

I very much appreciated your criticisms of loosy web related ontologies.
However I think you miss the point entirely with :

(from: http://www.fipa.org/mailman/private/ontology/2002-December/000096.html)
 
> 4. the software engineering implied by the automatic merging of
> ontologies is horrendous. It means that there is no sense of a
> definitive version of a concept - since anyone can extend it.

You see this a the problem, yet, THIS is where a solution must be found!

Given that NO ontology will ever be frozen to a "definitive version",
unless it becomes "dead" and useless, the real problem is how to 
reconcile two diverging instances of a given ontology on the spot.
And only temporarily for the purpose of the task at at hand at this 
very instant.
A while ago I argued a lot on the SUO and CG lists about the fact 
that what is needed is not a "common ontology" but a common brokerage
protocol but nobody catched up except may be, partly, Philippe Martin, 
another "evil" frenchman.

(as a summary and entry point see http://suo.ieee.org/email/msg08138.html)

> 5. there are no mechanisms for handling the history of a concept; which
> again makes writing software to a specific ontology risky to say the
> least.

Right, but you need much more than history.

The fools will waste millions an may be billions on ultimately useless
so called "ontologies", but this seems to be the way "progress" is made,
at a very high cost for shareholders and tax payers :-)

Best.
