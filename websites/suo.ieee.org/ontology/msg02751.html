Jon, 
	.	I can summarise all your responses below by reacting to your
claim: 

*	"I have done nothing but tell you the upshot of the lessons I've
learned since I came on board.  The catch seems to be what it's always been,
that unless you have had a similar experience, then nothing resonates enough
to get through what you'll just go on believing till it happens to you".  

	.	My reaction remains the same it has been for a long time
now, and is that, unfortunately, you never give concrete examples that allow
verification of your claimed experiences.

	.	When you give examples, ones that are self­evidently
unarguable, or that the experts in the group mostly support you on, then we
will have to sit up and take notice.  

	.	Accordingly, I suggest you change your approach, and
concentrate on substantiating your claims, rather than just stating their
upshot. The more fundamental and self­evident the substantiations, the
greater the persuasive power they will have.
