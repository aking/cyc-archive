> >I vote NO.

> >Comments: I don't mean to denigrate Niles' hard work, but it is

> 
> I agree with Pat that Niles has done some hard work in putting
> together the work of several other people whose efforts have
> been merged (including some of my own).  But the act of
> translating those independent collections of axioms into a
> common notation does not cause them to be integrated.

I agree that reformulating a bunch of different ontologies in the same
syntactic notation is not very useful.  However, what I have offered to the
group in both versions of the merged ontology goes way beyond this.  It
attempts to integrate the SUO sources into the same semantic base by linking
their primitive concepts into a simplified version of your (John Sowa's)
upper-level ontology.  If you could point to some examples where I have
failed to do this sort of alignment or have done it incorrectly, I could use
this information to produce an ontology whose concepts are better
integrated.

> 
> Pat continues:
> 
> >It has no overall coherent ontological model; it represents 
> a melange 
> >of conflicting and incommensurate views on many basic 
> ontological issues.
> 
> I agree with the first line above, but I disagree with the
> second line that those collections are "conflicting".  On the
> contrary, they are consistent with one another for the very
> simple reason that they are unrelated to one another.  Whether
> they become "conflicting and incommensurate" would depend on
> how they are used together.  But the really hard work involves
> determining how or when they could or should be used together. 

I don't agree that the merged ontologies are conflicting or unrelated to one
another.  As I mentioned above, I have tried to tie all of them into a
simplified version of your (John Sowa's) upper-level ontology.  If you can
indicate to me where I have failed to relate (part of) a merged ontology to
the superstructure, I will revise the document accordingly.

> 
> I agree with the general tenor of Pat's further criticisms,
> but there is much more to be said about the details of the
> issues involved:

I agree, but without detailed comments/criticisms, I have no basis for
correcting/extending the original two versions of the document.
