SUO,

I vote NO (re establishing Ian Niles' Merged Ontology as a base document),

reasoning as follows. 


The group is in a productive challenge-response mode. 
The Merged Ontology should be a working paper.
Formal votes should be taken, after discussion, on each
of the several ways it is claimed to be fundamentally flawed:

1) Requirement for a representation of time and change, 
2) Requirement for a representation of  alternative courses of reality,
3) Disallowance of incommensurate views unrelated to one another,
4) .....

   -Fred Chase
