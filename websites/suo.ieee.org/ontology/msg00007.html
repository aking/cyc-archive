Factorization SIG,

Précis of the Plot So Far ...

Graphical Conventions:

By way of representing projective relationships in this
ASCII-affine space, where only certain angles of lines
are allowed, to wit, elements of {"-", "/", "|", "\"},
I am using "parallel lines of identity" (PLOI's) to
collapse, equate, equivalence, identify, quotient --
or select your own favorite term of identification --
ostensibly multiple nodes into one, depicted like this:

      @
      =
      @
     ...     or     @=@=...=@=@
      =
      @
      =
      @

Typographical Conventions:

I will use "sharp brackets" around terms, as #A#, for bold face type.
I will use "scrip brackets" around terms, as $A$, for curl face type.

Illustration of One Brand of Factorization:

Example of a Certain Type of Transformation on Sign Relations,
To Wit, a Mapping of Type $R$ -> $R$, Where $R$ is a Suitable
Category or Collection of Sign Relations, To Be Nomenclated
As a Phantom Limb Extension (PLE) of Sign Relations in $R$.

> MW:  By individual, do you mean whole individual
> (e.g. me for my whole life) or do you allow any

See the thread where Peirce examines the "doctrine of individuals".
As a rough guideline, I would most likely start with what he says.
As with so many terms like this that we have all been given the
habit of treating as if they referred to an absolute attribute,
a basic being, a constant category, a dedicated determination,
an essential element, or a fixed feature of an object in the
world, I will tend to ask the question whether the condition
of being an individaul is not more like a role into which an
actor is cast than a cast that it bears as an indelible type,
in sum, whether it is more likely to be an "interpretive" or
a "variable" category than an "essential" or an "invariant"
category, and the answer that I almost always come up with,
except in the rarest of cases, at least, once I stop in my
headlong rush of thought long enough to fairly examine the
question, is "yes".
