I agree with Chris:

>As far as I can make out almost all of the main contributors to the list
>have suggested that we need to find some way to include a variety of working

>papers, including the Teknowledge (Daimler-Chrysler Merger) Paper.

I'm one of the people who voted in favor of Ian's document,
as long as it was considered a working paper.  The following
proposal is consistent with that view, and I would be happy
to endorse it:

>> We declare the Teknowledge Merged
>> Ontology to be one of the documents being worked on by the Working Group

>> (and Ian its editor).  Any member may post other documents to the list of

>> documents being worked on by this WG.  The member who posts the document

>> will be presumptively the editor or may name one by general consent of
>> the WG.

However, I don't believe that there is any need for any vote,
either pro or con on a working paper:

>>Objections will be resolved by a vote.  All documents will be
>> posted in the same place and enjoy the same status.  None will have
>> precedence over others.

The only reason for a vote would be to make a decision about
giving priority to one WP over another as a basis for a draft
SUO standard.  At this stage, such a vote would be premature.

My view is essentially the same as Chris's:

>I certainly vote for this - in fact, I thought it had been all but agreed.


Basically, I think we should give Ian a hearty pat on the back
with the statement "Keep up the good work."  But at this stage,
that is all we can say:  it's a good contribution as a working
paper, but we still don't know exactly where, how, and if it
will fit into the overall goals for the SUO project.

I don't believe that we need to take any votes on what anybody
volunteers to do with a working paper.  In standard-speak,
Ian's merged ontology is an "expert contribution", which should
be assigned an appropriate identifier, such as SUO-WP-2001-001,
duly noted in the minutes, and placed on the SUO web site.

John Sowa
