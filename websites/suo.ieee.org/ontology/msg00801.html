One of the distinguishing marks,
or the characteristic asymptoms,
of a "Flat Earth Belief System" (FEBS)
is this:

The belief -- I call it a "belief", but it is believed so earnestly
that it becomes tantamount to an anxious terror, a performance anxiety,
or a form of stage fright -- that the whole wide world drops away at the
apron of one's stage, the border of one's dais, the edge of one's map,
that beyond those terrortorial limits "there be only monsters", chaos,
and void.  Let us call this virtual edge the "Cliff Of Godot" (COG),
because it would forever have us waiting on the glassy knoll-edge
of the tiny bit of the cosmic plot that we already know, and not
just know, as in some way or another, but really, really know
in that "already been chewed" (ABC), basically deductive,
fully formalized, and thus completely staged way.
The only question that remains for us is this:
Can we ever escape the escapement of this COG,
once it has begun tick off our eternal souls?

Just A Little Bit To Think About,
But Not Too Long A Spell I Trust,

Jon Awbrey
