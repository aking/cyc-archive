Just for underlining the interest that can present the point of view of the
relative homological algebra in view to define "regional ontologies" which
could be useful for the standartisation of specific domains. It could allow
to reduce the complexity of the problems approached on the SUO. The
relative homological algebra was defined by EILENBERG and MOORE (Foundations
of relative homological algebra, Memoirs of the Amer. Math. Soc. n° 55,
1965) by specifying the morphisms of a category as "permitted morphisms" and
by requiring that certain number of properties is verified, the essential
being property according to the compound of two permitted morphisms is also
a permitted morphism. A similar approach, but maybe more binding, consists
in working in categories of diagrams of definite schemes, every scheme being
associated to a the "architectonics" of a domain .
