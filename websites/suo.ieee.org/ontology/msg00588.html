Email from Jon to Fred   Mon, 29 Jan 2001:

"
It needs to be admitted that divergent POV's almost always appear
to be "incommensurate", at least, at first.  However, to say that
our shared ontology must afford no "ultimately" discrepant views
is a position that is either, to some, just the very definition
of inquiry, that is, a statement of the regulative principle or
the hope that drives it to seek a final home base of knowledge,
or else it is, to some, just the most abased misunderstanding
of what it means to have any POV, at all, in an ontologically
relativistic cosmos -- I have made my choice, some time ago,
but I have no idea of how to lead others to take my outlook.
So maybe the spectre of the incommensal will win after all.
What do you think?  Me, I think that we will need, in the
mean time, the kinds of conceptual, linguistic, logical,
and software frameworks that will allow different POV's
at least to begin to communicate.  For my part, I think
that the formal perspective that is afforded by the use
of sign relations is an irreducibly minimum requirement
for being able to examine our actual activities of using
signs with respect to our prevailing or potential objects.
"
