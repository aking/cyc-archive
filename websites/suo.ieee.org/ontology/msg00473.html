At 18:40 2001-01-19 -0500, Josiah Lee Auspitz wrote:
> 
> 
> The missing definition of 'material interest' would appear to be crucial
> to the protocol, so it is hard to see how to approve it without this
> element. Does the nature of the material interest have to be disclosed? Is
> 'material' intended to contrast with 'intellectual'?

ANSI's definition includes the following:

        ... Further subdivisions that may be used to categorize directly and materially affected persons consist of, but are not limited to, the following: 

        - Consumer
        - Directly affected public
        - Distributor and retailer
        - Industrial/Commercial
        - Insurance
        - Labor
        - Manufacturer
        - Professional society
        - Regulatory agency
        - Testing Laboratory
        - Trade association

The IEEE Processes and Procedures (P&Ps) at all levels (IEEE-SA, CS, CS SAB) do not extend this list.

Yes, "material interest" is different than "intellectual interest".  No, the exact nature of "material interest" (e.g., revealing business relationships) is not required to be disclosed ... we've discussed this aspect before, let's not spend any more E-mail on this topic.
