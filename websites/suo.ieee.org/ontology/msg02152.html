I will try to stay focussed on my task, which is to explain
why all this fuss about "triadicity" that Peirceans make is
more than a just a tempest in their cozy little teapot, but
an aspect of turbulence on wider seas of being that we bold
and imaginative ontologists ought heed the warning signs of --
if you can wink at the dangling of that ominous preposition.

I hope that you will excuse me if I keep my meagre provision
of an outline at the ready -- though I would not bind myself
too fast to its mast, still fathom I must adjust its compass
as we go, especially as I venture into more uncharted waters,
where the fields of even our globe's own compass are regaled
to go completely haywire -- if you will excuse a superfluous
metaphor, if not an act of indiscriminal metonymy -- because
I fear that I would be lost, and may well perish, without it.

A.  What is this triadic relational POV, anyway?

It strikes me that the quickest way to set up the setting for this
particular crowd -- this prospective crew in part -- is to explain
it after the fashion of the design for a table, one that we should
find to serve us best in shaping all of our prospective discourses,
our plans, our quests from this day forth.  No, although it may be
round in some figurative sense, I am speaking quite literally here,
for once, if jest for the sake of novelty, so let us conjure up an
image of a brand of table that all of us will regard as one of the
most basely prosaic that we know, if not, indeed, the staple board
of our daily bread, a relational database table.

All that the 3-adic POV -- henceforth 3-POV -- asks of you is to
entertain the notion of adding another column to the 2-adic base
that all would agree is the minimum, "lowest common denominator",
if you will -- though of course you know the latter means a base
that is over our heads, being the Sup and Lub of our commonality --
that is, the smallest encompassing table of objects and of signs
that we would need to requisition in order to organize any shard
of significant discourse about most anything atoll -- if you can
excuse the shallow attempt to play on an isolated colloquialism.

Sounds simple enough, I know, especially in this day and age,
but even then, even now, with all of our intellectual luxury,
our training is such that we can't help but ask "Why bother?"

Well, many of the best reasons to bother have been before our faces --
some would say "in our faces", as the argot goes -- all of the time
that we motley crew have been animadly animadverting our animi here.
That brings me down -- indeed it does! -- no, I say down to Item 1a.

But I must break for coffee, lest some dis* befall me ...

Jon Awbrey

A.  What is this triadic relational POV, anyway?
    1.  Explain it in terms of 3-adic relation tables.
        Suppose one is given a 2-adic table of objects and signs.
        a.  Option (a).  Add a column for the interpretive agent.
        b.  Option (b).  Add a column for the interpretant sign.
    2.  Aristotle's Approximation to the full-fledged sign relation:
        a.  Assumption (a).  Commonality in the object domain.
        b.  Assumption (b).  Commonality in the interpretant domain.
        c.  Conclusion (c).  Canonically privileged frame of reference.
B.  Bearing of the sign relational POV on several design issues:
    1.  Ontological Integrity versus Ontological Modularity.
    2.  Ontological Invariance versus Ontological Relativity.
    3.  Interoperability among ontological frames of reference.
    4.  Differential topics.
        a.  Change.
        b.  Development.
        c.  Diversity.
        d.  Evolution.
        e.  Inquiry.
        f.  Learning.
    5.  Critical Reflection.
    6.  Dynamics of Inquiry.
        a.  Informational dynamics.
        b.  Psychological dynamics.
        c.  Sociocultural dynamics.
