Well, I will not quibble, for now, about the "locus of reality" (LOR),
and which of our instances is nearer to it, but I am pretty sure that
you and I will have many myriads more occasions to use sign relations
that are isomorphic to those in the Story of A and B before we happen
next to think on the purchase of a new lawnmower -- at least it shall
fall out that way in my particular Nex Of Web, my clime of world, and
my time of the year -- but never mind all that now, back to your tale.

Now, here -- here, now -- I find that I must quibble.
For what you say is what I understand well enough if
I grant you the grace of a poetic license (licence?),
but since we are striving to be just as picky in our
reading of the letter of the lore as we possibly can,
since we are straining to watch our P's and Q's even
as the moving finger sets them out in blackened writ,
well, then we must look to see if we can see what it
is that is really going on here, anyway, in, and for,
and all around and about the neighborhood of the new
transaction to which your currency is adverting here,
the one that lies behind, beneath, beyond all of our
many fine, but not so final signs.

So let us 'really' try to think about what is 'really' going on
in the database system, keeping mostly to the superficial level
of the software system, but not staying altogether oblivious to
what is going in the physical system that embodies the computer,
roughly analogous, if only through the sequence of by and large
historical accidents that we e-phemize as "incre-mental design",
to that species of biological system -- I did say "roughly"! --
that makes up our minds, so far as we know.

Let us say that you load up and initialize your favorite relational
database system -- I've got mine ready to go! -- and then you enter
the following sequence of signs into a relational database table of
the appropriate number of columns, breaking up the string as you go,
and parceling them out to the waiting table in the accustomary ways.

"<purchase, Carter's, lawnmower, Honda CB250, GBP, 500, 12:15 12th August 1999>"

> It might be said to represent a purchasing activity.
> However, you will note that there is no single element
> in this tuple that represents that activity, only the
> class of activity (purchase).  Similarly, there is no
> object that is a surrogate for the actual lawnmower
> purchased (e.g. its serial number), but only the type
> of item bought (lawnmower) and the manufacturers model
> (Honda CB250) and even this has the manufacturers name
> bundled in.  Since this is an international lawnmower
> shop, they have unusually given a unit of measure for
> the price paid, and we have the date and time of the
> purchase.  It is highly likely that there is only one
> activity that all these elements relate to.  But the
> question is, does this refer to an activity, or is it
> just a relation of elements that are each related to
> an activity and together uniquely identify it, but
> is not a surrogate for the activity itself?  I would
> argue that it is the latter, but custom and practice
> means that many would accept it as the former.
