Robert,

I wrote:
> Whoops, that was a boneheaded mistake on my part; I forgot my own
> terminology: "null" is the name of our distinguished (NOT to say unique)
> null class; as a class -- i.e., property -- it also has instances, viz.,
> all the classes that have no instances.  "nil", as you note, is the name
> of the empty sequence.

As I just pointed out in another message, I *still* didn't get my own
terminology right here.  I used "empty" in the axioms, not "null", to
denote the property of having no instances.  Consequently, let me try
again.  I had said:

  I can see that there is an ambiguity in my original statement.  I said
  that, according to the axioms, every class has a complement and hence
  (complement top) is null.

What I should have said was, according to the axioms, every class has a
complement, and hence (complement top) is *empty*, not *null*.  And this
really does follow from the axioms.

I think that that -- finally -- really does clear things up, doesn't it?

-chris
