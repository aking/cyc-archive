Forgetting the analogy for the moment, the point I'm trying to make is:

Some F2F contact is better than none, even if not everyone can have the same
amount.  This is precisely because F2F interaction for some things is MUCH
better than email, and is thus likely to produce something of HIGHER VALUE.
Lets compare the likely quality of the document with:
1. no f2f at all
2. some f2f, but not equal amount for everyone. 
   The worst case may be that there is a lot of f2f by a small few, and none by
   the majority.

If there is a clear and compelling argument that says the overal quality of the
document will be WORSE in case 2, will someone please articulate it?  To me the
opposite seems much more likely.

One might argue that even if the quality was worse, it would be better because
it was more fair. If it WAS more fair, then this is a matter of opinion. But I
don't buy the unfairness argument.

The people who had no f2f have every opportunity to evaluate the results of
f2f-assisted input. It seems to me a great BOON to have small groups go away
and settle things among themselves, and them present the results back to the
group.  It is more efficient.

Mike
