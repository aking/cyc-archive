SUO Folks, Especially Chris, John, Pat, Mathhew --

I think it is practically essential that the SUO embody
a "soundly understood ontology" of mathematical objects,
in particular, relations and tuples, especially since
these sorts of objects, notions, constructs, concepts,
or whatever you wish to call them, are the very sorts
of things that are typically used to build models of
other sorts of stuff in the world.

But before the SUO comes to embody such an understanding,
it will be needful for those who construct or design it
to arrive at such an understanding themselves, and our
current discussions around the topics of relations and
tuples do not, from my perspective, appear to reflect
any such understanding yet.

This is a problematic that looms rather larger than the question
of a "minimally adequate resource base" (MARB) for the ontology
of relations and tuples, but its practical consequences and its
logical implications are currently being thrashed at there,
and so it seems just plain golf to "play it where it lies".

Thus, I would like to dedicate this thread, not in the sense
of "in memoriam", I hope, but in the sense of concerted work,
to the task of coming to a "solidly underfoot organization"
of these concepts.

And now my prologue is logged.
It is time to face the music.

Jon Awbrey
