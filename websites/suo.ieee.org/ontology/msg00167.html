Norbert,

Your last line is something that Peirce and many of us who
have read his work would emphatically agree with:

>Especially the "why" is often lost in formalisation as we all know 
>too well when we try to understand somebody else's program.

You could summarize Peirce's entire philosophy as an attempt
to get "why" recognized as just as worthy of formalization
as "what" and "how".
