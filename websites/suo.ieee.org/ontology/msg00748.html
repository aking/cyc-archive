>
>MK> A complication is that the word relation, in the singular, is used by
>Ted Codd (and I believe generally by mathematicians) to mean the set of
>tuples, whereas the word relationship, in the singular, in natural English
>can only mean either a single instance, or a type of, relationship.  Thus a
>relation (singular) actually represents many relationships (instances).

In my dialect of English, one can say that a certain relationship 
holds between two people and that the *same* relationship holds 
between two other people. If A and B are in love and so are C and D, 
then both couples are in love. Which is about as close as one could 
get to saying that both 2-tuples are in the relation-set called Love.

As I said a while back, there really isnt any need to distinguish the 
individual case from the entire relation, sems to me; they are both 
relations, but one is larger (more inclusive) than the other. If 
type/token issues loom large with you, then by all means be careful, 
but I often don't bother to distinguish between me and my singleton.
