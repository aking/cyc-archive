JA: For future reference, I record here the sixteen propositional forms on
    two logical variables, expressed or illustrated in four different ways.
    Also, I include two families of convenient nicknames that I frequently
    use for these functions:  The "F" series codifies the "truth table" of
    each proposition as a binary numeral in its subscript.  The "f" series
    employs as its subscript the decimal equivalent of this binary numeral.
 
DW: I can understand the different forms, but why not include
    some traditional ways of calling the functions, like Sowa
    did in his KR book?

DW: f_0 is FALSE
    f_1 is NOR
    ...
    f_15 is TRUE
    etc.

david, an afterthought to tack onto the previous message.
i probably ought to emphasize here that the primary interest
is not finding new ways to notate low-dim cases, but to design
a calculus that facilitates exact calculations in larger numbers
of variables -- tens, hundreds, thousands of variables, eventually,
and also a calculus that does not inhibit or obstruct other types
of extensions and integrations, for example, assertoric/hypothetic
and declarative/functional syntheses, along with the differential
and the higher order propositional extensions (dif log & hop log).
i re-introduced the functional pictures specifically to prepare
the way for exploring a certain continuity between the logic
of inquiry and the laws of information that peirce foresaw.

jon awbrey
