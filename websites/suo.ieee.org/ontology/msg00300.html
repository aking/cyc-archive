>
>Ontology maps very well in to the concept of graphs. For example a single
>term can be a node, and an axiomatic relationship can represent an
>edge/branch.

Not quite. Since an axiom can use more than two concepts or terms, in 
general the relevant structure is a hypergraph, which is similar to a 
graph but allows 'edges' which link more than two nodes. The theory 
of hypergraphs is somewhat more complicated, and less thoroughly 
investigated, than that of graphs.
