John F. Sowa wrote:
> 
> Jon,
> 
> To give a brief answer to your question,
> 
> > Re:  "Is Icon A Proposition?"
> 
> I would say that an icon, by itself, cannot be a proposition
> any more than a symbol, by itself, can be a proposition.
> 
> Peirce in various places made the point that a formula in
> logic or more generally a sentence of any kind cannot state
> a proposition without the addition of at least one indexical
> to pin down the referents of its terms.
> 
> An icon could be used as part of a proposition by combining it with
> an index of some sort.  As an acquaintance of mine once remarked,
> "I can point in any language."  That pointing is an index, which
> is also incapable of stating a proposition by itself, but it
> could be used to state a proposition by combining it with
> icons or sinsigns (i.e., existing objects, which serve
> as signs of themselves).
> 
> John Sowa
