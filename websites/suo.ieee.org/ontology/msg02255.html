> These are not details. They are part of the meaning of (gives ?x ?y ?z).

What counts as a detail is relative to context.  I fully agree that,
relative to the purpose of axiomatizing the meaning of "gives", 1), 2),
and 3) or the like are not details.  But that wasn't the purpose of the
above.  Rather, its purpose was to illustrate the correct the logical
*form* of the analysis of "gives" in terms of events -- in particular, to
indicate the need for the existential quantifier, and the correct method
of expressing uniqueness.  Relative to that purpose, 1), 2), and 3) are
just distracting details.

> > correct approach to capturing the idea that standing in the giving
> > relation is equivalent to playing a certain role in a certain unique
> > event.
> > 
> > That said, can't more than one person give more than one thing to more
> > than one person in a giving event?
> 
> Not if the type of giving event is (gives ?x ?y ?z).

I'm not so sure.  It is not obvious to me that that the meaning of
"gives" entails that when A gives B to C, that A *alone* gives B to C.
If it doesn't, then, if both John and Martha give a book B to Mary, it
is correct to say both (gives John Mary B) and (gives Martha Mary B).
But if that is right, then your clause 1) above is false; the same
giving event can involve more than one giver.
