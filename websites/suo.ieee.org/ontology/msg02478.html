John F. Sowa wrote:
> 
> Jon,
> 
> Your concerns about the current state of the SUO
> are shared by many subscribers, including me.
> 
> > And I guess that my sense of the current scene in Ontology Systems
> > is that we have not even come close to what could be achieved if
> > we would just set to work with the tools that are laying around
> > waiting to be used like we meant it.
> 
> The best style is one that is not noticed as such.
> The problem with your style is that it overwhelms
> the substance.

I do not know if you have noticed this, or even care to,
but I do have more than one style of working.  When one
of them starts to dry up, and any one mode persisted in
for too long always does eventually, I am simply forced
to shift to another.  I wish I could pull a Kierkegaard,
and construct a separate authorship for each style, but
I realize that people would not take too kindly to that.
People who know how a mind is purchased will understand
that one cannot just go out and buy another one, and so
they always appear to be more tolerant of the momentary
equilibrations in each other's being.  If all of that's 
really too much to ask, if you cannot tolerate with any
equanimity how things balance out in the log term, then
I guess I have no choice but to keep this Pan outasight.

> Pat Hayes, for example, has also criticized the current proposals,
> but in terms that leave no doubt about the point he is attacking.
> For everyday communication, bluntness is more effective than subtlety.
> 
> John Sowa

I have been as blunt as I know how to be.
I do prefer the chisel and even the rasp
to the dull, blunt objection that leaves
no stone left to sculp.  My critiques of
the ways that we presently implement our
rationality have cut to the root of many
customs that are still taken for granted,
but I do not see them being welcomed for
their radicality, or any attempt to test
them out for their potential feasibility,
so I see a lot of hypo-critical radicals
hereabout.  Is that blunt enough for you?

Yes, I have seen some people attack with
the same blunt instrument what was, what
is, what could be, and all with the same
indiscriminate brutality, destroying the
endeavors that were going on to work out
the finer details of a positive material,
say, just for one instance that comes to
mind, meaning-maintaining-meta-morphisms,
and the entire spectrum of variations on
that theme.  So, if you prefer that form
of dull insensate bluntness, then I wish
the lot of you the content that shall be
its reap.  Is that blunt enough for you?

Dully Submitted,

Jon Awbrey
