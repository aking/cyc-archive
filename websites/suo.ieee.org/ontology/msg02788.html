I'm just curious about something.  If a flock of geese (in formation)
exhibits agency, what about:

* a swarm of bees?
* a culture of slime mold cells (collecting to form a stalk)?
* a crowd in a panic?
* a gameboard of Conway's 'Life'?
* particles in a multi-body physics problem?

I'm wondering about collective behavior or structure that looks
"organized" but that might have "intentionality" ranging from high to
none.  To me, "Organization" as a label implies intentionally-created
structure, rather than naturally-emergent structure.  

That is, if you would call a flock of geese an Organization, but you
wouldn't call a willow tree an Organization (even though we can observe
very rich and useful structure in it), it seems the difference is our
assumption that the geese are actively choosing to be in formation.  If
so, where is the cutoff between awareness, instinct, reflex, and low-level
chemical/physical processes that tell us when we're looking at agents or
organizations?  Somewhat off-topic, I know; I just found it a curious
problem.
