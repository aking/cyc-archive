Again, I have no special collection of axes to grind or bones to pick with KIF --
never even heard of it until a year or so ago -- bur I have been examining the
syntactic, semantic, semiotic, and general pragmatic properties of languages,
natural and technical, gross and subtle, for as long as I can remember, and
I crafted my first experimental langauge for a specific technical purpose
somewheres about in the 4th grade.  As far as logical calculi go, I pretty
much quit using, for the sake of my own thinking, the notational system that
builds so shakily on the familiar alphabet {"~", "&", "v", "=>", "<=>", ...}
as soon as I happened on the far more exacting and the far more powerful, for
all conceptual and practical purposes, logical sytems of Charles Sanders Peirce
and George Spencer Brown, sometime in the period 1967-1969, some very fuzzy years.
I gradually grew and scruffily sctibbled these two genre of logical graphs on paper
constantly, working in parallel under both the "entitative" and the "existential"
interpretations, and eventually evolved a few extensions of my own, discovering
the item that I sometimes still call the "boundary operator", on analogy with
a construct that figures mightily in algebraic topology, on the period around
1976-1980, some more hazy days.  This operator is the germinal root of both
the "reflective" and the "differential" extensions logical graphs, that
now constitute Ref Log and Dif Log, respectively.

So, to make a long story bear on the current point of application,
I would criticize in the just same way any concrete syntax that
proposes to use the factitious family of specious primitives
that are commonly symbolized by the means of the unsouper
alphabet {"~", "&", "v", "=>", "<=>", ...} and its ilk.
There are compelling formal and overwhelming practical
reasons for regarding any such system with the same
sort of disregard that anybody would have for the
unhappy motley of roman numerals, if shanghaied
as a crew in a futile venture to pirate the
differential and integral calculus.

I have mentioned all of these facts with all due consistency from
the very beginning of my service here, long before I ever dreamed
that something called "KIF" would become an issue -- it was when
David Whitten first asked for feedback on a Starter Ontology,
if I recall -- and the only thing that surprised me then
and still shocks me today is that it was news to anybody
in a group that numbered such lights as I regarded JSF
to be.  And the fact that I must report this anywhere
else but on the Main is to me a sign that some folks
have indeed understood perfectly clearly what I have
been saying all along, a leading indicator of just
how much force of arms lies behind certain fixed
arrays of economic and hidebound interests, and
a symptom of just how sick the dynamics of
a group can become, whenever it abdicates
its duty to inquiry under the influence
of that whole ilk of unreasoning force.
