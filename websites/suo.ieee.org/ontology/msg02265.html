Matthew,

Okay, kibbitzing on this pas de trois has helped me
to see a little more of what's going on here.  I was
going to comment on the details of what I see being
thrashed out here, but I think that I will take the
novel step of following my own advice to others, and
try to take two or three steps back, and speak to the
longer run view of things.

You are to be complimented for your persistence here,
and also for the realistic intuition that is leading
you to recapitulate in your ontogeny many aspects
of the perspective that has been developed in the
phyllogeny of mathematics and its applications to
dynamic systems.  And if anyone is responsible for
the fact that the underlying philosophy and working
attitude of this POV is not more widely appreciated
and recognized for what it is, I guess that I will
have to blame its generations of doctrinarians and
expositors who were apparently so concerned with
the appearances of their outer dress that they
would not dare to show you their workclothes.

Still, I must repeat the suggestion that I have made before,
pointedly on the "Manifolds of Sensuous Impressions" thread,
that the very like of all of these issues have been tackled
before, if perhaps in a deliberately quantitative disguise.

Yes, there are Realities and there are Representations.
Books have been written on the relation between these
two subjects, if not necessarily in that order.

Any Reality to which we are likely to point, appreciated
in all of its glorious splendor, is just bound to be far
more complex and vastly more subtle than the likes of us
fallible and mortal finite information beings could ever
hope to express in full, and so we arch-typically allude
to it under the aegis of "X", our Persean Shield, if you
will.  Here is where, I am beginning to suspect, a brand
of misapprehension first arises between us, for you seem
to advert to this X, arrogated as a realm in reality, or
to its typical element x, conceived as an individual, as
if this move were a simple step, even a simplifying step,
in our approximal approach to the twined reality, x in X.

Well, there is more to talk about here, but maybe I will
stop and check to see whether this accords with what you
see yourself to be doing, as you reflect on your customs.

Happy Monday!

Jon Awbrey
