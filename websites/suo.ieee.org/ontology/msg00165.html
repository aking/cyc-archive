SUOphists,

This will be a generic volley, after which I vow to
return to my more fitting and pedestrian occupations.

One of the ways that we can waste, not just the next 2, or 20,
but a good chunk of the next 200 years, is by failing to heed,
analogously speaking, what is most likely to be suggested by
some of the lessons that have been learned in other fields.
There is no assured application, no certifiable transfer,
of course, from one domain to another, but just by way
of our considering what a diverse spectrum of samples
from our human "Dialogue In Nature" (DIN), including
the "Nature Of Our Selves" (NOOS), may be trying to
tell us, I think that it behooves us to pay
at least a bit of attention to other fields,
especially those with some history under
their bootstraps.

So let me just point out, yet another time, the potentially deadly analogy,
for our sakes, between that old "Myth Of Determinism" (MOD) and our present
temptations to indulge ourselves with this new "Myth Of Unambiguity" (MOU).

Just to be clear about my use of the word "myth", just in case my ample respect
and utilization of polymorphous mythologies is not already enough in evidence,
a "myth" is a story that we are likely to already believe, long before we
ever think to reflect on the circumstance that we do, much less begin
to examine it critically, to wit, in the light of whatever else
we may happen to believe, or to say that we know.

e-nuff said,
e-yeow rite,
e-now again,
e-back tuit,

Jon Awbrey
