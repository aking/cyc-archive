English being not my native language, I am a bit unclear if "Upper" in SUO
scope is understood as a comparative or a superlative.
My dictionary tells me there exists a superlative "uppermost", but
translates "upper" to "supérieur" - used in French both as a comparative and
a superlative, which does not help much and indeed looks like the linguistic
mark of a deep cultural bias (if you are better than everyone around, you
are absolutely The Best)

So I wonder ... maybe some people here think Upper as a superlative, and
really aim to build an Universal Uppermost Ontology at the highest
meta-level ever conceivable. I must say that if it is really what it is
about here ... well, good luck and count me out.
I have kept myself away from SUO for months because I really felt like that
was the spirit of the thing. I won't point out at thousands of reasons to
believe that this is a completetly Unreasonable Unsustainable Unuseful
enterprise from every viewpoint: technical, commercial, philosophical,
cultural, human ...

But if we are aiming here to build some useful unified ontology (uuo),
bringing together a bunch of well-tried-and-proven concepts, definitions and
rules having been tested here and there with some efficiency in a wide scope
of so-called middle-level applications, and leveraging them in the frame of
some coherent meta-level language, without asking endless metaphysical
questions about their rights to sit or not in the Holy of Holies, that is
completely another story, sustainable from every above quoted viewpoint, and
able to gather IMO a wider effective task force among all the communities of
middle-level standards and specifications, well aware of the Babel syndrom
and eager to leverage quickly their languages somehow. And building from the
middle (kernel - center), as it has been suggested by several folks here
lately, looks to me quite the obvious path. It's maybe because it's where I
stand from, but OTOH most potential users of any uuo seem to stand also in
that middle and won't move easily ...

So, folks: comparative-u or superlative-U ?
