From: "Jon Awbrey" <jawbrey@oakland.edu>

> without even trying to give a complete answer to that,
> i would be willing to venture, as i've always ventured,
> that one object of logic is "to denote objects", in the
> many senses of the word "object", in the objective world.

An I am willing to venture that this denotation of objects is ~not~ the goal
of logic but a mere diversion, and is mostly a fantasy of pie in the sky.
Rather the goal of logic is to get agents to agree.

> these contemplations bring us to the following consideration:
> is it possible to make comparisons among different languages
> for zol with regard to their differential practical adequacy
> to represent the whole system of objects found in the zoo?

Yes.  The more often agents who talk only in well formed formulas of the
language agree, the ~better~ the language is.

Tis better to be in sync, than to be true.

Seth Russell
