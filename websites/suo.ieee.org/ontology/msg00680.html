Fred, Ian, Matthew, & All,

The thesis that I would like to contribute to this thread
is that the "ostensible question" (OQ) of needing to deal
with change is a "no-brainer", that is, as much as to say:

| What question!? -- Of course, we would be "dead in the water"
| alright, already, or "up the creek without a paddle" without
| the ability to adapt to change!

Aside from the fact that I am glad some other people are starting
to move "Toward More Picaresque Speech", the real question is not
whether we have "it", but whether "it's catching" and whether we
can indeed "give it" to our ontology -- which is so far little
more than the "rational oncology of the metacaucus" -- to wit,
whether our "irritation of doubt" and our "sense of uneasiness"
can be "transmitted" or "transmutated" from our native carbon
phylogeny to the minëd brand of silicon ontogeny that we are
prospecting to craft and to cast on the waters hereaboats.

And I will 'hic et nunc' submit to you all that 'that' question is not so much
a question of "whether we do it" -- see the above "no-brainer" -- but "how".

And on that issue, I do have one or two or three choice ideas,
on which I shall no doubt have "just a bit" (JAB) more to say,
but later.  You do understand "later" doncha?  As I suspected.

Sincerely Yours,

Jon Awbrey
