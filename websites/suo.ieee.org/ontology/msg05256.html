JP: Benjamin Kuipers' book 'Qualitative Reasoning:
    Modeling and Simulations with Incomplete Knowledge',
    is all about QSIM, qualitative differential equations.

Yes, I followed the work of his group, and also that of Ken Forbus
on Qualitative Physics, all through the middle 80's to middle 90's.
But I guess I have a different sense of how it ought to be done,
starting from the fundamentals rather than tacked on ad hoc.
Still, I believe that Kuiper's work is about as good as it
can get coming at the subject from that particular angle.

There has long been a study, with many standard texts already out there,
called the "qualitative approach to differential equations", but this
is more like the "differential approach to qualitative equations".

I will append the links to my own minum opus.
there's also a couple of briefer and prettier
introductions that Murray Altheim did up for me
with his A.P.T. text-to-hyper system, and that are
stored here:

http://www.altheim.com/cs/index.html

Jon Awbrey
