At 25-03-01 21:09, Chris Menzel wrote:
> > Maybe I'm not alone with in this state of confusion.
>
>Of which my blunder is at least a partial cause.  Sorry about that.
>With this cleared up, I can see that there is an ambiguity in my
>original statement.  I said that, according to the axioms, every class
>has a complement and hence (complement top) is null.  But "(complement
>top) is null" is ambiguous between (= (complement top) null) and (null
>(complement top)).  I think you took me (quite reasonably) to mean the
>former, but what I meant was the latter, and that is indeed provable
>from the axioms: (null (complement top)) means that (complement top) has
>no instances, which is provable from the axiom that everything is an
>instance of top and the axiom for complement.

### Correct as stated now, of course. Many thanks for the clarification Chris!
