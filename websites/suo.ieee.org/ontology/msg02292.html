MW: (To Jon Awbry)
>I have never felt the compulsion to be required to say
>everything about something as a prerequisite to recognising
>something's existance. I expect to have to say enough about
>something so that others can distinguish the same object,
>and what ever it is that I wish to communicate about it,
>but not more.

Quite; well said.

>MW: I also pick up from between your comments that in
>logical circles "=" means precisely "stands for the same
>thing". We do not have such an operatotor in data modelling,
>so I have been applying a stricter test of "is the same
>thing". It is useful to know the local conventions.

No, "=" does indeed mean "is the same thing (as)", in logical circles 
as much as elsewhere. So if one writes "2+3 = 5" one is saying that 
two plus three is the same thing as five. Of course, this could be 
rendered, somewhat obliquely, as saying that "2+3" stands for the 
same thing as "5", but that last assertion is about the quoted 
strings, not about numbers and sums. And of course the claim
"2+3" = "5"
is simply false, and one can see by looking at it.
