Well I stand with Jon here: the place to start is with 'tiny domains' ...
bottom up ... that which is closest to our senses.  Any standardized
ontology must always be primarially a political act where 'being right' is
not the salient issue, rather what is most important is just what we can
agree upon.  So to start at the most innermost (SUO calls it upper) concept,
those which are the most subjective and personal, would seem to be a big
mistake.  For example we are more likely to agree that LAX is the name of
the airport in Los Angeles than to agree that physical is disjoint from
abstract.
