Thursday, February 08, 2001, 6:38:28 AM, you wrote:

JVHc> Occurents and continuants:

JVHc> I'm certainly new at this, but I was becoming comfortable with the idea
JVHc> that occurents depend necessarily on contintinuents  (my life depends on
JVHc> me),
JVHc> but that the continuents are not parts.  (I am not part of my life).
JVHc> Mary's smile
JVHc> depends on Mary, but isn't part of Mary.   The stabbing of Ceasar is a
JVHc> stabbing
JVHc> (occurent) that depends on Ceasar, Brutus, and a certain knife, (all
JVHc> continuants),
JVHc> but these aren't  _parts_ of the specific stabbing.     This seems quite at
JVHc> odds
JVHc> with much of the continuant-occurent discussion I see.

JVHc> I've been away most of January and February till now, so I've certainly not
JVHc> read
JVHc> everything in this thread, but the idea of an occurant being a _part_ of a
JVHc> continuent
JVHc> bothers me.  I'm not sure if this makes me a non-extensionist...

Suppose the situation could be described in more easy way . I'll use
the EPISTLE Core model. Such the "occurent" will be an "individual
event" with such "class properties" for an event as "time", "place",
"the agents involved" etc. And the continuants will be "the agents" of
"physical object" type.
