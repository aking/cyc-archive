  I would like to comment on Philip Jackson's question:

> why should voting to Abstain allow one to keep a voting privilege
> while not voting could cause a loss of voting privilege? -- isn't not voting
> effectively the same as voting to Abstain?
    Voting to abstain is a more affirmative action than not
voting at all, and such an action could be counted as contributing
to a quorum, where a quorum is required, whereas not voting
at all leaves the group completely uninformed as to one's views on
a subject.  Voting  to abstain, I think, is equivalent to saying that
one is willing to accept the decision of the group interested enough in
that issue to vote on it.  Not voting says nothing at all.
   Requirement of a reaasonable quorum for passage of a proposal is,
I think, an important reuirement for making the votes and the final
document more representative of the potential community
of users.

    Pat Cassidy
