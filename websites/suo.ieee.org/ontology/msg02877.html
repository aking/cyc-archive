Dear, Michael

On behalf of my colleague who was presenting OntoMap in Montreal, I want to
make a small correction:
he said, that he is not subscribed for SUO, but he has a colleagues who are.
I am one of those - a proud owner of SUO folder in my mailer that contains
some 5000 messages.

We do not pretend to have deep understanding of all the discussions in SUO -
it would be really ambitious, right?

As for the open problems - yes there are many. The spirit of OntoMap was -
make something useful. We started graduate:
- first, we made some Java classes to allow better understanding and
management of Upper Cyc
- than we developed it further to support the mapping of the EuroWordnet Top
ontology into Cyc (Michael, you probably remember EKAW2000, and the
"Ontologies and Texts" workshop there)
- next we developed it further to its current shape

We have a kind of partial but very quick proprietary inference engine,
behind OntoMap of course. Michael asked my colleague (his name is Marin
Dimitrov) can OntoMap do the job of FaCT. The answer is NO - it is very
different, FaCT also can't do the job of OntoMap. Of course, we aware on the
best descr. logic machines, the stuff developed under the CG stream. There
was nothing to satisfy our needs - so, we preferred to make our own engine
rather than to start understanding, debugging, and adapting an existing one

Feel free to ask any questions - we will try to answer as much as we can. We
are very open to discuss possible improvements or extensions. Unfortunately,
our resources are limited, so, we will need to prioritise

Cheers
