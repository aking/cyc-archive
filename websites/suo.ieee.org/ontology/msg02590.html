Dear Matthew,

Been there, keep saying that, keep getting filtered out ...

Golly, are we in the same groove today! --
but you gotta realize that these folks
are speaking a whole nuther language --
one that encourages the preservation
of silly puzzles rather than helping
to weed them out of its garden growth.

To adapt an old line from a very old movie:

| Gosh, what a wonderful place the SUO Group is! --
| Yesterday I could not even spell "intension"
| and today I are an expert on it!

See my previous notes on the subject:

http://suo.ieee.org/email/msg04917.html
http://suo.ieee.org/email/msg04931.html
http://suo.ieee.org/email/msg04941.html

Since that time, I went through a sample of classic texts,
nothing specifically devoted to the strict formalization
of set theory in a computational framework or anything,
just loci where the notion of "disjointness" comes up
in passing, and they categorically say something like:
"Two sets, X and Y, are 'disjoint' iff X |^| Y = Null".
This seems to involve the conventional implicature that
the notion of disjointness is intended to have a sensible
application in situations where the sets are not identical
and where one or even both of them are non-empty.  So the
question to ask is "What function do we wish our concept
of disjointness to have?" -- as we are free to craft the
concept, our instrument, as we wish, subject only to the
iron(ic) rule of logic.  Well, if what you need this big
idea for is to facilitate the automatic inference from the
disjointness of X and Y to such an array of propositions as
that X is not a subset of Y (X ~c Y) and vice versa (Y ~c X),
then you really need to find an express way to express your
InTenTion, on which score the expression given still fails
to cut it.  But maybe you can tell 'em.

There is a much bigger issue here, of course,
and that is that we will never crack these
old nutchests of Fregean Fragen until we
leave behind the plane speech of these
Too-Two-Dim Flatlander Logicians and
the Towering Intellects of these
Two-Peg Hanoians and start to
view Logic in the Buena Vista
of a pragmatic and a semiotic
Peirce-spective that lives
in the ambient and ample
solidarity of three
dimensions.  But
maybe John Sowa
can tell 'em.

Cheerio,

Jon
