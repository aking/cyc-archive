Dear Chris,

Thank you for these comments and help with the technical bits.

> Matthew wrote:
> 
> >  (<=> (gives John Mary Book1)
> >       (and (gives x)
> >            (giver x John)
> >            (receiver x Mary)
> >            (given x book1)
> >       )
> >  )
> 
> This can't be right, as the free variable "x" occurs on the 
> right side of
> the <=> but not on the left; it just sits there like a pronoun with no
> associated context to give it meaning.   

MW: x is intended here to be a constant. It is relevant to just this one 
act of giving. Though I would agree it is a bad choice of name.

> Also, you are using "gives"
> ambiguously here. If I recall another of your recent posts 
> correctly, you
> are using "(gives x)" to mean something like "x is a giving", 
> i.e., an act
> or event of giving.  

MW: You are quite right. The two gives have different meanings. In
a later version I have used gives1 and gives2 (or some such) to
distinguish them.

> If so, I think what you're after is the 
> following (I
> change to the more mnemonic (and KIF'ish) variable "?e"):
> 
> (<=> (gives John Mary book1)
>      (exists (?e) 
>              (and (giving ?e)
>                   (giver ?e John)
>                   (receiver ?e Mary)
>                   (given ?e book1))))
> 
MW: Well no, as I said in my response to Fred it is necessary to
ensure that there is exactly one e for this case, which is probably
best denoted by a constant. Clearly there is a parameterised version
of both the initial giving relation, and the alternative
representation, but being slow with these things I like to start
with particular examples that I understand.

> I.e., John gives book1 to Mary iff there is a giving ?e in 
> which John is
> the giver, Mary the receiver, and book1 the thing given.
> 
> Fred wrote:
> > Dear Matthew, 
> > I wonder if the following isn't yet closer to what you have in mind.
> > 
> >    (<=> (gives3 ?d ?r &g)
> >         (and (gives2 ?x)
> >              (giver ?x ?d)
> >              (receiver ?x ?r)
> >              (given ?x ?g)
> >          )
> >     )     
> 
> Same problem with the free "?x" here.  Also, why would gives3 
> take three
> arguments while gives2 takes only one?  And where is gives2 
> axiomatized?
> As for this being closer to what Matthew had in mind, I 
> believe h was just
> giving an instance of what you intend with your more general axiom, 
> though I'd express it as follows:
> 
> (forall (?x ?y ?z)
>         (<=> (gives ?x ?y ?z)
>              (exists (?e)
>                      (and (giving ?e)
>                           (giver ?e ?x)
>                           (receiver ?e ?y)
>                           (given ?e ?z)))))

MW: I'm still not sure that this ensures there is exactly one ?e for
any (?x ?y ?z).
> 
> What all this means for the triadicity debate I have no idea, 
> as I have no
> idea what triadicity in the Mystical Peircean Sense is all 
> about.  I would
> understand the above axiom as nothing more than a way of providing a
> (perhaps) more robust analysis of the giving relation by 
> enlarging one's
> ontology to include events.

MW: You make a good point here that exercising this process involves
enlarging your ontology. Elswhere I have talked about using this
process to help identify things that have been overlooked (like
events/activities in this case). This amounts to the same thing.
