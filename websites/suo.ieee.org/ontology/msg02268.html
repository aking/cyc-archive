Robert,

Yes, if it were purely a matter of logical subsumption,
I would tend to agree with this ordering of the POV's:

|       CT
|       /\
|      /  \
|    ST    GT

And yet, I know myself to be biased in various ways,
not only by innate inclination and inured education,
but by the "communities of interest" (COI's) with
which I happened to spend the most time communing.

But there is more at issue here, which is why I used
the word "dialect" rather than "language", over and
above the substantive matters of the various theories,
that is to say, their common and shared object domains.

There are issues of innate disposition, acquired aesthetics,
institutional culture, ultimate practical utility, and many
other problematics too difficult to fathom, that go beyond
the simple matters of picking a language that works for
the given object domains.  The SUO group, especially,
in its quest for inter-calm and inter-opts, spends
most of its time half-addressing half-avoiding
these cultural and practical issues.

And you must know that "dyed-in-the-wool" set theorists
will say that ST is rightfully elevated to the top, and
that few of them will understand you if you say that it
is precisely the particular form of their transcendence,
their attempt to bite off more than any mortal can chew,
that turns out to be the overweening move that leads to
their eventual downfall and the collapse of their coups.

That is why that I take up here -- why I 'try' to take up here! --
the attitude of "comparative and structural anthropology" (CASA),
the same tack that I take toward my study of many other religions.

From this distance, one can recognize that the spirit of the difference
between the GT argot and the ST patois is very much akin, and perhaps
even the direct descendant of the ancient schism between the regimes,
the regimens, and the "regi-mentalities" of the synthetic geometers
and the analytic geometers.  Now, you will say that this divergence
cannot be so ancient, since it had to wait for Descartes to invent
analytic geometry, but Descartes only drew on a zwei-zeit-geist
that was already prevailing in its split existence at the time,
the more ancient factions of arithmetic and geometric spirits,
that goes back into the murky mists of prehistory, no doubt
to those first range wars between the herders of sheep and
the sowers of wheat.

Back to the present!

The reason why the GT mannerist paints the graph
so shorn of any labels is not just laziness, but
due to an aesthetic sense that these labels are
not constitutive of the structure at all, in the
way that a ST stylist can hardly help but to think,
but that they are in fact an epi-phenomenon to the
true form and substance of the subject, arising from
nothing more than the inconvenient conventions that
force one who would express the form to choose among
accidental, inessential, and particular manners and
mediums of coordinate representation.  Accordingly,
the GT genreist thinks to have presented the viewer
with a picture of the graph that is closer to the
truth, nearer to the invariant idea, by leaving
the coordinates off.

Many Regards,

Jon Awbrey
