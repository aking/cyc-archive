Pat,

In my definitions, I draw a distinction between "terminological
ontologies" and "axiomatized ontologies".  This distinction is
based on discussions we had in the ANSI efforts in 1996-97.

I also maintain that there is a continuum between the two
extremes, and that it is possible to derive an axiomatized
ontology by starting with a terminological ontology and
making incremental changes.

>John, I think it is misleading to call WordNet an 'ontology'. I am 
>pretty sure that George Miller would agree, by the way. At any rate, 
>if WordNet is an ontology, then I am working on something else. So 
>far, WordNet has not been, as far as I know, of any appreciable use 
>or relevance to any effort that is usually called ontological. (I am 
>aware of the 'alignment' work. Now, can anyone say what utility it 
>was to Cyc?)

Eduard Hovy was responsible for "aligning" the WordNet terms
with the corresponding Cyc concepts.  During that process, it
was necessary to make some corrections to both hierarchies.
But as far as I know, both G. Miller & Co. as well as Fritz L.
believe that the exercise was useful.

If you correspond with either of them (or their colleagues)
about that point, please share the info with the rest of us.

John Sowa
