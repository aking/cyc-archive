I just wanted to clarify (and apologize for) my use of modal
operators in Ch. 2 of the KR book:

>But in the context of SUO-KIF, you *are* insisting on the use of a 
>modal logic. That is largely why I am pushing on this issue so 
>strongly. Already, the only organized attempt to do ontology merging 
>on the SUO lists has been forced into using a modal pseudo-KIF in 
>order to be able to state John Sowa's modal axioms.

In an earlier note, I admitted that I only used modal operators
because I was lazy.  In Ch. 5 of the KR book, I recommended
Dunn's semantics for modality, which replaces modal operators
with turnstiles that equate necessity with provability from
some agreed upon laws (or axioms) and possibility with
consistency.  (Note to Chris M:  if you prefer, I am willing
to accept a double turnstile for semantic entailment.)

As I have stated repeatedly, the laws that determine modality
(and causality, in the case of physical laws) are more
fundamental than any version of modality.

John
