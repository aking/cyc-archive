Fred,

Do you even read the majority of stuff I send?
I realize that there is a point of view that
you can do ontology without logic, but that
to me is right up there with thinking that
you can do physics without mathematics.
Sorry, kant be done.

My main focus right now is the nuts'n'bolts business
of delivering to this agora a basis for doing logic in
a suitably computable and still humanely grokable way,
because -- and I do not know how to emphasize this fact
strongly enough without being suspected of hyperbole --
the basis of representation that is currently taken for
granted by most folks here is quite literally as big an
obstacle to clear-headed, effective, far-reaching logical
method as roman numerals are to diff-&-integral calculus.
It just so happens that the work of Peirce is instrumental
to making these developments conceivable, if not yet real.

It is one of the unfortunate effects, quite well-known
in the literatures of anthropology and sociology, that
members of marginalized communities, as Peirceans are
in philosophy, must constantly refer, in very explicit
and self-conscious manners, to the distinctive sources
of their beliefs, their culture, and their way of life,
whereas the members of dominating communities can just
take for granted the support of a background stage-set,
which is the only reason that you sense no "evangelism"
for Russell or the early Wittgenstein here, though you
automatically, constantly, habitually, unconsciously,
and unreflectively run in their ruts.  I realize also
that it can be distressing, when anybody first applies
a force that is designed to turn you out of those ruts
to which you may have become so accustomed as not yet
to imagine that any stream of thinking could have yet
another valid orbit, but if you bear with me, and if
we lean to, and put our shoulders to the task, I think
that you may, in good time, come to see the sense of it.

I do not want to be pulled into the errant knightmares
that will arise from everybody remarking on the level,
the personally perceived relevance, or the volume of
everybody else's contributions, since it is only the
substance, the soundness, and the real applicability
of what people write that should be our focus here --
I can only say that I would not send information,
other than the usual amenities, that I do not
personally believe is pertinent to the task,
so please, do not tempt me on this score.

BTW (Back To Work),

Jon Awbrey
