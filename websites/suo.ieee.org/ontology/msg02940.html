> > The term "reification" is a venerable old term from philosophy,

> > formidable names of "hypostasis" or "hypostatic abstraction",
> > at least two of which can be found in an ordinary dictionary.
> 
> We were aware of that fact when we took the very conscious decision
> at our Paris meeting to grasp the bull by the horns and use the term.

To be aware of a fact in making a choice
is not, at least, not for fallible and
mortal creatures, the likes of which
I am guessing that both of us are,
to be aware of every consequence
of that fact, nor of that choice.
That's why I set to work, twenty
very odd years ago, building up
my personal computing ideal
of a conjecture checker
cum theorem prover.

> Words have a way of changing their meanings over time.
> (Look at "ontology", a venerable old term from philosophy
> if ever there was one!)  Better to accept that fact and make
> use of it (though obviously not unthinkingly or indiscriminately).

Oh, don't get me wrong.  I am utterly in favor of making up new languages,
and try to invent five or six before breakfast every day.  Y'know what? --
it tends to annoy the heck outa people!  But ToPicMap is Quasi Modo Infantes,
and will be for a long long time -- so I am afraid that it has no choice but
to learn the language of its elders already on the scene, or it won't survive
long enuff to evolve any wits at all.

> Reification is here to stay, I think.

My point exactly -- it was already here.

> And I do like it better
> than "thingification"
> or "topification",
> don't you?

Well, if you're into neologism,
I've a got gads-hillion of 'em.
