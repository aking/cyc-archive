Jon,

I agree with John S. and John V. -- all the poetry and word play, gets in
the way. With the volume of email I need to read each day, I can at most
quickly glance at a message to determine if I'm interested in reading it in
detail. If the content is not clear and interesting, I have to ignore the
message -- a message with lots of poetry and word play is more likely not to
get read in detail.

For everyone's benefit, there is a good summary of principles for clear
technical writing at:

http://www.csr.city.ac.uk/people/norman.fenton/papers/good_writing/general_.
principles.html
