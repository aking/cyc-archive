If I offended anybody with my answer, I apologize.  I did not intend my 
response to be caustic or sarcastic. I do business internationally on a 
regular basis and shifting time zones is a semi-natural thing for me to do.  
In my dealings, I use Universal Time as a reference but always convert to 
local time so as to reference the practical happenings of the local 
references (are they in the office, Out to lunch, etc.).

I originally thought of referencing "noon, my time" but chose Mountain 
Standard Time as a more universally recognized reference.  You are correct 
that Universal Time should be the reference but I know that that is not as 
easily recognized by US participants as is our common references (EDT,  CDT, 
MDT, and PDT).  My reference also comes from the fact that Arizona does not 
observe "Daylight Savings" or "Summer" time.  (At 45 degrees C, why would we 
want MORE sunlight.)
