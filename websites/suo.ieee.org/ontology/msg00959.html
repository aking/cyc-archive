Pat wrote:
> [John wrote:]
> >The difference between us is that I maintain that it is much
> >more convenient to treat models as abstract constructions
> >that have a mapping to whatever aspects of the world that we
> >may be interested in studying, talking about, planning for, etc.
> >Pat, however, maintains that "a Tarski-style model" is or
> >should...be constructed out of actual entities in the world, not
> >just pointers to or surrogates of entities.

> No; COULD be.  I hope that isnt a provocation to war. My claim is 
> that the theory is entirely agnostic as to the nature of the things

Ironically (for John), in his major papers, Tarski himself almost
always restricts himself to models containing real world individuals.

"Tarskian" model theory as we know it is arguably a later invention.)
