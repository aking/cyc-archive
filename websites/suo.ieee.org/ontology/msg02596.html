Philip Jackson wrote:
> 
> Fellow SUO'ers,
> 
> I propose to rename the SUO Dictionary, as
> "SUO Common Ontology Develoment Architecture (Coda)".
> Otherwise, people may think it is just a glossary, to
> be developed and used as an appendix or afterthought --
> rather, I hope that SUO members will find it a useful
> tool in the development of SUO itself.  It can support
> collaboration in development of one or more ontology
> efforts, i.e. "merged ontology", "4D", "IFF", etc.
> 
> Robert Kent has begun using this framework and
> you can see the contributions he has provided at:
> 
> https://www.quickbase.com/db/6urbwpxk?act=q&qid=1
> 
> Coda provides a collaborative framework that people can use
> to document and define ontologies, effectively providing a set
> of common documents for SUO to focus on.  Coda addresses the
> following requirements:
> 
> - open to input from multiple contributors
> - a contributors input is easily published on the web,
>   directly by the contributor
> - readers can view any contributor's work
> - contributors can only edit their own contributions
> - teams of people can collaborate to develop consensus definitions,
>   or agree to disagree
> - multiple ontology efforts / directions can be pursued in parallel
> - support for namespaces within each ontology effort
> - openness to different notations, and different levels of formalization,
>   to facilitate flexibility in exploring new ideas, as well as rigorous
>   formalization of ideas.
> 
> For more information about SUO Coda, the main page is at:
> 
> https://www.quickbase.com/db/6urbwpxk
> 
> and the adjunct Ontology Modules database is at:
> 
> https://www.quickbase.com/db/6ztfq6sg
> 
> Cheers,
> 
> Phil Jackson

Phil,

I cannot resist a good acronym and a pithy Peirce quote
all on one and the same page, but now that I have flung
open this window I am forced to ponder over what to say.

I need to try again to explain some things about my present POV,
as it is beginning to get through to me that many folks do not
know what the heck I am saying here.  I am not a philosopher,
I do not play one on TV, I did not begin my logic education
with Homer and Lao Tzu, and only recently catch my reading
up as far the 19th Century.  Nor have I always been this
contortured poet-manque* that you read before you today.
I was once as happy as a lark -- no ravens in sight! --
in my nutshell by the seas of Ars Combinatoria, with
the likes of graph, group, logos, and number theory
to while away my days with the building of this or
that well-posed sandcastle on those halcyon shores --
if it were not that I had "Bad Conjectures" (BC's).
A BC is one that you do not really care whether
it is false or true, but you really just feel
like you have to know, one way or the other.

After wasting my youth and a good chunk of my mental vitality
trying to work 'em out with the muscles of my own bitty brain,
I finally overcame the post-traumatic stress dissorter that
I had acquired on account of Fortran, Cobol, and the two-day
turnaround, in those early days of computing, well, for me,
and I turned once again to the computer as a potential aide.
There were no reasonably adequate automated reasoners around,
not for what I had in mind, so the first thing I had to do was
to set to work on building one.  Long story foreshortened here,
but within five years I had already abandonned the mainrut of
the "resolution unification technic" (RUT) and set out after
the Cibola of "total information reasoners" (TIR's), with all
of the blood, sweat, and toil that are their fellow travails.

In good subgoaling fashion, I have been forced to dial back
my initial ambitions time and again until I reached a stone
that I could use as a spade.  I've been alluding constantly
to it ever since I washed up on this shore, but I do not
get the sense that I am getting across the distinctive
brand of potential that is present in this particular
style of implementation, though, heaven knows, nobody
knows better than I know how far it is presently from
being scaled up to its ultimate potential.  So I will
try another time or three before I hit the dusty road
to gleaming El Dorado, or else to Seige Persilous and
the Home of the Grail.  Here is a smattering of links
on my notion of what it will take to devise a really
merciless logic checker, for the sake of upbuilding
the edifice of our discursive character, of course:
