Patrick Cassidy wrote (in part):
----------------------
    Well, I thought that there was general agreement that,
as in CYC, "collections" can be physical objects.
---------------------

In Cyc, Collections can have physical objects as members (if that's what Patrick meant), but the collection itself is an Intangible.  In Cyc, if you want a collection that is itself a physical thing located in time & space, you have to make it a Group, not a Collection.

By the way, in Cyc, they don't assume that time and space have to go together.  There is one class for TemporalThing (located in time) and another, partially overlapping class for SpatialThing (located in space).  The presumption is that there are some things with temporal attributes but no spatial attributes, and some things with spatial attributes but no temporal attributes.  I haven't seen this possibility represented in the ontologies proposed here so far.
