Frank Farance wrote:

> I'd say that the problem, from my perspective, is that the wording of
Jon's postings (including the original posting you reference above) are
unnecessarily complex...

I think that we all would agree.  And the reason isn't that Jon is
too concise, but that his words do not explain the relevance of
his major contribution.  Note how I had to write two full paragraphs
to explain the relevance of "Just Ducky", while Jon gave much more
detail to other background information that did not explain how the
contribution related to the SUO.

> Recommendation: Higher priority should be given to being clear and
concise ... it helps for native and non-native English speakers.

I agree.

> In my experience in E-mail and netnews distribution over the past 20+
years, the technology doesn't matter as much as the willingness of the
communities to be "self-policing".  Even better would be self-discipline
... self-policing wouldn't be necessary.  Occassional face-to-face
meetings help, too: they build they bonds for self-discipline,
self-policing, and common goals ... 

Yes.

> Recommendation: Determine the policy before choosing a distribution
technology...

I agree in part, but I still maintain that a better distribution
technology would go far toward solving a problem that would exist even
if we all did our best to follow a strict discipline.  We just have too
much material for an e-mail list.
