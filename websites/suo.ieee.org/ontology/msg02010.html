yy,

I presume that you are referring to the structural level. What I am
describing there might be called the super-deluxe enterprise version. I
believe this is what we need in the SUO.

There is one stripped down version that eliminates the category theory
terminology in namespaces such as 'CAT' for categories, 'FTR' for functors,
etc. This is about half of the structural level terminology that was
mentioned. However, although one still has classifications, IF theories,
languages, models, etc. and the expressivity of a hierarchy of logics, one
cannot reason about them at the structural level in any depth.

There may be another, further stripped down version, where sorts are used
instead of the types in Barwise's notion of a classification, with the same
idea extended to relations.
