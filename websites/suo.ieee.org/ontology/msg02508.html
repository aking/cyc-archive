At 22:09 2001-05-15 +0100, Chris Angus wrote:
> 
> As this is an international group can I suggest that you either choose UTC
> as your timebase or give us some idea of how Mountain Standard Time relates
> to the rest of the world.
> ...
> > The ballot will close at noon Mountain Standard Time on Friday May 18,
> > 2001.

Paul said "noon Mountain Standard Time".  MST is UTC-0700, so that would make it:

        2001-05-18 19:00 UTC
        2001-05-18 20:00 BST (British Summer Time)
        2001-05-18 15:00 EDT (Eastern Daylight Time) 
        2001-05-18 13:00 MDT (Mountain Daylight Time)
        2001-05-18 12:00 MST (Mountain Standard Time) -- Arizona, US (no Navajo)
        2001-05-18 12:00 PDT (Pacific Daylight Time)

Note that Paul is from Arizona, which doesn't observe daylight saving time (summer time), so he probably really means Mountain Standard Time, not Mountain Summer Time.  Right Paul?
