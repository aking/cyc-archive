"John F. Sowa" a écrit :
> 
> Jon, Jim, and Matthew,
> 
> It is a fact of life that people in different communities
> tend to use the same words in different ways.

What a surprise!!!

BUT, everybody at SUO and als... (all "common" ontologies) still 
expect to force the whole world into the same straightjacket.

One size fits all!

Even within the same field of interest people tend to have each their
own peculiar definition of the meanings of even trivial words.
Yet they DO succeed (mostly) in communicating.

As you said, John:

> The hard part is to get some very thick skulls to recognize that they 
> would have a disaster on their hands if they continue as they have.

And I go beyond your argument for multiple modular ontologies 
organised in a lattice.

Even within the same subfield, ontologies from various participants
SHOULD be allowed to diverge and still be interoperable.
This just because, above a handfull of interacting parties, NO CONSENSUS
will ever be reached, and yet, they NEED to talk and exchange knowledge.

Thanks to you John for trying to stop the runaway train, but unfortunately
only the *wall* will stop it.
