> > MW: I consider time dependent attributes to be indicative 
> of states of
> > something.
> > 
>    This is a reasonable way to handle such attributes.  I just use
> a different terminology -- I like to consider a "state" as the set
> of all of the time-variable attributes of an object at a particular 
> time.  The term "state" has been used in different senses, such as in
> Petri Nets and in computer science to describe an attribute-value
> that persists for some period of time.  To avoid debates over
> terminology, it may be a good idea to eschew the term "state"
> itself and use more specific terms like "instantaneous_state"
> or "persistent_state"  (once we decide how to define them).

MW: Bad luck, I meant a particular spatio-temporal extent when I said state,
which I would then have as a member of a number of classes, rather than
aggregating all the possible combinations into particular classes of which
it was one of (though I don't have any objections to this).

What you refer to as state, I would refer to as class of state.

MW: I am not sure where I would look (it depends on what you mean by

catalogue. Again, ISO15926CD deals with required, planned, and actual things
(though we are revising this as a result of ballot comments).
