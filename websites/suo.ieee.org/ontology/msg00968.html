> Dear Matthew,
> 
> You wrote:
> 
> > In EPISTLE we modestly claim that the EPISTLE Core Model can
> > represent "life the universe and everything" (well almost).
> 
> OK, how about the phrase "life, the universe and everything" 
> as another
> "challenge concept"? How would you represent this in EPISTLE? 

MW: Well I guess the simple answer is "thing" our supreme supertype. That of
course is the set of all things, which includes classes and relations and so
on. If you want "life the universe and everything" from the dawn of time to
the end of the universe, then we are talking about that spatio-temporal
extent that is the whole of space for all time, and that is also easy
enough.

> It seems this
> could be interesting... How well can any existing ontology framework
> represent the fullness of this "grand challenge concept", the range of
> things that people think of (or may think of) when they hear the LTUAE
> phrase? (Considering they may think for quite a long time about it..)
> 
> One answer may be that LTUAE refers to the full scope and content of a
> complete ontology itself. In which case the question because, 
> how well can
> the ontology represent itself, and all other possible ontologies?

MW: Well the EPISTLE Core Model does have an ability to model
representations of things, and it can model classes quite generically. It
further allows classification of anything, and supports specialisation
between classes too. So you can go quite a long way. The basic 4D ontology
is explicit.
> 
> > For some 8 years now I have regularly issued a challenge 
> for people to try
> > to identify things it can't represent (provided you accept the 4D
> paradigm).
> 
> Can you give examples of things that EPISTLE can't represent 
> because they
> are outside the 4D paradigm?

MW: The biggest issue would be representing other paradigms. But then the
model is not really trying to model that. We would expect other paradigms to
be mapped in and out.

MW: Well as I said above, there is nothing to stop you representing other
ontologies in the EPISTLE Core Model. We only use binary relations at
present, and there is no explicit support for concepts like variables, that
can give some difficulties with specifying complex constraints. We do not
major on constraints, another entity relationship limitation.

MW: This is partly about what you are trying to model. The main disadvantage
of an entity relationship language, such as is used to model the ECM is a
disadvantage.

MW: I think I have.
