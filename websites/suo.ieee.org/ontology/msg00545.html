First, let me provide some general comments and references.
 
Mathematically and semantically, the *formal contexts* of Formal Concept Analysis (FCA) and the *classifications* of Information Flow (IF) are identical. Formal Concept Analysis is surveyed in the rather mathematical text "Formal Concept Analysis: Mathematical Foundations" by Bernhard Ganter and Rudolf Wille [http://www.springer.de/cgi-bin/search_book.pl?isbn=3-540-62771-5#english]. Amongst other things this book discusses the so-called "fundamental theorem of FCA" where a formal context induces a *concept lattice*. A very readable discussion of the fundamental theorem of FCA is given in chapter 11 of the text "Introduction to Lattices and Order" by B. A. Davey and H. A. Priestly. Information Flow is discussed in both a very intuitive and reasonably mathematical fashion in the text "Information Flow: The Logic of Distributed Systems" by Jon Barwise and Jerry Seligman [http://www.cup.org/ObjectBuilder/ObjectBuilder.iwx?processName=productPage&product_id=0521583861&origin=redirect]. Information Flow adds a way to relate classifications through morphisms called *infomorphisms* -- in short, it is a category-theoretic presentation of classifications (it also defines and uses colimits). 
__________
 
Second, let me give the definition of a classification (formal context).
 
In FCA a *formal context* is a triple K = (G, M, I) consisting of a set of *formal objects* "G" (Gegenstande), a set of *formal attributes* "M" (Merkmale) and a binary relation "I" between objects and attributes called an *incidence relation*. Information Flow (IF) uses the same notion, but the following alternate terminology: a *classification* is a triple A = (inst(A), typ(A), |=) consisting of a set of *instances* "inst(A)", a set of *types* "typ(A)", and a binary relation "|=" between instances and types called a *classification relation*.
 
A formal context (classification) is visualized as (and regarded to be) a 2-dimensional matrix, where the first dimension is index by the objects G and the second dimension is indexed by the attributes M. In either case (FCA or IF), what is an object (instance) and what is an attribute (type) is somewhat arbitrary, with a (theoretical) involution always possible that flips the sense (transposes the 2-dimensional matrix), with objects changing to attributes and vice-versa.
__________
 
Third, let me provide comments and references for the application of classifications to SUO (in reasonably chronological order).
 
The message [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg00142.html] refers to *extensionality*, a property of classifications that is axiomatized on page 3 in the PDF document [http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/ontology/pdf00004.pdf] that is attached to the message [http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/ontology/msg00469.html].
 
My ISKO 6 paper [http://www.ontologos.org/Papers/ISKO6/ISKO6.pdf] that is referenced in the message [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg00405.html] has a compact but detailed discussion of classifications and infomorphisms with applications to *ontology sharing*.
 
The notion of a *conceptual knowledge system*, which is briefly discussed in the message [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg01953.html], is a kind of formal context (classification) that includes aspects of a concept lattice within its incidence relation.
 
The definition of a formal context (classification) is given in the correction message [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg01961.html].
 
A very particular, but very important, classification (formal context) (and its associated concept lattice) called the *truth classification of a first-order language L* -- whose instances are L-structures, whose types are L-sentences, and whose classification relation is satisfaction -- is recommended in the message [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg01758.html].
 
The messages [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02093.html], [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02195.html] and [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02201.html] discuss *abstract objects* in the light of classifications (formal contexts).
 
The messages [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02424.html], [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02422.html], [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02351.html] [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02477.html], [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/bin00006.bin], [http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/suo-kif/msg00022.html], [http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/ontology/msg00001.html]discuss a particular formal context (classification) called the *Heraclitus distinction*.
 
A review of results of FCA and IF appropriate for the *lattice-based approach to SUO* was given in the message [http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/ontology/msg00362.html].
