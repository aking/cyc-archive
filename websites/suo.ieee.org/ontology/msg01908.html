All,

	I now count 5 additional objections to past actions (total 7), which
is more than enough to declare that the 'Proposed Conclusions to the Straw
Poll' do not stand.  I am considering these objections to still be timely
since this matter has obviously remained open this entire time.

	Ian and Adam are free to propose again if they wish.  We can now
conduct an official vote.  Under Robert's Rules (still unclear the degree
these apply to email votes), a motion needs just one 'second' to be put to a
vote.  I suggest a greater number should be required and will propose such a
clause tonight. 

	Please understand that with only 2 objections to adopting the
'Proposed Conclusions,'  I felt it was the fairest decision to adopt them.
With 7 current objections, it now is clear we should not.  

 I believe we will eventually learn that an email group cannot function if a
small number of objections can force votes.  I doubt we would have ever
completed the Scope and Purpose if this was the rule.   As I said, I will
propose a clause this evening addressing this issue.

Jim Schoening
