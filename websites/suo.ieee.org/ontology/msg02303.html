Hello, 
	I am new on the list.  I was interested in your conversation with Pat. I am
sorry because I haven't got the initial discussion. Perhaps I was not yet on
the list. 

1 -  I will discuss your comments in relation to my data (analysis of
thinking aloud verbal reports in problem solving). There is in fact a real
interaction between the subject and the structuration of external objects.
First there are complex agregates which are not integrated in a whole, then
when the whole emerges, every element has to be progressively reconstructed
at this new level of granularity, and then locally and progressively linked
together in the whole. 
Then Pat is right with his criticism, but you are right also. Moreover it
appears to me as necessary that you have to consider some other cases.


2 - Furthermore, when only the whole has emerged, but not yet the elements
(that were only agregated elements at the precedent level of granularity),
the elements have no specific property, and the whole appears as without
elements. Can you say in this case that the whole is a continuous object ? 

(perhaps ? I can't answer myself to this question. 
But, if a function is defined on a discrete set, it can't be continuous ! It
is surely the raison for that Pat thinks of mereology)

3 - I think that I understand you, but it is not clear for me what you call
the 'superficial part of a continuous object'. Is it about the level where
there is the whole when the elements which belonged to the previous level of
granularity were not yet reconstructed ?
Do you consider externel elements agregated to the whole but not integrated
in it ?
From a topological point of view a superficial part is the boundary between
interior and exterior.  
I think that  you owe work a litle more to make understand your terminology.

4 - about action I do not agree with what you are saying. Actions are much
more complicated entities, with parts and whole, in order to construct
macro-actions and automatizations. Furthermore, when an action was
associated to an object, the object and the action are not generally
structured at the same time. It complicates enormously things. 
Besides  several sorts of intentionality are to be distinguished, but this
is another matter. I am presently working on the problem of generation of
goals from the organisation and functioning of agregates. But for that I try
first to modelize my data to get them clear.
