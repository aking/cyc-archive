Chris,

There are two widely used conventions:

 1. Unique names:  Distinct names represent distinct individuals.

 2. Possible aliases:  Distinct names may represent the same individual.

In theorem provers, the distinct name convention allows a significant
improvement in the performance of unification.  KIF must be able to
support both unique names and possibly nonunique names.

CM> Except that using a constant doesn't do a thing to guarantee
uniqueness
> (at least, not without adding an ungainly bunch of "unique names" axioms
> ...)

One metalevel axiom saves an infinite number of object-level axioms.

The use of loaded words like "ungainly" or "confused" is a typical
philosophers' ploy of putting down a colleague without giving a
serious argument.  It is very similar to the mathematicians' ploy
of using words like "obvious" or "clear" when a proof is difficult
to formulate, discover, or remember.
