MW: The thing to bear in mind is that a temporal relationship represents one
of the following:

 - a state of one object that is a member of a class (classification)
 - the states of two objects that are related timelessly (composition,
connection)
 - the state of one object that is also a state of another object
(installation, assignment to position)

The problems are 2 fold.

 - the different patterns that temporal associations hide can lead to a
misunderstanding of what is really going on (they all seem to be the same
when they are not)
 - when the states that are hidden in the associations become of interest in
their own right, independent of the association they are part of, then there
is data duplication, which may not be recognised.

The first caused us in practice quite a lot of trouble in misunderstanding
the nature of many things. The second was more of a nuisance, but was an
indication that something was wrong - conceptual models really shouldn't
have redundancy.
