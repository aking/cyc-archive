	> > 1.  Simplify the tip of the ontology so that it has the

> My concern about simplifying this is that you have already 
> gone through one phase of simplification, and this is the 
> second one. In neither phase did you have anything other 
> than the terms themselves in the ontology to define them, 
> and so I couldn't figure out what  was 'lost' in the previous 
> simplification. I would much prefer a definition phase as the 
> next one for the working paper rather than another deletion 
> phase.

Well, I don't think it's true that the terms have only been defined by
themselves.  The tip of the ontology consists of subsumption relations,
which show how meaning is inherited.  Furthermore, each term in the tip is
associated with a docu-string, which attempts to explain in informal English
the corresponding term.  I agree that it is very useful to cash out the
meaning of concepts with formal axioms.  Some concepts already have
associated axioms, given in the "General Axioms" section of the "Merged
Ontology", and I will incorporate additional axioms as the document matures
(axioms developed both by me and by others). 
> 
	> > 
	> > (subclass-of Individual Entity)

	> > (subclass-of Physical Entity)
	> > (subclass-of Abstract Entity)
	> > (subclass-of Continuant Entity)
	> > (subclass-of Occurrent Entity)
	> > 
> You deleted Mediating. Why? I only saw one axiom for 
> Mediating Entity:
> (=> (and (instance-of ?m Mediating) (has ?m ?x) (has ?m ?y))
>     (nec (or (has ?x ?y) (has ?y ?x))) )
> 
> did you delete Mediating because this includes nec ?
> Or because Mediating is an example of Thirdness?
> Or because no one has helped define it better in FOL terms?
> Or some other reason?

You're right that I did delete 'Mediating'.  I did this, because I and many
others found it very difficult to distinguish this notion from that of
'Relative'.  It may be that the Secondness/Thirdness distinction is crucial
from a philosophical perspective, but I don't see that it would be essential
to facilitating system interoperability or fulfilling its role in the other
applications envisioned for the SUO. 

> 
> In fact in reviewing, I see you deleted Independent and Relative both.
> So you want to delete this entire triad, not just one part of it...
> Is that because Triads are controversial, or just hard to explain?

Well, the distinction between Secondness and Thirdness is extremely
difficult to explain and, thus, controversial.  I didn't actually delete
'Independent' and 'Relative'; I just assigned each a nominal term -
'Individual' and 'Relation', respectively.
