>"Frederick N. Chase" wrote:
> >
> > It is necessary that the IEEE SUO include
> > a means for articulation of domains of interest for subtending ontologies.
> >
> > The current Merged Ontology is fundamentally unsuited for this.
>
>
>
>Email Ian to Fred Mon, 29 Jan 2001 on the same subject:
>
>"If by "means of articulation of domains of interest for subtending
>ontologies", you mean that the upper-level ontology ought to provide hooks
>upon which middle-level and lower-level ontologies can be positioned, I
>agree with you, and I think the merged ontology, as it stands, provides such
>hooks.  It is, in part, a taxonomy of very general concepts, and I am not
>aware of any concept which could not be related to the taxonomy.  If you
>know of a concept which cannot be subsumed by anything presently in the
>ontology, please let me know.  This would be very useful information."

How about:
an 'information-bearing object' (see my long comment in a recent message);
a piece of a certain kind of cloth which was used in evidence in a trial;
the color of that piece of cloth;
the act of breaking an egg by hitting it on the side of a bowl;
a style of dress used by a certain social class in 17th-century France;
the shape of Marilyn Monroe's left ankle;
the cause of the Concorde disaster;
a dish (in the sense that Eggs Benedict is a dish, ie a something 
that could be a menu item; as opposed to any particular example of 
that dish, or the symbols on the menu describing it.)

That might do for a start. If you need more, let me know.
