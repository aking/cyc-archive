Dear Matthew,

You wrote:

> In EPISTLE we modestly claim that the EPISTLE Core Model can
> represent "life the universe and everything" (well almost).

OK, how about the phrase "life, the universe and everything" as another
"challenge concept"? How would you represent this in EPISTLE? It seems this
could be interesting... How well can any existing ontology framework
represent the fullness of this "grand challenge concept", the range of
things that people think of (or may think of) when they hear the LTUAE
phrase? (Considering they may think for quite a long time about it..)

One answer may be that LTUAE refers to the full scope and content of a
complete ontology itself. In which case the question because, how well can
the ontology represent itself, and all other possible ontologies?

> For some 8 years now I have regularly issued a challenge for people to try
> to identify things it can't represent (provided you accept the 4D
paradigm).

Can you give examples of things that EPISTLE can't represent because they
are outside the 4D paradigm?

> We know were some limits are, but it is quite unusual for anyone to find
> them.

Can you give us a summary of the limits you currently know of? (That will
save us time finding them :-)

> The worst that has ever happened is I have learnt something.

That can also be the best that happens :-)

> Well that ought to do it ??

I think so... Presumably, Pat Hayes now has this on his list of things to
think about :-)

This is fun -- I haven't typed so many smileys in a while :-)

Cheers,

Phil
