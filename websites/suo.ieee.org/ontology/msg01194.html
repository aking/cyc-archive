Fred,

I completely agree with your opening paragraph:

>Dear Learned Gentlemen and Ladies,
>
>After having spent much of this afternoon trying to install
>DocBook, plus ahem all the other seemingly innumerable bits'n pieces
>that are required ... style sheets ... jade ... documentation ...
>fighting the fact that the OASIS site seems to have vanished into
>thin air, I humbly submit that the words "DocBook" and "beginners"
>form an oxymoron. In fact DocBook is a typical Unix beast, pain 
>in the arse to install, complete with typical Microsoft-like advice
>to consult with the systems administrator if the tests fail. No
>ordinary business in its right mind would have anything to do with 
>this kind of grab-bag of software, standards, and config files if it had
>an option. And to say that this software is 'free' is really to indulge
>in a little poetic-license, if installation and learning costs 
>are considered. 

Unfortunately, your experience is painfully true.  It is
essential to have a smooth installation procedure for any
software product.  DocBook is not yet a product, but I would
love to see someone make it into a product and package it with
OpenOffice.  That could be done.  (But I don't plan to do it.)

I would add a great deal of qualification to the following:

>No matter how awful Microsoft software is ... at least you can install 
>it in minutes ... and be typing away pretty dern fast!

To make that true, replace "Microsoft" with "Macintosh".
As somebody who once upgraded an old 486 to an AMD 586 with
extra memory and a CD-ROM attached through a parallel cable,
I don't expect anything from Microsoft to work "out of the box".

But companies that manage to stay in business (despite the
overwhelming presence of MSFT) do it by making their software
much more bullet proof.  (Unfortunately, UNIX lovers have
more brains than business sense.)

> The Unix
>community simply never seems to be able to compete on this level! "Vision"?

>bah! Gimme software that works outa the box!

I would also make that same comment about the CG community,
the KIF community, and many others we all know and love.

John Sowa
