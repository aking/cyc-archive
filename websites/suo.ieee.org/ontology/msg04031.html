| No doubt, Pragmaticism makes thought ultimately 'apply' to action exclusively -—
| to 'conceived' action.  But between admitting that and either saying that it
| makes thought, in the sense of the purport of symbols, to consist in acts,
| or saying that the true ultimate purpose of thinking is action, there is
| much the same difference as there is between saying that the artist-painter's
| living art is applied to dabbing paint upon canvas, and saying that that art-life
| consists in dabbing paint, or that its ultimate aim is dabbing paint.  Pragmaticism
| makes thinking to consist in the living inferential metaboly of symbols whose purport
| lies in conditional general resolutions to act.
|
| Charles Sanders Peirce, CP 5.402 n3 (1906).

I will copy this sutra (Sanskrit thread) to the CG and SemioCom forums,
at least fora while, because I believe it is relevant to understanding
the "true life of signs" and also bears on the concerns of those of us
who "got" what graphical logic was about the first time we saw it, who
don't need to be coaxed away from the false idols of inquiry that bear
the fasces of "truth in bonds of totentance syntax" (TIBOTS).

The epigraph with which I started above is one of my all-time favorite themes from Peirce.
Another is his compelling image of existential graphs as the "moving pictures of thought" --
I keep reading his books, but I am still waiting for the movie!

So why are we failing to get the living quality of his logic across?
Maybe we should give up trying to convince "some people" and go back
to our studios and our labs, and for his sake and ours, get back to
the picture!

Jon Awbrey
