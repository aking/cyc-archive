I'm assuming you are making a point like "no man is an island"?

If so I couldn't agree more of course. To say anything even remotely
intelligible I expect to need something like 100 simple statements like
the one above.

What is x?
What is n?
What is "in"?

never mind a, b and what bounding might be, and why did you think it worth
noting in the first place, and who noted it when and where.

So it isn't that I think you can say something coherent with just one binary
relation. I also don't think you can say something coherent with a tertiary
relation, not by a long chalk (remember Graham's attempt to write an
unambiguous sentence?)

So the point about 2 or 3 is really a representation issue as I see it, and
then a matter of whether you think you are talking about edges or nodes.
