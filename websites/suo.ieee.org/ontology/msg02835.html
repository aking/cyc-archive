Josiane,

This request is not mine, but was directed at me by James Schoening,
who was apparently objecting to my brief quotation from Dante, which
happens to be the motto of my own university, and which, of course,
I thought to be apt on a thread that someone a long time ago began
with a question of the form "What the Hell ... ?"  At any rate,
until this time I was personally under the impression that the
SUO group was supposed to be an international forum, but I
will leave that to others to clarify.

Because the Babel Fish has trouble with the Medieval Italian,
I provide a translation:

| Considerate la vostra semenza:
| fatti non foste a viver come bruti,
| ma per seguir virtute e canoscenza.

Roughly:

| Consider the seed from which you sprang:
| You were not made to live like brutes,
| But to go after virtue and knowledge.

Ironically enough, the site was Circle 8.8 of Hades,
the place of infernment for all the False Counsellors,
so some of us refer to this as the "Motto from Hell".

http://www.divinecomedy.org

Jon Awbrey
