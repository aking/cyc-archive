Jim,

In trying to respond to your questions, I constantly found myself
wondering if you mean the same thing by each piece of terminology
as I do whenever I use it, or what would my own closest term be.

So let me propose the following:  Just for the sake of novelty,
let's dispense with all of the jargon that we are able to drop,
pick an actual, concrete, non-mickey-mouse example of a dataset
where the issues of relating data models (defs, schemas, specs),
constants of an ontology (= first order theory), and the matter
and the method of the science in general that is known as logic,
can be brought together in concrete illustration of quite a few
salient points at issue.

I just happen to have such a dataset on hand.

So let us begin by conveying to each other
what each of us calls the various bits and
pieces of this following true-life dataset:
