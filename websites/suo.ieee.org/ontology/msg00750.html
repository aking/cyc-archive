>"Frederick N. Chase" wrote:
> >
> > It is necessary that the IEEE SUO be able to represent
> > alternative courses of reality.
> >
> > The current Merged Ontology is fundamentally unsuited for this.
>
>
>
>Email Ian to Fred Mon, 29 Jan 2001 on the same subject:
>
>"Since the merged ontology incorporates the modal operators of 'possible' and
>'necessary', it can represent alternative courses of action.  Of course,
>there is much work left to be done in this area, but all of this work can, I
>think, be easily fitted into the present framework."

No, come on. Just having the modalities doesnt allow one to 
*describe* alternative courses of action. In fact, 'necessary' doesnt 
even let you refer to alternative courses of ACTION, only alternative 
possible worlds (and even then you can only quantify over them, not 
refer to them, so you can't do any planning.)

This is a genuine issue, though I think that Niles' answer on time 
and 4d (which wasnt very good there) applies here: this issue CAN be 
left until later, since needing to describe alternative courses of 
reality doesnt even arise until we are able to decribe a single 
course of reality, so let's get that right first.  There are 
well-understood ways of making time " branch", eg by relativising 
theories to timelines, or by enclosing alternative 'realities' in 
separate 4-d bubbles, or by using situation/action descriptions. 
Right now we cant even talk about one thing happening, so lets stick 
to one problem at a time.
