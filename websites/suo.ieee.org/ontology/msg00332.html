Dear Matthew,

Wednesday, January 10, 2001, 9:48:18 PM, you wrote:

WMMSGU> I would say that there are 3 basic types of thing:

WMMSGU> 1. things that have an extension (exist) in space time (I would call these
WMMSGU> individuals - but this is a broader use than some would give this word).

WMMSGU> 2. Things that have members (rather than parts), such as
WMMSGU> classes/sets/collections. These are timeless.

WMMSGU> 3. Ordered collections - Tuples, classifications of them are known as
WMMSGU> relations. These are timeless, and indeed one way of defining tuples derives
WMMSGU> them from sets.

May be there is a unified approach to the three things description
if look at them from the two different "time-scale types" and two different
"instantination types" . Such classes will be not timeless but defined
in "long term" time-scale but individuals will be defined in "short
term" time-scale. Such two types of time-scale are used in biosemiotics -
http://www.ento.vt.edu/~sharov/biosem/txt/biosem.html
What about the relations that from the EPISTLE Core model point of
view as any things they have "instantination type" attribute with two
values : "class" and "individal" and if a relation is of "individual"
type it will be described in "short term" time-scale and if a relation
is of "class" type it will be described in "long term" time-scale.

Leonid
