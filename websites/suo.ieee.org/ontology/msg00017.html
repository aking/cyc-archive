Matthew,

Yes, as it happens, if I had my own particular druthers --
I think that is a pluralized syncope for "would rather" --
I would rather use the term "particular" over "individual" as often
as possible, by way of recognizing that the powers of determination
of a "fallible and mortal finite information creature" (FAMFIC) such
as we are -- well, me, anyway, I am pretty sure of my being fallible,
unless all of my former, not former enough, and many, all too many
apparent mistakes were somehow to be revealed one day as unerringly
righteous and true all along, in which case my present imagining
that I am fallible would turn out to be the epitome, the paragon,
and the very type of my original sin against my immortal divinity --
but never mind all that! -- who do I think I am, Descartes!?

Where was I?  Oh yes, 'determination' ...

Now that you and I have concurred on this admirable form of usage,
what odds do you think that we would have of converting many others?

But my powers of determination being finite, indeed, almost infinitesimal,
I have found that I cannot control the exorbitant diversity of ways that
others will go on choosing to use words, and so I have no choice but to
recognize that many will persist in using the term "individual" to mean,
roughly, what I mean by "particular", at least, in the original syntactic
place of it.

All of which brings me back to my actual point,
which is that the use of most terms like these
is bound to be bound to particular interpreters,
and thus "interpretive" rather than "essential"
in their character.

I hope that goes to clarify what I was trying
to say with my admittedly "leisurely" example.

Cheers,

Jon Awbrey
