Dear Ian,

I disagree strongly with what you say below. This is not a matter of
philosophy, it is a matter of project management. Put simply: "failing to
plan is planning to fail." I am also reminded that: "Those who ignore
history are doomed to repeat it".

The implications of what you say below are:

 - it is alright if the SUO is inconsistent and patchy
 - it is alright if we can't answer the question "why does it look like
this"
 - it is alright if we have no philosophical foundation at all

> ontology.  It seems to me that the 2,500 year history of 
> philosophy is a

> philosophers agree.

> useful when it is
> grounded in a concrete fabric within which this discussion 
> has a context and
> an application.  I would argue that the best way of obtaining 
> consensus is
> to produce an initial document (no matter how flawed) and 
> then to revise it
> over many, many iterations of criticism and comment.
