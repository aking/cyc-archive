>
>Right.  My initial position would be that the ontology should take 
>an intentional view of terms, that disjointness should have a model 
>force of "necessary" (Bill Andersen pointed this out to me in a 
>private email) and therefore the axiom should be simple implication.

That decision would be a complete and total disaster. It would 
immediately make the structural ontology unusable, if taken 
seriously. It would be require that KIF be replaced by a modal 
language and render all the axioms first-order invalid. And moreover, 
it would be based on a stupid misunderstanding of the nature of modal 
truth - sorry, but I have tried to explain this politely to you guys 
on a number of occassions.

This is not the same issue as the debate over understanding classes 
intensionally, by the way. The new semantics for KIF admits 
intensional classes without using modality.
