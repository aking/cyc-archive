Dear All,

Rather than pursuing the general thread that I leapt into earlier, I'd like to ask about the focus of this discussion and/or methodology of this group, and perhaps to make some suggestions.

As I noted in my previously note, I'm quite new to the list.  I therefore may have missed significant background for this discussion.  Nevertheless, let me share my reaction so far.

At least some of the issues being tossed around -- e.g., ontological implications of choice of representation, effect of biases ("personal," "political," or cognitive) on both representation and ontology content -- are surely important.  But there is nothing to be gained by discussing them at a level disconnected from real choices.

That is, I take it that the purpose of this list is *not* to attempt to answer these (interesting) philosophical questions.  Rather, I take it that the purpose *is* to discuss issues and considerations directly, immediately relevant to the development of a standard upper ontology, with the aim of guiding and enabling the best possible decisions regarding such development.

If this understanding is correct, I would expect that discussion of large, potentially open-ended issues would be constrained and framed by the decisions that need making, to which those issues are immediately relevant.  For example, say that the task under consideration is to choose a representational system.  It then becomes relevant to consider the possibility that such choices make ontological commitments, and/or lock in significant limitations on use.  The best approach, I would think, is *not* to argue this in the abstract.  Instead, a sampling of candidate representations would be put on the table, and each participants would learn something about them.

Then each would consider the implications of such a scheme from his or her perspective:  Does this system of representation allow for the expression of the kinds of information I normally have to represent, or to work with?  Does it limit use wrt the kinds of applications I'm concerned about?  Have I worked with such representation before, and if so, did I run into any particular difficulties, and if so, can they be resolved?  I.e., to the best of my experience and understanding, what happens when the rubber (an ontology so represented) meets the road (of my domain or application-type)?

Doing this kind of serious evaluation of the candidates, and sharing such specific discussions and examples with the group would, I think, enable the most progress.  A burden, to be sure, but I don't see how it can be avoided if one wants to make progress, rather than going in circles.  This methodology is far more likely to increase objectivity than is the attempt to discuss the issues in abstraction. Insofar as the membership of the group is sufficiently broad (e.g., ontologists from different research programs, potential clients from different domains), problematic partialities should be illuminated by such a process.  The rubber meets, and is tested on, many different roads.

So, is there such a framework for this discussion, not obvious in recent posts?  If not, ought there to be?  Have I understood the purpose of the list correctly?
