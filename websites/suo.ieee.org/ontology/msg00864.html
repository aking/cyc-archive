Bob,
   During the HPKB project, we computed that Cyc had about 400,000 
assertions.    The government release of Cyc (free for use without royalty 
on government contracts) has significantly less.  I'd estimate about 1/3 
although Cycorp has stated it's about 10% (this may be the case because Cyc 
has grown significantly since I had access to the full version during 
HPKB).  The public Cyc upper ontology has 2703 Terms, 13507 Assertions and 
161 Rules.
   You can find some additional figures in the citations below.

Adam

Pease, A., Chaudhri, V., Lehmann, F., and Farquhar, A., (2000), Practical 
Knowledge Representation and the DARPA High Performance Knowledge Bases 
Project, proceedings of KR-2000.

Cohen, Chaudhri, Pease and Schrag (1999), Does Prior Knowledge Facilitate 
the Development of Knowledge Based Systems, proceedings of AAAI-99.

Cohen, Schrag, Jones, Pease, Lin, Starr, Gunning, and Burke (1998), The 
DARPA High Performance Knowledge Bases Project, AI Magazine, Vol. 19 No.4, 
Winter.
