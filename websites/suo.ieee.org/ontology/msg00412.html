Jim, Adam, Pat, Matthew, et al.,

I agree with John Thompson's suggestion:

>It seems that we might be able to achieve consensus if we were to withdraw
the proposal to make the Merged Ontology file our "Base Document" and instead
vote to make it our "Working Paper" on which we will focus our efforts.  Without
some straw horse such as this, I don't see how we're going to make much concrete
progress.

I don't like the idea of watering down the definition of
"base document", and the term "working paper" is an accurate
description of Ian Niles' document on the merged ontology.

I believe that most of us would be happy to take a good hard
look at that merged ontology, analyze it in detail, and help
to make further extensions and refinements to it.

After we have done more work with it and on it, we might all
feel more comfortable in voting for the result as a "base
document".

John Sowa
