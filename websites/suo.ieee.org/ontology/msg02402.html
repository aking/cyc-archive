The terminology "formal concept", "intent", "extent", "formal object",
"formal attribute", "formal context", etc. is terminology from Formal
Concept Analysis. To quote from page 17 of the the book "Formal Concept
Analysis: Mathematical Foundations" by Bernhard Ganter and Rudolf Wille
(ISBN: 3-540-62771-5 Springer-Verlag 1999
[http://www.springer.de/cgi-bin/search_book.pl?isbn=3-540-62771-5]) "the
adjective 'formal' is meant to emphasize that we are dealing with
mathematical notions, which only reflect some aspects of the meaning of
*context* and *concept* in standard language".
