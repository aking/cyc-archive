Bob and all,

 

            Our next higher body is working on a variation of RRO for email groups, but they are finding it’s a larger effort than they anticipated.  We could certainly also try but I fear this could take far longer than expected and sap a lot of energy.  Perhaps we should form a subgroup and set up a separate list for these discussions.

 

            I forget who suggested today that we try to work within Robert’s Rules and see if it works.  This is probably worth a try.  If it works, great.  If it doesn’t, we’ll know for sure we need to adopt something different.  

 

            If we do make changes, I suggest the following:  To conduct a vote, you would need half as many supporters as would be needed for the vote to pass.  If half is too many, maybe only a third. Example:   If we anticipated 20 YES/NO votes(based on prior votes), 11 would be needed to pass, and half would be 6, so 6 voting members would be needed to bring a motion to a vote.  Another example:  If the Chair believes there may be enough support to pass something, he or she may ask if there are any objections to a motion.  Following the same rule, if 5 people objected, it would be forced to a vote (If 20 votes were cast, 10 NO votes would prevent it from passing, so half of that is 5).    This might sound a bit complicated at first reading, but I think it would be rather simple to administer.   The benefit would be fewer votes.  Under Robert’s Rules, 2 people can force a vote on just about anything.

 

Jim

In a message dated 01/31/01Robert Spillers wrote:

Since there have been several statements that the IEEE is silent about voting procedures in this type of group, as a separate matter I will propose a set of voting rules for the group's consideration, comments, revision, etc. If the group can come to a general agreement on a particular procedure, then I will move that they be formally adopted.

We later told that the WG rules of procedure are Robert's Rules. Still later we were told that the rules are essentially what ever the chair thinks is best.   After the pending issues are resolved, I will propose a set of rules that are (I hope) a few simple changes to Robert's Rules.

Since Robert's Rules cover many circumstances that are unlikely to occur, but could occur, I think it wise to maintain the base and change a few things that make them more useful to an email group.  These changes will deal with issues like a quorum (in an email discussion group, I think a quorum is always present - if a few days notice is given for a vote), making and seconding motions, how votes are counted, etc.

I ask that those who are interested think about what changes are appropriate and I would appreciate suggestions after current issues are resolved.

Thanks,

Bob
