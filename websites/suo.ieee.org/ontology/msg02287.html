MW: Yes, accessibility of existing knowledge is a real problem
that often leads one to solve the problem again as being a more
efficient route to a solution than finding where it has already 
been discovered.
> 
> Still, I must repeat the suggestion that I have made before,
> pointedly on the "Manifolds of Sensuous Impressions" thread,
> that the very like of all of these issues have been tackled
> before, if perhaps in a deliberately quantitative disguise.
> 
> Yes, there are Realities and there are Representations.
> Books have been written on the relation between these
> two subjects, if not necessarily in that order.
> 
> Any Reality to which we are likely to point, appreciated
> in all of its glorious splendor, is just bound to be far
> more complex and vastly more subtle than the likes of us
> fallible and mortal finite information beings could ever
> hope to express in full, and so we arch-typically allude
> to it under the aegis of "X", our Persean Shield, if you
> will.  Here is where, I am beginning to suspect, a brand
> of misapprehension first arises between us, for you seem
> to advert to this X, arrogated as a realm in reality, or
> to its typical element x, conceived as an individual, as
> if this move were a simple step, even a simplifying step,
> in our approximal approach to the twined reality, x in X.

MW: I have never felt the compulsion to be required to say
everything about something as a prerequisite to recognising
something's existance. I expect to have to say enough about
something so that others can distinguish the same object,
and what ever it is that I wish to communicate about it,
but not more.

MW: I also pick up from between your comments that in
logical circles "=" means precisely "stands for the same
thing". We do not have such an operatotor in data modelling,
so I have been applying a stricter test of "is the same 
thing". It is useful to know the local conventions.
