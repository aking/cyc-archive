MW: I think this is the wrong process to operate. In ISO TC184/SC4 we
operate a process of Project and Editorial ownership of material by 
those taking the lead for a particular deliverable. The project team
is the final arbiter of the content of the document they propose. This
gives a better chance of internal consistency, and slightly reduces the
chance of a camel. This point is important, since we are dealing with
a body, a consistent whole ontology, not individual axioms that can
be just thrown together.

The deliverable is published in draft form from time to time, and 
participants are invited to vote on the draft. The options are:

Abstain
Yes
Yes with comments
No with comments

Yes with comments implies that the comments are minor or editorial,
and that they could be ignored at the discretion of the project team.

No with comments means that the document is not considered fit for
purpose, and that at least the major issues identified need to be 
appropriately addressed before it could be considered fit for purpose.

In general an issue has been satisfactorily addressed when the raiser 
of the issue agrees it has been.

The issues raised are formally logged, and resolutions prepared and
incorporated into a new draft for a new vote.

At some point you hopefully find that most issues that can be resolved 
have been, and you move to a final vote to give the document some sort 
of approved status.
