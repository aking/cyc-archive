Doug,

Another couple of facets of the topics you raise that draws
my attention is what I see as a shared concern with what we
used to call "open societies", on the one hand, and not too
coincidentally, "whole systems", on the other hand.  In the
aspirations that I am currently entertaining to become some
manner of an "intelligent systems engineer" -- hyphenate it
how you will -- and one that is sensitive to the impacts of
science and technology on society, these are crucial issues,
and areas that deserve a due measure of critical reflection.

Perhaps these sorts of concerns will take us beyond the
mereology of clubs and kiffee-klatschen, but it is also
very likely, as it appears to me, for one, that we will
never be able to understand the architectronic dynamics
of even these deliberately impoverished, duly reductive,
and quasi-single-minded "teams of consciousness" if all
we examine is the anatomy of their organization charter.

What Do You Think?

Jon Awbrey
