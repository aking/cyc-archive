Jon,

I agree with Graham Horn's comment:

>My reaction remains the same it has been for a long time
>now, and is that, unfortunately, you never give concrete examples that allow

>verification of your claimed experiences.
>
>When you give examples, ones that are self­evidently
>unarguable, or that the experts in the group mostly support you on, then we

>will have to sit up and take notice.  

Specific examples are always best.  And when you give
an example, please draw the parallels explicitly.  Don't
assume that everyone will consider the examples to be
self explanatory.

And if there is something that you object to in the merged
ontology, please be specific.  If you object to everything,
then for each point you object to, starting with the most
egregious, you should state explicitly why you object --
preferably by giving a specific example.
