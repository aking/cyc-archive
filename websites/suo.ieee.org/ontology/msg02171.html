Computation And Logic As Semiosis (CALAS) ---
Do not worry over the acronym's etymology ---
It is a Greek transliteration of Egyptian ...

This is a blend of a few loose threads
from the PERS and the Triadicity texts,
that I gathered it would serve to draw
together for a more focussed treatment.

Object >>>--->>> Sign

If one views the trip from objects to signs
as a one-way business, making a beeline from
one's objective ambience, base, concern, ...,
to its comment, description, elucidation, ...,
terminating in the first signs of the situation
that come to mind, after which one can dust off
one's hands of the whole unpleasant matter of the
infernal truth, material earth, or platonic cosmos,
then the lion'share of what I say, hic et nunc, will
no doubt sound utterly redundundant and res publically
exorbitant, to say the least, which you know I never do.

Object <<<===>>> Sign

And even if you fancy that the round-trip between object and sign,
between the constituent res public and its elected representative,
is a relatively straightforward matter of a simple correspondence,
then I'm sure that you will miss, but not really miss, the fun of
what this fuss is all about.

Object >>>--->>> Sign >>>--->>> Sign'

Yet again, if you believe, by dint or by dun of a prior catechism,
or by presumptive grace of what you imagine to be some infallible
intuition, that every consorting of objects with signs is able to
be sorted into two contingents, between objects and signs or else
between signs and signs, wherefore accordingly every relationship
among the lot of them all can be decomposed and factored into two
components and facets, with nary a manner of "triple interaction",
as they would fashion it in a few fields of study, being observed
to influence the mode, then you will fail to see the significance,
too, of why I would be wasting so many words and time enough, too,
on the topic of sign relations, in general, in genuine triadicity.

But I will leave it to those other arenas, dry and dusty as they are,
with all their painful re-circuses yet ongoing, to sort out the form
of the matter at stake, and here, in this side-show of computational
paradigms, try to focus on the aspects of semiosis that affect logic
and inference, especially when the performances of the corresponding
competencies are seen as particular styles and types of computations.

But modern marketing experts have advised me that the circus parade
is no longer effective, or else in fashion, when it come to hawking
one's wares, soft or else, so I will pipe down and tune my calliope,
and address you anon in another dress.

Jon Awbrey
