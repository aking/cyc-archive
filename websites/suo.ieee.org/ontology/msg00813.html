Adam,

You wrote:

>    I take John's comments not to suggest that we should avoid
> semiotics per
> se, but that our inclusion of such concepts in the SUO should be
> motivated
> by practical representational needs rather than an infinite philosophical
> regress that might result from considering the SUO itself as a
> set of signs
> (although it certainly is that).

I agree that we should be concerned with practical representational needs,
and avoid infinite regress. I think it can be relevant to consider SUO
itself as a set of signs, and might not lead to infinite regress, but would
agree this may not be an easy path to explore. These points are not
inconsistent with the "third perspective" I described.

However, Jon can be expected to speak for himself, and indeed already has in
his polemic on the "veil of transparency". At this point I will cease
hazarding to interpret his remarks.

>    In a long ago thread, I suggested that the SUO need to support stating
> things such as
>
> Hamlet the fictional character
> Hamlet an edition of the printed play
> A performance of Hamlet
> A performance of Hamlet captured on video and encoded as a bit stream
> The text of Hamlet as character strings
> What Fritz Lehmann has called a "conceptual work" - the timeless
> informational content of the play
>
> These notions require a well worked out theory of semiotics but
> this would
> have a practical focus rather than being an academic exercise.

Yes, I agree.
