Hello John,

Friday, January 26, 2001, 1:53:18 AM, you wrote:
...
JFS>  2. Therefore, I suggest that as a working hypothesis for the
JFS>     purpose of the SUO list, we adopt an assumption of "naive
JFS>     realism" that says that the world exists, and most of the
JFS>     words we use in ordinary language refer to something, but
JFS>     let's not worry too much about what it is.

Following this direction suppose it will be helpful to define an
"explicit border" between an "AI" and "NI" . There are some things
exist which we "don't want" to formalize explicitly and only define the
"naive realism" taxons . They will be something like a "middleware"
between a formalized and nonformalized parts. A "border" is relative
of course and could be "shifted" during a formalization activity.
Supose such "explicit border" model will make the SUO construction
more flexible.
