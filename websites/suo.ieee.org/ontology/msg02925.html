In the sentence of my claim 'unfold' and 'nicely' are just natural language
words and have no precise definitions.  I believe you are using the term
'reducing' to refer to the same thing I refered to with the word 'unfold'.
The point is that there is no information gained or lost by reducing higher
level arity to BinaryRelations.  I used the term 'unfold' because that is
the way I think about it.  If you unfold (take apart) the more complex
structures you can find just BinaryRelations.

I've already listed the advantages of this approach elsewhere, but the one
that you and I could perhaps discuss would be that the algebra of
BinaryRelations is better known than that of higher arity relations.  There
are lots of axioms that apply to classes of BinaryRelations and to paths
through them.  I have included a list of these classess from SUMO below.
Outside of the knowledge gained from the process of grounding our symbols in
reality, the axioms that apply to these classess are the only logical
knowledge we have about our symbols that I know of.   Perhaps you could
provide a list of classess of higher arity relations that have algebraic
properties ... I can't find any such list.

IrreflexiveRelation,
IrreflexiveRelation,
SymmetricRelation,
AntisymmetricRelation,
TrichotomizingRelation
TransitiveRelation,
IntransitiveRelation.
