I wonder if I have the temerity to suggest a correction to 
Wittgenstein. What the hell, lets give it a try.

> 5.5301. That identity is not a relation between objects is
> obvious....
>
No, on the contrary, it is obvious that it IS a relation. A rather 
special and trivial relation, but a relation nonetheless. It is the 
relation which holds between everything and itself, and between 
nothing else.

One might, at first blush, think that such a peculiar relation would 
have no useful role to play, but that thought would be a mistake 
similar to that made by someone who decides that 0  has no role to 
play in arithmetic.

> 5.5303. Roughly speaking:  To say of _two_ things that they
> are identical is nonsense.  To say of _one_ thing that it is
> identical with itself is to say nothing.
>
But to say of one thing, USING TWO DIFFERENT NAMES, that it (under 
one name) is identical to itself (under the other name), is not to 
say nothing. Indeed it is often a very useful observation. It is true 
that one might then argue that the relation of interest is that 
between the names, rather than between the thing named by them (and 
itself, see above); but that argument, while perhaps correct in some 
metaphysical sense, misses the rather key point here, which is that 
this useful thing that was said, was said by USING the names rather 
than MENTIONING them, so it did not require the use of anything as 
awkward as a metatheory. Thus, the utility of allowing identity to be 
a relation (apart from the mathematical elegance of doing so, since 
it would require a special rule to forbid it) is that it allows us to 
state that two names are coreferential by using, rather than 
mentioning, them.
