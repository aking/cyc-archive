> All,
> There are subtleties and potential ambiguities at every turn.
> 
> It does appear that relation and relationship have two 
> different senses or
> referents (in some cases one, in some cases the other)
> when used by different members of this forum.
> 
> Here is a rough trial baloon for an agreement to distinguish the
> referents for  SUO usage:
> 
> A relation of arity n, n>0,  is symbolized as predicate with 
> n arguments.
> The extension of a relation of arity 1 is the set of things 
> in the domain
> of quantification
> for which the predicate is true.  The  extension of a 
> relation of arity n>1
> is the
> subset of the nfold cartesian product of the domain of 
> quantification for
> which
> the predicate is true.
> 
> A relationship is an instance of a relation.    E.g.,  married(X,Y)
> describes a relation.
> married(Bill, Hillary) describes a relationship.
> 
> I've probably missed some subtleties (or even gotten it 
> backwards), but
> I hope this is unambiguous enough to be ammended.

> The real problem with relationship and relation is that they 
> are frequently

>

> relationship diagrams,

> "relation" for what
