Chris,

I have posted a collection of materials on the intertwined matters
of hypostatic abstraction, reflection, reification, subjecture, and
thematization, and I will post more later as I find it.  This ranges
from tidbits that I have collected over many years, to discussions
that I had last year on the Peirce, CG, SemioCom, and SUO Lists,
to links that I have run across more recently.  In character it
crosses the spectrum from standard definitions, as used in the
human sciences and philosophy, from hermeneutics to logic, to
my own ideas worked out in dialogue, to recent uses in web
and computer science settings.  My sense that the words in
question have begun to be corrupted in their meaning is an
impression that I get from what I hear a whole lot of folks
saying of late, and not from what might be writ in specs.
It is prefectly possible that there remains a common wit
here that is yet to be saved, but the odds are against it
from what I have seen.

The place of "hypostatic abstraction" (HA) in forming the creation
of formal objects and mathematical ideas is an especially important
topos for me to defend.  No doubt it is destined to be my Alamo,
under any name.

Let me know if any of these notes resonate with your own
sense of what is crucial in the concept of reification.

Jon Awbrey
