JA: Aristotle verges on using a trichotomy here,
    dimly highlighting the scheme that involves
    possession (competence knowledge), exercise ('how to' knowledge),
    and critical reflection (descriptive and/or normative knowledge).

WMJ: Could you elaborate on the trichotomy
     in the context of knowledge engineering?

I am going to make a fresh attempt to focus
on the specifics of your question, and then
I will have to gather together another load
of background material to fill in the frame.

The main idea that comes to my mind is this:
In my way of thinking, a state of knowledge,
information, consensus, clarity, certainty,
belief, and like conditions of intellectual
settlement are the end results of a process,
which is convenient to call "inquiry", thus
encompassing the everyday actions of living
souls, from animals to angels and all that
occupy the mediate domains, along with the
rather more formalized methods of science.

This means that the whole focus of my research
and hopeful application to intelligent systems
engineering has shifted toward an examination
of the varieties of inquiry process that lead
to this "knowledge", or whatever else in time
the end products of inquiry come to be called.

One other thing, I will try to avoid speaking
overmuch of "trichotomies", since most of the
universes of discourse that I find hereabouts
are covered with overlapping discs of domains.
Accordingly, it will generally be more apt to
speak of 3-adic relations, 3-fold categories,
degrees of freedom or qualitative dimensions
that come in 3's.

Now, when it comes to inquiry by 3's, there
is another one of those Very Big Ideas that
CSP selected, mutated, and evolved from the
work of Aristotle.  This is the 3-fold duct
of abductive, deductive, inductive thinking,
on which it would well repay our attentions
of in the study to expend its due attention.
And so I will, anon.

Jon Awbrey
