I think that it might serve the discussion to put aside our troublesome,
prior but disparate notions of consistency and contingency for a little
while and see if we can find a "language independent terminology" (LIT)
for describing the cloven aspects of meaning that we might, among us,
be able to recognize.  Now, I realize that this LIT rubric is likely
to sound like a contradiction in terms, so I had better explain what
I mean.  Think of how we acquire the concept of "coordinate-freedom"
in our personal mathematical development.  We start out with courses
where coordinates are everywhere, we gradually pick up the idea that
"many bases look alike" in so far as their adequacy to tag the points
of a space, and then we learn to value invariants, in effect computable
from coordinates, but in essence arising whole and entire from their ashen
graphite characters, as if the true spirits of the whole mathematical corpus.
That is what I mean by LIT.

Jon Awbrey
