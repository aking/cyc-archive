Robert,

01.12.2000 You wrote :

> In the Heraclitus context (the mathematical context from Formal >Concept Analysis) *Heraclitus=(Entity,Class,instence-of) , where >Entity is the disjoint union *Entity=Individual+Class, a class can 
>appear as either a "formal object" (1st argument of instance-of) or >a "formal attribute" (2nd argument of instance-of).

Suppose your views are close to mine about regarding an "instance" 
can be and an individual and a class (if we will accept such understanding of the "instance" and the "member" as a synonym for 
"instance"). The different logical types of things are something "singular" (let it is named as "individual") and the "class" . 
 The EPISTLE Core Model (http://www.stepcom.ncl.ac.uk/epistle/ ) too defines for its "subject" an "instantination type" charachteristic with "individual" and "class" values . Suppose it is possible not to introduce special term "entity" (Entity=Individual+Class) if we use "thing" (subject) , individual and class . There is another problem - to point to something and recognize it as a separate entity . Look at a quote. "I try to use a consistent phenomenological
method. For me there is no such thing as an "object" to which we chose a "name". An "object" is already a name (which is rather generic) that points to everything that we can recognize as a separate entity (e.g., we can detach from envirnoment)" - Alexei Sharov .
