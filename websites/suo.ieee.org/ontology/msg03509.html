JA = Jon Awbrey
MW = Matthew West:

MW: What do you mean by surcatenation?

JA: short answer:  it just means placing a list
    of propositional expressions e_1, ..., e_k
    in the slots of a comma-separated bracket
    of the form "( _ , ..., _ )" to obtain
    a new propositional expression of the
    form "(e_1, ..., e_k)".  the so-called
    "existential interpretation" assigns
    this form of expression the meaning:
    "just one of e_1, ..., e_k is false".

MW: Seems a bit complicated.  What is it for?

it's 1 of 2 operators -- some would call it 1 of 2 'multigrade' operators,
others would insist on calling it 1 of 2 parametric families of operators --
that can be taken as a basis for zol (prop calc), that has among its jobs
the task of serving as a notation for boolean functions on so many vars.

the discussion from this point would normally proceed as follows:
"so it's a basis, so what?  there are many conceivable bases for
any conceivable realm of formal objects worth noting.  what makes
this one worth 1 bit of further notice?"  and there would usually
be a basis for deciding these questions one way or the other.

jon awbrey
