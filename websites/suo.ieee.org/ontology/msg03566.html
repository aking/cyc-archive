Sounds good.
Do you see the focusing knob as additional information you are adding to a
propostion, like a context in which to situate it ?
or is the focusing knob a way of defining the terms in the proposition?

On the statement  "all cats are black",
to add context might be to say this proposition is true because it happens
to be said about the context of the "Black Cat Lover's Cat Show Exhibition"
where no other cats were present in that place and time but black cats.

Alternately, on the statement "all cats are black",
to focus using definitions might be the world of my two-year-old daughter
who thinks "black" is a way of describing fuzzy and soft to the touch
things, and "cats" are any four legged creatures who let her pet them,
including dogs, ferrets, and gerbils, but definitely not the black male
tomcat of my neighbor who hisses at her every time she toddles toward it.

Or were you seeing the focusing knob as something else entirely?
