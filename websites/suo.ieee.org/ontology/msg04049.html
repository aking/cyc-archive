May 3, 2002 2:00 pm   GC Room 8405
Professor Robert Cowen   ( Queens College of CUNY )
Graph Coloring Compactness Theorems Equivalent to the Boolean Prime Ideal
Theorem

Abstract. In 1951, de Bruijn and Erdos proved a compactness theorem for graph
colorings which stated that a graph is k-colorable if every finite subgraph is
k-colorable. J. Mycielski seems to have been the first to ask, in a 1959
lecture, what is the strength of this compactness theorem as a set theory axiom.
He pointed out, among other things, that it follows from BPI, the prime ideal
theorem for boolean algebras. BPI is weaker than AC, the Axiom of Choice, but
has many useful forms that can often be substituted for AC in mathematical
proofs. Finally, in 1971, Lauchli showed that this theorem of de Bruijn and
Erdos is in fact one of the equivalent forms of BPI. More recently the notion of
coloring has been generalized in various ways. We consider a very general notion
of coloring (referred to in the literature as "generalized colorings") and
formulate a compactness result for these colorings and then derive several well
known graph theory compactness theorems from it. The theorem itself and several
consequences are then shown to be equivalent to BPI. It is an interesting fact
that the methods employed in these proofs closely parallel the corresponding
proofs of NP-completenss of the notions involved. This talk, which should be of
interest to graph theorists as well as set theorists, is a joint work with Steve
Hechler and Peter Mihok. It is based on our paper which will appear in the July
2002 issue of Scientiae Mathematicae Japonicae.

May 10, 2002 2:00 pm   GC Room 8405
Professor Juris Steprans   ( York University )
Sacks like forcing and its applications

Abstract. Sacks forcing and its close relatives will be described in
combinatorial and geometric terms. Some applications will be discussed. Among
them, it will be shown how to: 1. deal with the question of covering Euclidean
space with a small number of smooth sets; 2. construct non-isomorphic quotients
of the power set of the integers with relatively simple definitions.
