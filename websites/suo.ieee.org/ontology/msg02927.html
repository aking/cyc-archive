Seth,

New remarks unindented below.

SR: I have found that higher arity relations
    unfold nicely into BinaryRelations [1].

SR: [1] http://robustai.net/mentography/arity.gif

JA: Seth, I do not know what you mean by:
    (1) "unfold", (2) "relation".

SR: BinaryRelations is exactly as described in SUMO:

SR: http://ontology.teknowledge.com:8080/rsigma/SKB.jsp?req=SC&skb=Merge&id=249

It would serve communication best if you could tell me
in your own words what you mean by the word "relation".

SR: In the sentence of my claim 'unfold' and 'nicely' are just natural language words
    and have no precise definitions.  I believe you are using the term 'reducing' to
    refer to the same thing I refered to with the word 'unfold'.

This does not seem likely, considering the further wrappings of words
within which you have swaddled here the words "relation" and "unfold".

SR: The point is that there is no information gained or lost by reducing higher
    level arity to BinaryRelations.  I used the term 'unfold' because that is
    the way I think about it.  If you unfold (take apart) the more complex
    structures you can find just BinaryRelations.

This sort of statement leads me to believe that you are most likely using
at least some of the words "arity", "complex", "reducing", and "relation"
in ways that I do not.

SR: I've already listed the advantages of this approach elsewhere,
    but the one that you and I could perhaps discuss would be that
    the algebra of BinaryRelations is better known than that of
    higher arity relations.  There are lots of axioms that apply
    to classes of BinaryRelations and to paths through them.
    I have included a list of these classess from SUMO below.
    Outside of the knowledge gained from the process of grounding
    our symbols in reality, the axioms that apply to these classess
    are the only logical knowledge we have about our symbols that
    I know of.  Perhaps you could provide a list of classess of
    higher arity relations that have algebraic properties ...
    I can't find any such list.

SR: IrreflexiveRelation,
    IrreflexiveRelation,
    SymmetricRelation,
    AntisymmetricRelation,
    TrichotomizingRelation
    TransitiveRelation,
    IntransitiveRelation.

Until I know what you mean by "binary relation"
I can hardly begin to imagine what you might mean
by "the algebra of binary relations".

Jon Awbrey
