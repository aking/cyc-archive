Mike, Martin, et al.,
I agree with you that most of the interesting relations that we want to talk
about correspond to something real in the world.  The point I was trying to
make, however, is that we can do everything we need for the SUO without ever
talking about the things that those symbols *actually* represent.
>Whatever you call it, there is something in the real world which
>entails two things being related to each other in a particular way.
>Take for example, being siblings.  I'm sorry, but I can see no
>way round siblinghood being in the real world.
>
>MK> I absolutely agree
>
> You can choose to call it a
>relation, or a relationship, or a frobnit, but it is a real
>world notion, which from time to time one may wish 
>to represent.

The point I was trying to make is that the KIF expression (sibling ?x ?y)
provides the means to say everything that is necessary to represent that
relation for the purpose of defining the SUO.
If we want to go to the next step and discuss the relation named sibling, we
can do so by talking about the KIF expression without getting involved with
all the issues about what that expression represents.
I am not making this proposal for the purpose of eliminating metaphyics,
which I have a very high regard for.  I am only making it for the very
selfish purpose of reducing the clutter in my input mailbox.
John Sowa
