GR = Gary Richmond
JA = Jon Awbrey

GR: Peirce noted somewhere that America will have little
    to do with finished work.  It's too impatient;
    it wants  "immediate gratification".

there's a troubled meaning in that, of course, for what
is "immediate consummation" but a precocious exaltation.

and yet i'm afrayed that peirce knew a nuther frontier
than the selvage of america we see in the mirror today.
that thrill of being in medias res is all but vanished,
and packaging is all, never mind what's inside the box.
speak of process, of ongoing inquiry, and folks demand
that you summarize the things that they do not know in
twenty-five words or less.

GR: But things are finished in the details and in the
    relationship of the details to each other and to
    the whole.  I would say that your work

JA: toward a functional integration
    of logic and information within
    a reasonable account of inquiry

GR: is especially important at this juncture of cyber-history.

naturally, i have to concur, but recurring events recurrently remind
me of what i can never more than transiently bring myself to forget,
that the technology will not save us from the dynamics of our actual
and far-off-ideal communities of inquiry.

GR: As for your seeking an organized way to begin something new,
    I myself continue to explore trichotomic as being the most
    promising avenue toward intellectual advancement and, perhaps,
    eventual breakthrough (though a recent decisely dyadic analysis
    of logical breadth/depth reminded me that not everything lends
    itself to that sort of analysis-synthesis).  [?]

GR: As regards your commenting that maybe [you] mistake the nature of the organon - -
    I will only comment that since scientific advancement in the Peircean sense
    is "by nature" necessarily collaborative (setting aside the rather personal
    exception of abduction), I would say you need your collaborators.  My own
    work is in such a decidedly different direction -- at least in form --
    that I have not been able to engage you, though -- as I have noted
    publicly and privately to you -- I continue to learn from your
    important work.

JA: would that i could be starting something new ~~~
    but there is all of this unfinished business - -
    and so many threads spin out from this point - -
    the endeavor toward a functional integration
    of logic and information within a reasonable
    account of inquiry, the obstacles to inquiry
    that rise up to block any way forward, as if
    in the very moment that we derive a positive
    momentum in any direction at all.  and still ~ ~
    i look for an organized way to begin and see
    nothing at all of that organic kind to start ---
    or maybe i mistake the nature of the organon - -

the sign of the times, for those who read between the lines:

"youthful folly pushing upward"

jon awbrey
