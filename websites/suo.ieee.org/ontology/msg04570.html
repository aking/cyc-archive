PLG.  Discussion Note 2

o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o

The off-list queries of a reader lead me to make the following
explanations of what I'm up to in the present line of inquiry.

In the "Peirce Through The Looking Glass" thread I am posting
quotations that will eventually be crucial to a discussion of
the Duhem-Quine thesis, along with some of the related themes
that Rorty raises in his 'Philosophy and the Mirror of Nature'.
The quotes from 'Measure for Measure', that will include Peirce's
notorious use of Isabella's speech, lead up to the suggestion that
Rorty misinterpreted both Shakespeare's and the Author of the Bible's
use of mirror imagery in their respective works in a way that reflects
Rorty and his whole Army's misunderstanding of correspondence theories
of truth, and that this is also the most common misinterpretation of
Peirce's use of the word "correspondence" in the NEM definition of
the sign relation.

As it happens, it has been some years since I last visited these texts,
and I am pleasantly surprised to see that I am seeing some new things
in them, hopefully on account of having learned some things in the
mean time, and so I am planning to go with the flow as far as
that goes.

Jon Awbrey
