Pat & Pat,

A really small, indeed, a micro-physical point:
You seem to be ignoring all of that stuff in
high-energy physics and quantum mechanics,
about the different kinds of statistics,
specifying what types of particles are and
what types of particles are not indistinguishable
from each other, subject only to group symmetry laws,
about interpretations of QM in which positrons are
just electrons travelling backwards in time,
and so on, not to mention the rub of making
sense of "physical continuity" atoll at
a microphysical level, ad finitum, ...

So would it not be better just to formulate
the logical definitions of these properties,
if you can, or to borrow them from folks who
already have, if you can find them, and let
the chips of the actual physical world fall
where they may, as gathered by experiment?

Just A Thought,

Jon Awbrey
