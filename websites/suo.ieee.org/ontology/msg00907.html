But I can't avoid making a strong WARNING here:

At 2:25 PM -0800 7/2/01, Ian Niles wrote:
> > From: Chris Partridge [mailto:chris_partridge@csi.com]
> > [snip]
> > Ian suggests that "there is no overlap between ContinuantType and
>> OccurrentType. I think this needs clarification. If one is an
>> extensionalist, one would typically identify a thing with its
>> life (they
>> have exactly the same spatio-temporal (and modal) extension). So the
>> property of being a human life and being human would be the
>> same property
>> (as they have the same extension). So body-type and
>> event-type would not be
>> disjoint. However a non-extensionalist (such as an
> > endurantist) may prefer a
> > scheme where these were disjoint. It maybe useful if the SUO
> > strategy vis a
> > vis extensionalism were clarified (some baggage comes, of
> > course, with KIF).
>
>This is a good point.  If we included 'Human' under 'ContinuantType' and
>'Human-Life' under 'OccurrentType' in the merged ontology, then we would
>violate the disjointness condition on ContinuantType/OccurrentType, because
>'Human' and 'Human-Life' are co-extensional and we are assuming an
>extensional interpretation of SUO-KIF.

WARNING!!! you are using the adjective "extensional" in two different 
senses here:

The SUO-KIF is "extensional" in the sense that it has a standard 
(Tarskian) semantics (that is, roughly, predicates denote sets). So 
the "extension" of a predicate is a particular set of elements of the 
domain. Two predicates are coextensional if they denote the same set.

In Chris Partridge's comments, an extension is a spatio-temporal 
entity. Two entities are coextensional iff they have the same 
spatio-temporal extension.

In the former case we are talking about predicates (universals), in 
the latter we are talking about particulars (instances of universals).

So there is no contradiction at all in including Human under 
ContinuantType and HumanLife under OccurrentType, as long as we 
assume that a person and its life are different. Even if we assume 
that they have the same spatio-temporal extension, the contradiction 
only arises if we assume that two things are the same iff they have 
the same spatio-temporal extension.

So the reason of the contradiction is the extensionality principle 
(for those who assume it! - I don't), not the extensionality of 
SUO-KIF. Unfortunately, these two uses of the term "extensionality" 
are completely different.
