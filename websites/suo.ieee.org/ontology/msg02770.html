I agree that there are many other important "measurable attributes" besides
space and time.  However, I think that location in space/time does rather
simply pick out a class of entities which are very important to us.
Furthermore, there is a large subhierarchy of quantity types in the Merged
Ontology (this subhierarchy was extensively revised by Helena Sofia Pinto):

(subclass-of LengthMeasure ScalarQuantity)
(subclass-of MassMeasure ScalarQuantity)
(subclass-of TimeMeasure ScalarQuantity)
(subclass-of ElectricCurrentMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of ThermodynamicTemperatureMeasure ScalarQuantity)
(subclass-of LuminosityIntensityMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of AmountOfSubstanceMeasure ScalarQuantity)

(subclass-of PlaneAngleMeasure IdentityMeasure)
(subclass-of SolidAngleMeasure IdentityMeasure)
(subclass-of FrequencyMeasure TimeDependentQuantity)
(subclass-of ForceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of PressureMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of EnergyMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of PowerMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of ElectricChargeMeasure TimeDependentQuantity) 
(subclass-of ElectricPotentialMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of CapacitanceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of ElectricResistanceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of ElectricConductanceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of MagneticFluxMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of MagneticFluxDensityMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of InductanceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of LuminousFluxMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of IlluminanceMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of ActivityMeasure TimeDependentQuantity)
(subclass-of AbsorbedDoseMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of DoseEquivalentMeasure FunctionQuantity)
(subclass-of CatalyticActivityMeasure FunctionQuantity)

(subclass-of CurrencyMeasure ScalarQuantity)
(subclass-of InformationMeasure ScalarQuantity)

You can get more detailed information about any of these classes by entering
their names into our ontology browser
(http://ontology.teknowledge.com/rsigma/).

> 
> The measurable attributes of "sunlight" for instance are not 
> space-time, but
> e.g. "spectrum" and "intensity".
> A "fluid state" measurable attributes are e.g. density distribution,
> pression, temperature, Reynolds number, viscosity ...
> 
> So it seems that Measurable is more generic than space and time.

Well, the fact that 'Physical' is located at a higher level in the ontology
than 'Quantity' doesn't mean that location in space/time is regarded as more
privileged than other sorts of measures.  It's just a useful way of picking
out a large class of entities that we're interested in.  We could have used
other measurable attributes, but space/time is traditional.  It is important
to note too that 'LengthMeasure' and 'TimeMeasure', the classes of
quantities of space and time, respectively, are located below 'Quantity' in
the Merged Ontology.

Note that the Merged Ontology does contain a distinction in types of
quantities that is similar to your Discrete/Continuous dichotomy.  That
distinction is 'FunctionQuantity' versus 'ScalarQuantity'.  Again, for
further details about these classes, you can just enter their names into our
ontology browser.
