Matthew wrote:
> > number ::= [-] digit+ [. digit+] [exponent]
> > exponent ::= e [-] digit+
> 
> MW: 1. This does not include support for imaginary numbers.

There is all the support you need.  Just axiomatize a "square-root-of"
operator appropriately (notably, as applied to -1).

> MW: 2. In the interests of honesty you might point out that only rational
> numbers are supported ...

I believe the right word is "accuracy", not "honesty".  (Or would be
if your point were sound.)

> ...(and not all of them).

Granted, you can't have a finite decimal representation of every
rational, but of course one isn't forced to use that representation in
an ontology of rationals.  If you introduce rationals explicitly as
ratios of integers, represented as "m/n", say, then there would be a
representation for every rational.

-chris
