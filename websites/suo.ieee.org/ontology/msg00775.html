Mathias,

When I was passing through the field of psychology,
I eventually came to write a program, "Theme One"
by name, that was designed to integrate a number
of prototypical aspects and selected functions
of inductive and deductive reasoning by basing
them on a "common denominator", as I called it,
that is to say, the LUB in an implicit lattice
of abstract graph-theoretic data structures --
but a surprising side-effect of this work was
that I learned a whole lot about the reasons
why "empircists" and "rationalists" have so
much trouble understanding each other, and
even when, or especially when, it is the
divergent sides of the very same thinker
that one discovers to be at issue here.

The deductive functions of my program are
implemented via an interpretation of this
underlying data structure that I developed
from the Alpha Layer of Peirce's ExisGraphs.

The inductive functions are built around a core utility
that consists of a "two-level formal language learner",
and which I applied at the time to problems in protocol
analysis and sequential data sets, while working within
a qualitative research paradigm.  Here is an example of
the sort of dataset that I used it to explore and model:
