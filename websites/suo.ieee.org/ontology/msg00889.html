Let me just try to start here a more casual rendition
of what is bound to become a very formal animadversion
on another strand of this very same thread.

I would like to broach the subjects of "Efficiency" and "Elegance".
Strange as it may seem, there is a positively reinforcing relation
between the pair, and so you might say that I am a believer in the
aesthetic-efficient, "kleeneliness next to gödeliness", maxim that:
<Function Follows Form Follows>*.

After some early and very bad experiences with Fortran and Cobol,
I somewhat later worked up the resolve to return to computational
approaches to mathematics solely because there had arisen in my
work in the meantime too many mathematical questions to which
I just had to know some kind of answer, and which it became
clear that I just could not achive through my own devices,
without building more devices, that is.  Well, I imagine
that you know what happens -- I never goy back to those
instigative questions, but I can still remember what
they were, and I have a better sense of the orders
of theoretical power, in the sense of implemented
pers-picacity, that it would take to return to
them -- and I am just nowhere near there yet.

In a sense, this has more to do with "selectivity",
yet another way of saying "elegance", than it does
with rawsome "generativity".

> (Higher-order quantifiers?  Type theory?)

Just a hint as to what I would like to see in these connections:
There is a way of extending ZOL in the general direction of FOL,
perhaps not entirely getting there, but establishing a logistic
resource of useful base-camps, way-stations, or stepping-stones
along the path, in a sense, by pitonning the "fractional orders"
that one can see between the Zeroth of ZOL and the First of FOL.
This is an old idea that goes back to the beginning of the last
century, but the basic insight is documented in Quine's article
on the computational complexity of propositional calculus which
was collected in his little anthology on 'Theories and Things'.
