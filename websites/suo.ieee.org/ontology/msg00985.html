At 10-02-01 18:23, skydog@pacbell.net wrote:
>Pat,
>
>pat hayes wrote:
>>Let me restate my position more delicately. I don't want to be on
>>record as claiming that it is *impossible* to merge them. I do think
>>that axioms written using one framework of ideas won't be mixable
>>with axioms written using the other: their conjunction will just be
>>nonsense, and even simple quantifier restrictions won't get them
>>integrated properly. I also think that to do this merging will
>>require a lot of work, both analytical and technical, and that it
>>isn't going to get done by any methodology which tries to 'fit' both
>>kinds of ideas into some common upper heirarchy.  Either they won't
>>both fit, or else the heirarchy will simply classify them into two
>>disjoint areas.
>>
>>
>I agree.
>
>Determining the framework (i.e. the universe of discourse, the 
>mathematical structure, the factors, etc.) is a necessary first 
>step.   However,  the analytical work required to place a concept in any 
>ontology should be (at least partially) reusable.  Even if all of the 
>axioms are unusable in the new ontology, just the thoughtful consideration 
>of the issues the axioms were intended to resolve is a major advantage.
>
>Bob

### A useful remark supportive (as I see it) of my earlier comment on the 
need of development of minimal methodological principles for ontology 
engineering in parallel with their axioms and concepts. But beg pardon, 
just who is this (anonymous?) Bob Skydog, please?
