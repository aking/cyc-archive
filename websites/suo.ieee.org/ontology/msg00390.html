I agree with Adam that the best or at least a good way to make
progress is to address concrete proposals with updates and
revisions that are at least as specific, if not more so.

But I also agree with Chris P:

>I am not sure that - as Adam suggests - the process of making concrete
>suggestions via email is adequate for the task at hand. A point that a
>number of people have made in the past.

I agree.  E-mail updates are good for making incremental
changes, but we still have major disagreements about the
foundations that are not going to be resolved by multiple
e-mail messages.

>I suppose that you could argue I should make a concrete suggestion for a way

>to produce an adequate product. I think the task is of a size that anything

>I would suggest would involve the old standards such as a project with
>resources and a plan that gave some substance to the process of analysis to

>be followed and the deliverables. But there is no point in starting this
>unless there is some kind of commitment in terms of resources.

Resources are essential for getting people to do serious work
on a project.  Most of us have many competing demands on our
time, energy, and funding.  We cannot afford to devote the
necessary effort to the SUO project unless we can get adequate
compensation that would make it worthwhile for us to drop or
defer other projects we are involved with.

John Sowa
