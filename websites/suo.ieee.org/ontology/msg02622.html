Bernard,

You now have your first bit of anthropological data
about the local culture of the island nation that
the locals in question call "SUO IS AN IS LAND",
and into which you have so haplessly wandered.
I hope that you are not going to be yet another
one of those missionary types who make the mistake
of trying to prescribe your ways to the inhabituants.
They tend to end up being thoroughly decanonabilized.

They denizens of this loci once had a mantra that
they chanted for many days and many nights on the
theme of -- what else? --:

| Excessive Volume Of Effort For Far Too Few Results
|
| http://suo.ieee.org/email/msg04456.html

What caused this outbreak of sutrification?  Well, I'll tell you.
Two or three fellows with nothing better to do one rainy weekend,
globally speaking, fell into a discussion of what was actually
a very pertinent matter, in their own minds, of course, and by
the time that they had got very far in hammering out the finer
points of their diverse and sundry opinions, which is about
the best that can be envisioned here, they had tributaried
many a de trop to the flood.  Comes the bright Monday
morning, and all the rest of people of the land wake
up aghast at the deluge of the others' delusion that
meets them when they open their windows.  Now, most
folks are polite, but one who is self-elected to
the august body impolitic fires a volley of rude
and obnoxious remarks, and, being self-elected,
who can question his authority but he, and he
never does, so far as I can see.  The fact
that these aspersions said nothing of any
substance, nor addressed that in their
hapless targets, made them impossible
to counteract on substantial grounds,
and so they went down as holey writ.
Whereupon an uproar was raised, and
the hue and cry of the e-flamed mob
sought in vain for a sacrifical goat.
Instead of commenting on the obvious
circumstance that some people had just
said what one person did not want to hear,
and that is all there really was to it, they
blamed the general volume in which they all live
as being the culprit and the perpetrator of their
unaccounted, but not literally unaccountable unease,
their affective distress and their cognitive dissonance.
You, with your anthropological and psychological insights,
recognize this phenomenon as a textbook case of "displacement",
blaming the weather, or whatever, for the undiscursable causality,
but this particular culture has yet to e-volve as far as ontologizing
themselves, that is to say, the contents and the conduct of their psyches,
and what they do not deign to ontologize, is quite simply not allowed to exist.

But I digress ...

After it had droned on for what seemed like an unbearable length of time,
I found myself led into the temptation of using a kleene star to abridge
its point, say in the form [Too Many Notes About]*, but by the time that
occurred to me the actual subject matter under the subject line had quite
magically mutated into something else entirely, of a character that I had
to admit to myself, albeit a bit grudgingly, might just grow up oneday to
be potentially constructive and even positively creative, so I decided
that it were a far better thing I do to simply let slipping dogmas lie.

| "Moral Of The Story" (MOTS):
|
|  When it comes to what e-volves from a
|  natural selection of random mutations,
|  you have to take the bad with the good.

Jon Awbrey
