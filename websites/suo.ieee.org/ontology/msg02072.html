Hi, Pavel,

You should look at the tutorials/presentations that have been given in
the very recent past by Mike Uschold & Mike Gruninger (multiple AAAI
tutorials), Nicola Guarino & Chris Welty (AAAI-2000), and Gomez-Perez on
the very same topic, and their citations. I also have given a tutorial
to business people on the same topic and am preparing a university
undergraduate/graduate course. CGs are only part of the story; there is
a very rich and formal literature in ontological engineering. 

I would include some notions about formal language theory, logic, formal
semantics, knowledge representation in AI, and notions from philosophy
such as formal ontology, and formal methods in CS (all of these
necessarily abbreviated in any given course, i.e., you can have more in
the graduate, less in the undergraduate). Also, logic and constraint
programming (in fact, I am thinking about employing Protege or OILed and
then having students transform/compile the knowledge so represented into
Prolog Horn clauses, although we might also use Cyc). Also, the recent
web languages such as RDF/S, XML Schema, etc., and their relationships
to KR/OE. You might look at some recent comparisons by the Semantic Web
folks (Staab, Decker, Fensel, etc.) about the latter emerging
languages.  When I have my final syllabus, I will send it to you (and
yours to me, if you wish). 

Best,
Leo
