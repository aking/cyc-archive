>Frank,
>
>I agree "There is no need to be cutesy...".
>The Webster Dictionary defines Cutesy as [cute + sy (as in folksy)] : (1914)
>: self-consciously cute : MANNERED, and Cute \'kyut\ adj cut-er;
>cut-est[short for acute] (1731) 1. CLEVER, SHREWD 2: attractive or pretty
>esp. in a dainty or delicate way 3 : obviously straining for effect...
>Oh by the way Frank, what is cutesy in your opinion?  How does word 'cutesy'
>is best applicable to Jon's writings?

Allow me to interject: Jon's contributions are (1) mannered (2) 
self-conscious and (3) obviously straining for effect (rather than 
content.) They are usually preceded by a comment on the comment 
itself and usually terminated by an excuse for not actually saying 
anything. Most of what takes him a paragraph to write could be said 
in a few words. When he does say something concrete he usually gets 
it wrong.

>Jon Awbrey is an interesting and witty writer who at times can be perceived
>as a prankish Shakespearian Ariel, but who brings good ideas to the 'table'
>and who can poetically express them.

I wish he were Ariel; at least he would be fun to read. I wonder if 
you could point to, say, three or four of those good ideas that Jon 
has brought to the 'table' in his postings to this group? So far I 
have found one - it was basically Korzybski's dictum that the medium 
is not the message, but it took Jon two screen-fulls to (almost) say 
that and it was so buried in meaningless word-salad as to be almost 
incomprehensible.

I have never met Jon Awbrey. I wish him no harm, and I know nothing 
of his work, but reading his emails has all the appeal of watching a 
dog lick its own arse.
