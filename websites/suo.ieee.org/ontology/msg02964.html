KJ: What is the former really saying in relationship to the latter?
    Well, we realize it’s saying that the latter is an instantiation
    of the former and that we could substitute the former for the latter.
    So, we’ll substitute that in.

KJ: Now we still recognize that “S = Not S” is false.
    But we also recognize that we are not saying that.  
    We are simply saying “S = S” which is true.

KJ: Digression:  We know the fraction ½ is Not the fraction 3/6.
    We also recognize that 3/6 is an instantiation of ½ and we can
    substitute the former for the latter.  Now we recognize that we
    were really speaking of ½ and ½.

KJ: There is no restriction on the persuasive arguments you might employ
    to attempt to establish or elaborate on the underlying identity of
    an object of interest.  Again, I don’t have a problem with that
    per se but I recognize it because I think it illustrates ‘moves’
    (shifts, etc.) that our formal reason can and does make.

KJ: It has to do with the ‘borders’ whereon a paradox sits.  Does the border belong

KJ: No, I was thinking of the borders of ‘reason’.
