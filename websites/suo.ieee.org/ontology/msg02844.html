Jim Schoening wrote:

> All,
> 
> A common netiquette rule is to NOT post "I agree" messages to a large list.
> 
> Since we have a volume problem, I suggest we start following this rule.
> 
> These messages do have some value, but on a large list are usually not
> worth the added volume.  If everyone did this, it would be a flood.   
> 
> One alternative is to send a private message to the person you are
> agreeing with.  Another is to add value in some way and then reply
> with your support.  
> 
> Jim Schoening

¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤~~~~~~~~~¤

Jim,

I agree.

But seriously, here is another idea for reducing email volume.
I mentioned this before, but perhaps the message got lost in
a mess of other messages.  Currently, all of the mail from the
main list is being dumped onto the "dedicated ontology" list.
Not only does this make for a major redundancy in the Archives,
but even if people wanted to put themselves out to that idyllic
pasture, they would still be bogged down by all of the email on
procedural matters, basic set theory tutorials, and all of the
other stuff that currently occupies the main list.  Since I do
not really get off -- strange as the thought may be to some! --
on wasting people's time with contributions that they do not
eventually come to view as contributions, and I have no wish
to be always IYF ("in your face") about it, just in order to
stay in touch with the subgroup that is still talking to me,
I would be more than happy to restrain the majority of my
email to that list, so long as this negative feature of it,
which I am guessing is inadvertent anyway, were remediated.
Of course, I can only speak for myself, and I still suspect
that people who presently choose the main list do so because
they really believe that they are right on target, and would
not like to endure the sense of isolation that comes from not
being where the perceived action is.  Just my guess, though.
I do notice, as evidence of this, that neither does the KIF
sublist get used for KIF matters, nor the CE sublist for CE.
Therefore, I am only saying, contingently, what I will do.

Yours truly,

Jon Awbrey
