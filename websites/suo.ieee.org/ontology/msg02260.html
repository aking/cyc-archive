I wrote:
> > > That said, can't more than one person give more than one thing 
> > > to morethan one person in a giving event?
> > 
> > Not if the type of giving event is (gives ?x ?y ?z).
> 
> I'm not so sure.  It is not obvious to me that that the meaning of
> "gives" entails that when A gives B to C, that A *alone* gives B to C.
> If it doesn't, then, if both John and Martha give a book B to Mary, it
> is correct to say both (gives John Mary B) and (gives Martha Mary B).
> But if that is right, then your clause 1) above is false; the same
> giving event can involve more than one giver.

Just to be clear, the idea in the example is that John and Martha
*jointly* give the same book (token) B to Mary in the very same giving
event.  The question, then, is whether, in such cases, we can correctly
say both (gives John Mary B) and (gives Martha Mary B).  My intuition is 
that we can.  But that is inconsistent with Fred's principle 1).
