"YAP"? Not sure about it ... I did not intend to write that any "positive"
ontology will ever come out all-armed from the process I propose. It's not
even in a corner of my mind - except maybe in some weird dreaming remote one
... So I suppose I have to go deeper in what I am really about.
I'm tracking the moves of vultures to try and find out where the corpses lie
in the desert. Or maybe I'm more interested in the vultures moves themselves
than by the stinking carrions they gather around. In the topic map community
these days, people are hypnotized by a metaphysical question: a topic is a
system representation of a subject ... but what is a subject? The approach I
am pushing here and there is that a subject is a gathering of vultures,
about a carrion that is generally so much rotten and with so many birds
around that it is no more possible to identify it. And that much of the time
you would find nothing there more than this gathering, because the first
vulture to home there was misleading or blind and the others made the
mistake to trust its indication.
So what I'm interested in is the geography of vultures gathering points. And
for that I claim we can be positive and see this geography from the sky
above. The clustering algorithms will never ever let us discover any
absolute "core concepts" and even less "core relationships" - that is "why"
the vultures are there. But maybe they will help us to target and focus on
those binding points we tacitly agree to gather around. What lies at these
points we'll not identify in a positive way, nor why those points are linked
the way they are. I fully agree that *whatever* is focusing conversation
around those points is best defined as "nor-neither". And going to
"fundamental" is always cutting off rather than putting more, I could not
agree more about that.

To build a fundamental tool for this "more and more" civilization out of a
"nor-neither" vacuity-driven approach would be a delightful paradox indeed
... But I digress ...

Bernard

Bernard,

I think I am having a near-death experience.  Every half-baked idea
that I ever had as a kid is flashing before my eyes all over again --
when I thought that I used know just how the mind did its bit -- that
was before, of course, I ever sat down to deal with the computer and
it gave me all the rope that I needed to call my own bluff.  But now
we have systems that are designed to help us preserve our illusions --
just like the earliest of all virtual reality environments, by the
name of "Fortran", that allowed the physicists to deny for just
a little bit longer the reality of the information dimension.

What you are proposing here is all too familiar to me.  My personal pet
name for it is "yet another positivism" (YAP) -- all puns are
intentional! --
by which I mean that it is embodies the mistake of starting out with
linkages
of a positive character and failing to realize that no real logic can ever
come
of that.  Clustering, connectionism, for all Icarus, they are birds of no
feather
in this.  What you need to realize, and to comprehend the significance of,
is what
Peirce knew when he discovered the "amphecks" (from the Greek for "cutting
both ways"),
that the logical negative, in some form or other, is more generative than
the positive,
since you can get positives from joining only negatives, but no negatives
from linking
only positives.  This should tell you something very telling about what you
should pick
as logically primitive relationships.

Jon Awbrey
