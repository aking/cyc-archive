Jim, 
	.	I want to support Pat on this issue. Some of us are very
busy and work long hours.  

	.	While I'm unaware of ever missing a vote, I couldn't be at
all confident I mightn't miss some inadvertently in the daily flood of
e­mails. This applies  especially when the pressure is on here and/or being
away from the office for a few days, or on leave, in which case the e­mail
backlog rapidly becomes enormous. 

	.	I think missing 2 votes is too few; and I think some form of
warning notification as per Pat's suggestion would be excellent. 

	.	In modern days where e­mail far outruns snail­mail, I
suggest the notification should be (also) by post (where participants have
advised postal addresses). This would be far more conspicuous than just
another e­mail buried in the flood, and hence far less likely to be
overlooked, mis­filed, or otherwise missed.

Jim --
   I think the criterion you suggest for loss of voting rights is too
stringent -- I would prefer that a voting member be required to vote
(possibly by abstention) on a majority of ballots over some three-month
period, and a probaytionary period be then instituted before voting rights
are terminated.  Also, I think that it should be easier to resume voting
rights once terminated than to acquire them initially.

    Pat Cassidy
