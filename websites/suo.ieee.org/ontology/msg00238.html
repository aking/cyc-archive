John,

I have to respectfully disagree with your last statement.  The definition of mapping specifications between data models as been part of the ISO 10303 (STEP) standard for over 10 years now.  Within my company, we've been building tools to leverage these mappings for model-driven data conversion/translation for several years.  And personally I have been researching what this mapping - which we call Semantic (data) Mapping - is all about.  ("Semantic Mapping" entails "Data Mapping", i.e., the conversion of one data representation into another, not unlike the way XSLT behaves.) 

I believe it IS possible to map a sentence from one ontology to another.  It merely takes the assertion of some human to say "these bits of this ontology mean=the=same=thing=as that collection of things from this other ontology".  It is true that it will only yield an approximate semantic equivalence (like translation of natural languages), but multiple "executions" of the map will surface the ambiguities and, thus, the ontologies and the map can be "tuned" so the translation becomes more correct.  Just like natural language behavior, a certain amount of level-setting is needed before communication works smoothly.

Bill
