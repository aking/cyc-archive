Bernard,

I think I am having a near-death experience.  Every half-baked idea
that I ever had as a kid is flashing before my eyes all over again --
when I thought that I used know just how the mind did its bit -- that
was before, of course, I ever sat down to deal with the computer and
it gave me all the rope that I needed to call my own bluff.  But now
we have systems that are designed to help us preserve our illusions --
just like the earliest of all virtual reality environments, by the
name of "Fortran", that allowed the physicists to deny for just
a little bit longer the reality of the information dimension.

What you are proposing here is all too familiar to me.  My personal pet
name for it is "yet another positivism" (YAP) -- all puns are intentional! --
by which I mean that it is embodies the mistake of starting out with linkages
of a positive character and failing to realize that no real logic can ever come
of that.  Clustering, connectionism, for all Icarus, they are birds of no feather
in this.  What you need to realize, and to comprehend the significance of, is what
Peirce knew when he discovered the "amphecks" (from the Greek for "cutting both ways"),
that the logical negative, in some form or other, is more generative than the positive,
since you can get positives from joining only negatives, but no negatives from linking
only positives.  This should tell you something very telling about what you should pick
as logically primitive relationships.

Jon Awbrey
