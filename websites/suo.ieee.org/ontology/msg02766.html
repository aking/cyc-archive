Title: RE: RE: Re: Now What?

Graham:

>       .       What I believe you are forgetting is that the core of
> natural languages, and of how human societies function, are very stable, and
> strikingly similar, even from very different roots.

If you consider the features top-to-bottom of what makes up natural language and human societies, I would agree that they are *relatively* stable given our lifespans.  However on a moment-by-moment, surface-level basis, they are completely diverse and utterly unique.  For example, we are both using English to communicate, but I use it differently than you.

 
>       .       If we can capture the stable core of information/knowledge
> structures for interchange, we will still have something to
> be proud of.

But it would be useful for our day-to-day tasks and would deteriorate over time as people/societies/endevaours evolve and change.

I don't believe that pursuing this goal - however pure and idealistic - will yield us anything useful.

Bill Burkett
