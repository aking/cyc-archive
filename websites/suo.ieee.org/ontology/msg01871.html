All,
        Allow me to reply to a few more of Bob's points. 
 
>  In private communications to me, Jim has asserted that he has broad power to do what he thinks best,   
 
I have been advised by representatives of our next higher body that a WG Chair has broad power to do what he or she thinks is best, plus I was advised to wield this power to keep order and help with progress.  I am taking this advice.
 
> [He] does not recognize my objection to this action and is not bound by Robert's Rules.  
 
In declaring the selection of Ian Niles as Technical Editor, I did recognize your objection, but concluded it was not enough to prevent this action.  I originally didn't recognize it because I interpreted that you were objection to a prior decision, but you cleared this up and I later did recognize it.
 
[He] is not bound by Robert's Rules.  
This is admittedly a void.  Common practice in IEEE is that Robert's Rules do not apply to email discussions, since they could easily be abused to stall progress. In a meeting, quick votes as possible by voice or show of hand.  Via email, each vote can take 1-2 weeks and are burdensome.  The IEEE CS SAB (our next higher body) is working on rules for email groups.  I'll post them when available, but for now, I will attempt to keep things as fair as possible.
 
>  In another circumstance Jim has asserted that since there were only two objections, those were not enough.  He seems to think that he has the right to determine if objections meet his own (unstated) test of reasonableness and how many objections are enough.  
 
> I do think we should specify the number of objections that can force a vote, but for now, I (as Chair) made that determination and stand by it.  You are free to appeal this.
 

If the chair does not act to resolve this issue,  I will make a formal motion that incorporates the process outlined above.  This will require a vote. 

In a physical meeting (which we are not), working under Robert's Rules (which we are not), a motion with a second gets voted on.    In our current situation, I would say that any relevant motion that has enough support to show any conceivable chance of passage, I will gladly put to a vote. 

Bob
