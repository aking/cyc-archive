Title: RE: SUO: RE: Commonwealth of Ontologies

John:


> >I think tying the semantics of an ontology to the
> >target community is the only viable and practical way for
> the elements
> >of the SUO to "mean" anything.
>
> Unfortunately, such a strict tying cannot support communication
> between communities.  And every major enterprise or even every
> large project in an enterprise involves multiple communities
> that use and reuse the same words in different language games.

I agree that tying semantics to a community of users inhibits communication between communities.  However, the solution that I prefer to this particular problem differs, I think, from most of those on this list.  I think it is ultimately both unrealistic and impractical to purse a single universal ontology that everyone on earth can agree to and use.  Won't happen.  I have seen far too many global data models and corporate data dictionaries  - all of them in fact! - turn into expensive boondoggles, leading me believe that it is not possible to come up with this global model with single universal, accepted, and understood semantics.

I would rather see more work put into bridging the community ontologies with semantic mapping language that acts (or can act) as a translator between the language.  My motto these days when it comes to ontologies and data models is "Have It Your Way".

Bill
