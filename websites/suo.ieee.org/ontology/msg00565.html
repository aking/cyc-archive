> I vote NO.
> 
> Comments: I don't mean to denigrate Niles' hard work, but it is 
> ridiculously premature to adopt this (or anything else like this) as 
> a base document. This document doesnt even come close to the 
> requirement for a base document given in the accompanying text:
> 
> >base document: Starting point for standards words (it's 
> clear the document
> >*can* be transformed into standards words).
> 
> It has no overall coherent ontological model; it represents a melange 
> of conflicting and incommensurate views on many basic ontological 
> issues. Its axioms have no global coherence (for example: the 
> intended meaning of the quantifiers varies from one place to another 
> in the document; later axioms do not conform to the categories 
> introduced in the earlier axioms; many, if not most, of the major 
> classifications depend on questionable and controversial philosphical 
> presuppositions.) It fails to address many of the basic ontological 
> issues that ned to be got clear before we can put any ontology to 
> practical use (how to represent time and change, how to deal with 
> alternative courses of reality, for example. ) Moreover, it is clear 
> that the more 'pieces' are added to it, the more these problems are 
> going to get worse rather than better.
> 
> This methodology is fundamentally flawed. There is no way to achieve 
> a coherent ontology by 'merging' axioms from incommensurate 
> ontological perspectives in this simplistic way. It is rather like 
> trying to create  a large piece of software by just adjoining pieces 
> of code written by different authors in different computer languages, 
> and expecting it to magically work.
> 
> Apart from the general issues of a lack of a coherent methodology, I 
> have many particular problems with much of the actual content of the 
> document. As it stands, the ontology it outlines would be unworkable 
> for me for almost any practical purpose, even if it were globally 
> coherent. But this is not the right place to go into details.
> 
> Pat Hayes
