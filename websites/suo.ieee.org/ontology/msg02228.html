Again, not at all.  I do appreciate that there are many different ways to 
model general ontologicial issues.  I simply believe that reasonable 
compromises and agreements can be made.  The motivation is that if people 
do agree then their software will be able to communicate effectively 
without the need for translation.  Maybe French is really the best language 
for communication but English, Spanish and Swahili are all adequate.  If 
there's a reason to agree on a common language (and my opinion is that 
there is such a reason), people will agree.

>Well, you introduced the example, so let me respond. First, KIF is a

Another difference of opinion I think.  In KIF we're creating a foundation 
and with that foundation in place I think there will be less flexibility 
for difference on the ontology.  A great deal of misunderstanding happens I 
feel because we're talking in ambiguous English.  Once people get to the 
point of being willing to state theories as formal axioms, I think 
resolution on many issues will be quicker.  I also should add that it is 
the highest levels of the ontology which are the hardest.  I've found 
progress in practice on application level ontologies to be much faster and 
so I think we'll see a dramatic acceleration in progress as agreement on 
upper levels is reached.

>>We don't all need to agree on every aspect of the SUO.  We merely need to

That's a neat example, and one I'd be happy to work through.  I'm likely to

all the details of the Merged Ontology.  Also, you'll have to grant that 
the Merged Ontology is a work in progress and that even in this example, 
we're likely to find concepts which are incomplete, underdefined or 
missing.  I think what we're trying to accomplish with this exercise is to 
zero in on parts of the ontology that which are somehow incompatible with 
each other.  Since I think that finding an unsolvable incompatibility is 
unlikely, I'll hope to have the result that we flesh out some missing 
concepts which Ian can include in a new version of the ontology.
