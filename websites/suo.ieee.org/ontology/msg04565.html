PLG.  Note 5

o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o~~~~~~~~~o

| The fundamental-seeming philosophical question, How much of our science
| is merely contributed by language and how much is a genuine reflection
| of reality? is perhaps a spurious question which itself arises wholly
| from a certain particular type of language.  Certainly we are in
| a predicament if we try to answer the question;  for to answer
| the question we must talk about the world as well as about
| language, and to talk about the world we must already
| impose upon the world some conceptual scheme
| peculiar to our own special language.
|
| Quine, IOH, p. 78.
|
| W.V. Quine, "Identity, Ostension, and Hypostasis", pp. 65-79 in:
|'From a Logical Point of View:  Nine Logico-Philosophical Essays',
| Second Edition, Revised, Harvard University Press, Cambridge, MA, 1980.
| Original version first published in the 'Journal of Philosophy', 1950.
