I agree with Jim that once one accepts a minimum participation
requirement, other things follow coherently, including provision for loss
of voting rights.  Otherwise, the dead weight of non-participants can drag
down the effort. 

As with a quorum/participation requirement, a central criterion for
judging procedures to purge the voting rolls is to guard against
domination by a rump. The incentive should always be to expand rather than
to decrease participation.

It is in this context that I strongly agree with all of Pat's points, the
general thrust of which is to make the procedures for purging the voting
rolls err on the side of inclusiveness:  the need for a time element, so
that a rapid sequence of votes in a concentrated period is not used to
disqualify people who happen to be temporarily engaged in some other
pursuit; a warning period and subsequent probationary element; the
distinction between returning from probation, which should be lenient, and
signing up in the first place, which takes six months.  The aim is to set
the requirements so that those dropped from the rolls are only those who
have lost their interest in the work.

Here, as elsewhere, if SUO has multiple documents pursued concurrently, we
need to distinguish carefully between group-wide balloting and technical
votes within multiple document-oriented groups, where soundings and
procedures are naturally looser. For each document-oriented subgroup I
would again refer to Matthew West's proposal-- as amended with respect to
a more inclusive time requirement by Pat Cassidy-- as a good start.  It is
within these sub-groups that the suspension of procedural requirements
(relating to participation levels, voting loss provisions, multiple
seconds) seem most appropriate.
