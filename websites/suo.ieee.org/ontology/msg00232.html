Title: RE: Commonwealth of Ontologies

Robert,

The notion of "communities" is becoming more recognized as a source of commonly-held semantic understanding of a language such as an ontology.  The Geographic Information System (GIS) research community call them "information communities"  (see Goodchild, M., et al., eds. Interoperable Geographic Information System. . 1999, Kluwer Academic Publishers: Norwell MA); data people in DoD call them "communities of interest"; even W3C has a loosely and poorly defined notion of "community" in Namespaces.  There are even sociological theories that state that the meaning of a language is a jointly negotiated and accepted convention among the members of a language community.

I think tying the semantics of an ontology to the target community is the only viable and practical way for the elements of the SUO to "mean" anything.

Bill Burkett  (still lurking - mostly silently)
