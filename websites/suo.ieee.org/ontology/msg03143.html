You.

Yes, you!

It always continues with you, does it not?

But your continuity breaks like waves on my shore,
Comes up to the edges of my sand-castle fortress;
It laughs at the sounds of the mock ocean's roar
In the sleepy hollow places of my conches's nest.

And I cannot see the wind that drives your waves,
Or the spirit that moves on their face and under,
Nor can I form any image at all, none that lasts,
None that stays the press of the next inundation.

A lass thass all sea rote ...

Jon Awbrey
