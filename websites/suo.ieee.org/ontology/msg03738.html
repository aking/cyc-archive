4.3  Multi-Sage Deconstruction of the Post*Modern Elephant

Here are some thoughts on how we might use a relational database system to
perform the kinds of reasoning tasks that I think are required to maintain
some level of intelligence in our tutoring system.  I start by enumerating
a list of needs that I think are categorically imperative and pragmatically
minimal in order to perform the logically essential services, especially the
functions of analysis and inference.

4.3.1  Prologue:  Recycling "A" for Analysis and "I" for Inference

In this note I pay careful attention to the problem of representing
negation and doing it properly from the beginning.  This is important
because the negative forms -- negation, inequality, "all not", "not all",
and so on -- are in a generative sense more basic than the positive forms.
We can generate the positive forms from the negative, but not vice versa.

Logical inference requires something beyond the simple association
of positive terms.  Inference is a species of association that is
able to function under critical control.  To accomplish this feat
it must be subject to reflection, to critical examination in the
light of certain goals, and to controlled change in the direction
of better achieving those goals.  The apprehension and application
of negations, the sensitivity to and responsibility toward negative
states, the recognition of differences in observation and exclusive
alternatives in action, are all essential functions (sine qua non's)
of this critical intelligence.  If we do not deal with these aspects
of logical reality at the very outset, and leave plenty of room for
the continuing examination of negative and differential features,
then we almost never regain the capacity to deal with them later.
We should not let our prospects and programs give up their hope
of a due extensibility for the sake of a preclusive modularity.

Jon Awbrey
