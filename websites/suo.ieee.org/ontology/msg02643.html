This passage is interesting because it shows two hierarchical sructures that
has to be taken in consideration for defining the context:

a - one has already been presented in my precedent mail (mail of Fri, 18 May
2001 00:15:14, The Very Model Of A Dogmatic Razor) and concerns
organizations at situational level : here disk 1 is located to disk 2
(repetition of 'le vert' in 3 and 4). At enunciative level, only disks 1 and
2 are selected (criteria; anaphore 'le'), the two other disks 3 and 4 stay
at situational level; furthermore, disks 2 is located to disk 1 in 1-2
(connective 'pour qu'après', et en 6-7 (connective 'parce que') 
So it is sufficient for the participant to focus his attention on disk 1 at
enunciative level, disk 2 is in the background. And all the other elements
and moves were deposite and structured in external environment. 

b - The second hierarchy corresponds to the structure of episods. The
criteria are connectives and thematic organizations. In this sequence, one a
main episod starts in 8 (accumulation 'bon ben alors' + le rose je), a
second level of episod appears in 1 (with only the connective 'après' + le
rose je), and a third in 5 (with après, but without anaphore); this
hierarchie give us the organisation of chunks, i.e. the constitution of more
or less complex agregates.

Here the interesting fact stands in the interjection 'oh la la', and in the
change of linguistic form which follows the interjection. A minimal
interpretation is to say that the participant has constructed a new operator
of the problem space. 
It can be possible to explicit this operator from linguistic analysis, and
also to caracterise at least partly the context (conditions) of this operator.

Josiane : 

Jon, your caricature of problem solving is right. Recent and important
papers on ToH considers only the number of trials in order to achieve two
consecutive times the optimal solution. And if the participant is too much
long they stop him. They are based on performance, and they do not concern a
semiotic approach of verbalizations. I don't think I have to be put in this
class. The difference between me and them is the same than the difference
between a pragmatic approach integrated to semantic and a propositional one.
I can retreive any classical result but I have to elaborate every
theoretical step of my pragmatic approach, and also the appropriate terminology.


Jon : 
But is there really something so generic that it rises above
the "family dissemblances" of these seemingly diverse games?
That is a question that may take a thoughtful person not
a little while to answer.

Josiane :
Yes, above the family dissemblances, linked to the diversity of language,
there are a lot of regularities. I will give you just some examples :

- first I thnk you know the triangle of ToH (the problem space of all the
possible states), with the optimal strategy on the right of the triangle. A
condition for staying on the optimal strategy at step (4)(3)(12) is to focus
attention on disk 2. If it is the participant can stay in the same problem
space, disk 1 is in the background (for example at situational level)? and
if she or he thinks of disk 2 as the disk 1 was before, it is tha same
problem as before, and she or he can stay on the optimal strategy. But if
there is no focus, or if the focus is on disk 1, and if the participant
continue in the same problem space, then she or he left the optimal stategy
and goes inside the triangle.

- Generaly the main goal is to put disk 4 on the goal peg (4 disks ToH), but
very often it is not, participants focus more on critical goals, for example
concerning moves of disk 2. Generally also there are connectives and
demarcation of episods just before the move of disk 4 to goal peg (cf in 5
for example given above : Jus)
