Graham,

The problem that Doug and I have been discussing is the very
painful one that plagues any programmer who has to convert
a working program from one compiler to another, even on
identical hardware.

>Allowing for the severely restricted vocabulary, if I communicate to you in

>a computer language in which you are literate, surely you will understand
>precisely what I say? 

Unfortunately, the most popular programming languages currently
in use are not as well standardized as one might be led to
believe.  What is called the "C language" by one software
company is not identical to the "C language" by another
company or even by the same company with different versions
of the compiler.  The differences are serious even with the
same operating system on the same hardware.  They get worse
when you go to different OSes on different hardware.

People who develop products that run on multiple platforms
have to go to enormous expense to accommodate the differences
between compilers for "the same language".

John Sowa
