Dear Matthew,

Saturday, March 31, 2001, 7:52:10 PM, you wrote:

WMMSGU> The world then divides neatly into two basic sorts, sets
WMMSGU> (including relations) and individuals (or particulars if you
WMMSGU> prefer) where individuals are things that have an extension
WMMSGU> in space time, and sets are things that have members.
WMMSGU> One thing to note about this position is that any piece of
WMMSGU> space time is an individual, just as any collection of things
WMMSGU> is a set. That is different again from having to be interested
WMMSGU> in all of them.

As I wrote to the SUO before suppose it  will be helpful to make one extention to your
point of view . After  the great many  discussions  and practices  in  biology  and
"scientific/technical  bibliography" there were formulated  some very important
 thesises (in the 70th) . For example :
- a real subject area with real  "individuals"  needs  a nonformal  prosedure
 of "transforming"  to a  "good set" - a  "classification field"  with "minimal
  taxons"  for  any modelling, discussion , research etc. There could be many ways to
   "organize"  a  classification field.
- the "minimal taxons"  are  our "reality" in models but  a subject area  must taken
 into account too.
- An identification  of any individual  to a  minimal  taxon is a nonformal
 procedure.
- The most  close  relation  between the individuals  (described by  a classification
 (nonformal classification !))  is their belonging to the same minimal taxon.
- More far  relations  are determined  ONLY  through  a  classification field
- Any classification system  has  a "sign system" nature  in principle .
- There must be  distinguished  two "dual"  parts in any classification system.
"External"  taxonomy  with  class-subclass/part-whole etc. relations
and  "internal"  meronomy ( a "properties"  structure with the assosiative relations )
- The set theory approach is not sufficiant  for modelling  without the "dual"  part.
- Etc.
Some info in English is in -
http://www.ento.vt.edu/~sharov/biosem/schreidr/schreidr.html
