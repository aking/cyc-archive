Chris Angus said:
> we may also want to refer to some associations
> between topics as if they were topics.  At a data modelling level this can
> lead one to simply treat 'association between topics' as a subclass of
> 'topic'.  But this leads to problems, in particular it is not necessarily
> appropriate to have an association inherit from topic because, in a
general
> case, properties of a topic are not all applicable in the case of an
> association.

Can you clarify which properties of a topic do not apply for an association?
Please don't confuse the model with the current XTM syntax, which (to keep
things simple) has decided to not allow some <topic> elements to be added
directly to an <association>.

I contend that an association "is a kind of" topic in every way.  A topic
reifies a subject; so does an association.  A topic can participate in
associations; so can an association.  The only difference right now is how
many of what kind of associations (a topic can be the subject of any number
of class-instance associations, an association just one).
