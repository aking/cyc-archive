Chris,

>>The most interesting thing Maddy does in defense of the physicality
>>thesis is to marshall neuroscientific evidence for the existence of
>>"set detectors" that enable us to perceive sets of physical objects.

Putting aside all doubts about "set detectors" in the brain,
I would raise the very serious question of whether the
neuro-physio-psycho-logists had even thought about the
distinction between set theory and mereology, let alone
trying to refine their tests to the level where they could
tell the difference.

I think it is better to treat sets as abstract and to use
a different term for physical collections.
