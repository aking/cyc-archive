MW: let me try something "real"

<purchase, Carter's, lawnmower, Honda CB250, GBP, 500, 12:15 12th August
1999>

This is reasonably typical of what you might find in a purchasing system.
It
might be said to represent a purchasing activity. However, you will note
that there is no single element in this tuple that represents that
activity,
only the class of activity (purchase). Similarly, there is no object that
is
a surrogate for the actual lawnmower purchased (e.g. its serial number),
but
only the type of item bought (lawnmower) and the manufacturers model (Honda
CB250) and even this has the manufacturers name bundled in. Since this is
an
international lawnmower shop, the have unusually given a unit of measure
for
the price paid, and we have the date and time of the purchase. It is highly
likely that there is only one activity that all these elements relate to.
But the question is, does this refer to an activity, or is it just a
relation of elements that are each related to an activity and together
uniquely identify it, but is not a surrogate for the activity itself? I
would argue that it is the latter, but custom and practice means that many
would accept it as the former.


MK>  Isn't it absolutely fundamental to any representation that:
1.  the representation is necessarily incomplete?
2. that being incomplete is very valuable, because it is easier to handle
for particular purposes?
3. that the incompleteness may be extreme, depending on what you judge it
against (e.g. a reconstruction of Matthew walking into a shop with a real
credit card in his hand and wheeling out an actual machine and putting it
in his car)?
To me it follows that if someone with a particular purpose in mind
demonstrates that a particular representation is complete enough for that
purpose, then it is a valid representation for that purpose.  Of course,
someone else with a different purpose in mind may find the same
representation of what he considers to be the same real world activity
deficient.

None of this, of course, should stop us trying to agree what would be a
useful level of detail of representation of activity for inclusion in the
SUO, but there will be no absolute criteria of whether what we agree is
right, only judgement of whether it is enough to useful for what we think
to be the likely uses.
