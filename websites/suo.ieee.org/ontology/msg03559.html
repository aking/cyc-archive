Dear Jon,

Thank you for your heroic effort at continuing your present modes of 
inquiry on these lists. I for one have benefited immensely from it 
intellectually--and sometimes spiritually, if I may use that expression 
in this context--at least when I was able follow your arguments and 
interpret your diagrams adequately. This one, for example, seems clear 
enough though I cannot say I grasp every dot and tittle. Your socio- 
psychological critiques have also been enlightening.

I am about to head off for an out-of-town family reunion and must leave 
my beloved New York City for my beloved family. But I  couldn't leave 
without saying   thanks a lot. And a healthy and happy holiday season 
and new year to you and to your wife Susan (whom I unfortunately only 
know from two papers she co-authored with you on aspects of education 
and which I quite  recently reread to advantage).

Best regards,

Gary

>Here, I have sketched, in what amounts to nothing so novel as
>would exceed a variant form of presentation, the old familiar
>story of "How the proposition about the world beyond our cave
>got itself factored along the lines of a bitwise coding and a
>propositional connective" or how the proposition q : X -> %B%
>is factored across a composition q = pc, where c : X -> %B%^k
>is a coding map and p : %B%^k -> %B% is the boolean function
>that corresponds to the propositional connective in question.
>
>As far as I can tell, all of these elements really are present
>in one implicit form or another whenever we use a mathematical
>style of sentential calculus to reason with propositions about
>an external universe of discourse X.
