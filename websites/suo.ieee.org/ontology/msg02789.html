Matt,

	I think we need to distinguish two different senses of
"organization".  In one sense, an "organization" is an institutionalized
group of people that exhibits intentionality.  In the SUMO, this sense of
"organization" is referred to by the concept 'Organization' (this class is a
subclass of 'GroupOfPeople' which, in turn, is a subclass of 'Group').  In
another sense, an "organization" is a structure (with a certain degree of
complexity and regularity).  This sort of "organization" can, to my mind, be
either intentional or naturally occurring.  This second sense does not, from
a quick search and from what I can recall, have a representation in the
SUMO.  It's an important notion, but so far it isn't covered.  If you would
be willing to define a concept and locate it in the SUMO, that would be very
worthwhile.  

	Now, to analyse the examples you give, I would say that a swarm of
bees, a culture of slime mold cells, the gameboard, and the particles are
"organizations" in the second sense, but not in the first sense.  However, a
swarm of bees would be a 'Group', to use the SUMO term, because it is a
group of Animals that exhibit agency. The culture of slime mold cells, the
gameboard, and the particles do not exhibit agency, so they are not examples
of 'Groups'.  A crowd of people in panic would be a 'GroupOfPeople', again
to use the SUMO term, but it would not be an 'Organization', because their
grouping is not institutionalized.  I hope this clarifies things.

-Ian
