now, it is manifestly unlikely that a single visit to the circus,
no matter how singular, nor a single affective impression of the
juggler's artistry, no matter how striking, could provide enough
data of the sort from which i might acquire the knack to produce
an adequate approximation in my own attempts to match the action.

so let us generalize the singularity of this signal but imaginary
event to encompass the general impression of virtuoso jugglers as
a genus of genius in a specialized art.  in corresponding fashion
let us broaden our conception of what i need to accomplish by way
of becoming accomplished in the art of juggling to take in rather
more than a single isolated act, for who would consider my action
a fait accompli if i could accomplish it but once, never again to
repeat the feat?  in deed, it would be needful to display in each
performance not merely the singularities of that sheer occurrence
but a "general conditional disposition to act" in equally crafted
exhibitions of competence, and on a recurring basis, would it not?

on jugera ...

jon awbrey
