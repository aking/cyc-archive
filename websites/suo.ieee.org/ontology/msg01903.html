Chris & Pat,

Sorry if I missed some details, but about all
this extensional versus intensional business --
how come you don't just use the language that
has been traditional since Leibniz at least,
saying that intensions are properties by any
other name, that classes are defined by their
extensions, that when life is good there is
relation between them that is fairly easily
"rationalizable" in terms of our human, all
too humanly partial knowledge of the world,
or "sampling considerations", if you prefer
to express it that way?  Just on behalf of
declaring my own lack of position, I think
that both extensional and intensional views
of things are necessary in practice, that
the common "tension" between them is most
likely essential, or at least unavoidable
in the mean time, and that our task is to
coordinate the knowledge that is gathered
on both of these two fronts.  But, aside
from the issue of remoting ourselves from
the stream of conversation that prdcsss us,
we cannot do this if we try to reduce either
one of them to the other, as the attempt to
use the term "class" in a way that confounds
it with the term "property" (even "concept",
for that matter) is just plain doomed to do.
Even if you believed that one of these entia
has just got to go, it's Ockham's razor, not
trash compactor.

Just My Spin,

Jon Awbrey
