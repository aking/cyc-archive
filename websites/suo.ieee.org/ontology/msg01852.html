At 25-03-01 15:42, Chris Menzel wrote:
On Sun, 25 Mar 2001, Robert Meersman wrote:

> At 25-03-01 09:33, Chris Menzel wrote:
>
> >I wrote:
> > > [Matthew wrote:]
> > > > If classes are non-extensional then I don't see what point there is in
> > > > having null.
> > >
> > > Nothing in the axioms entails that there is a null class.
> >
> >This is false, of course.  According to the axioms, every class has a
> >complement, hence (complement top) is null.
>
> ### No woman overboard, but note one can only prove this from the
> axioms if both top and null are known to be unique. There is as yet no
> axiom or meta axiom to this effect (nor is it provable of course).

I think you are confusing the property *null* (i.e., the property of
having no instances) with the class *nil*, Robert. 


### I am sure you are right, Chris. The confusion remains however after this reply --actually it is greater, since neitehr the ontology nor my query contain the notion of "property" and the ontology only uses "nil" in the context of a null sequence. Maybe I'm not alone with in this state of confusion.

The conclusion says
only that (complement top) has the property of being null, not that it is
identical with the class nil.  Since everything is an instance of top
(axiom), nothing is an instance of (complement top) (axiom for
complement), i.e., it is null (axiom for null).

-chris

### Perhaps there are some hitherto undocumented properties of the interpreter for KIF++ (I'm sure there are and that they need to surface in fact), but I stand by my earlier comment that without additional axioms and/or knowledge about the said interpreter it is not possible to prove from

(forall (?x) (instance-of ?x A)))

that

A equals top,

or

(= A top)

depending on the imagined "semantics" of your interpreter. I admit that all of the stuffin your ontology is intuitively clear, and I even understand most of it, but I prefer to behave as your deliberately obnoxious ignorant interpreter-- after all when your ontology hits the world at large you should maybe expect that to be the standard reader attitude... :-)

### so I still look forward to the proof, or axiom, that there is only one null class.

### this said, let me tell you unequivocally and sincerely that I think you did a great (and courageous) job in publishing this cold-pressed extra-virgin ontology for all of us to peek & poke at. But since it's there I feel ok in poking at it, and even to be wrong about that occasionally (but not this time though)

--Robert Meersman
