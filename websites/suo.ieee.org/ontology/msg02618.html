Comments:  I will consider here the relation between disk and peg,
for example 'disk 1 on peg B'

One can notice several way to expresse disk and piton.
Each linguistic formulation gave a specific determination
of the relation.  I interpreted that as giving a specific
context for the move.

The important effects are:

1)  The situational level

- emphasis on the peg in 13 with the repetition 'sur le B' in 13 and 14

at the first level, i.e. 'disk vert on peg B' 
                         'disk rose on peg B'

the emphasis (or focus) on peg B give a first structuration of the situation.
The corresponding linguistic formalization suggests that every disk susceptible
to move on peg B are equivalent.

- emphasis on disk jaune in 5 and 6, since disk 1 is located to it.
  I interpreted this as an attentional focusing on disk jaune since
  disk rose was put in the background.  This could be formalized as
  an inference drawn from disk 3 in this context.
  (S. thinks of 3 and disk 1 goes automatically)
 
These two effects show that organization in aggregates
is started at level of disks and also at level of pegs.
This organization depends on context.  N.B. it is the
same in 2 and 3 for disk vert.

- Another important effect is the creation of new elements
  as 'disk b' in 1 and disk C in 18

2)  At another level of analysis I took in account anaphoric relation 'le'.
    In this example it appears for every disk (except disk C), but it is not
    always the case.  I interpreted it as a selection of elements of the situation.
    Disks are detached from situation. I called this level 'enuntiative level'.
    One can notice there is no direct relation between selected disks at this
    level.  But it could have, and it is the case at trial 2.

But there is connectives 'ensuite' and 'alors' that cut the protocol
into episods.  So a set of independant disks -- and moves -- were
agregated in a whole;  Furthermore the verb 'reprendre' in 4,
different of verb 'prendre' suggests that disk rose is put in
the background, i.e. linked to others at enunciative level.

3)  Situational level works as context for enunciative level.
    One can notice that there is a link with emphasis on disk vert
    in 2 and 3 at situational level since at enunciative level there
    is the boundary between episods.

Later (in trial 3 and 4) with the presence of interjections
there is the construction of new operator of the problem space.
The construction can be explain with a parallel treatment at both
level enunciative and situational.  But before I have to give
you some other examples.  Tomorrow, I have to leave now.

Josiane,

Before I delve into the details of this protocol,
I need to preamble a bit on two preliminary points.

First, what are the larger reasons that we should be
interested in such things in a group that is devoted to
developing an engineering standard for computable ontology?

For my part, I would say that a general study of problem-solving
is very material to solving problems, and problems are something
of which we have a plenty here!  (<<<---<<< Big Interjection).
But is there really something so generic that it rises above
the "family dissemblances" of these seemingly diverse games?
That is a question that may take a thoughtful person not
a little while to answer.

Second, I need to know your attitude toward the circumstance that
the TOH game is not, game-theoreticaly speaking, a genuine game,
since there is no "strategy" to it, by which I mean that it has
a "deterministically unique minimal solution" (DUMS), and once
a person discovers that, then there is no more problem to it.

Bien à vous,

Jon Awbrey
