Matthew,

This is an important issue to me, as I'm supposed to be dealing with the
dynamics of systems that change in the intensions (properties) that they
possess over time, but let me recommend the position to which almost all
formal investigators that I have ever come across eventually settle down,
to wit, leave the sets and the spaces and all of the rest of those ideal
objects where they lie, on the virtual side of that Platonic mirror that
reflects whatever reality we hold up in front of it -- even if there are
those who would rather say that it's we who are the flecks in the mirror --
and merely interject the notion of a so-called "representative point" to
move through the set, the space, the universe, multiverse, transverse as
it may be.  Trust me -- it all works out much better in the end this way!

Jon Awbrey
