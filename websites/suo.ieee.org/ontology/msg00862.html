Jim,
I think that most , if not all, of the CYC upper ontology has been declared to
be available without charge.  Some portion of it (I think the parts that enable
it to work with CYC lower ontologies) is still private.  I know they reserve the
right to change the ontology without notice.

Asking for a copyright for all future versions in all possible media seems
excessive.  A letter  from CYC stating that IEEE may use this (whatever they
choose to make public) without payment seems reasonable.  I would suggest to
Doug that the terms of the release prohibit SUO / IEEE / ISO etc. from charging
for use of the SUO ontology or any ontologies that are derived from CYC in whole
or in part.  The prohibition for requiring payment for use should also include
administrative fees or development expenses, membership dues, etc.  Further the
release should not be transferable to other organizations or companies (profit
ot non-profit).  CYC may chose to do so in the future, but not IEEE.

Bob
