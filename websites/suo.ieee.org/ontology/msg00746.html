>Hi All,
>
>	Here's my response to comment #6.  The reason that I didn't refer to
>linguistically motivated ontologies like WordNet and EuroWordNet in
>developing the first two versions of the merged ontology is that I wanted a
>philosophically rigorous conceptual skeleton upon which we could hang more
>specific content, and one limitation of linguistically motivated ontologies
>is that they often contain concepts that are not precisly defined or that
>violate non-circularity constraints.  However, I for one think that these
>ontologies will be essential for fleshing out the skeleton that we are in
>the process of refining, and I am willing to devote a certain amount of my
>time to investigate the alignment of these ontologies with the merged
>ontology.

I agree strongly. Linguistically (and particularly *lexically*) 
motivated ontologies are not a good source for a coherent overall 
structure. WordNet in particular is not an ontology, but a compendium 
of textual relationships between lexical items in one natural 
language. For all my carping, I think Ian did exactly the right thing 
here.
