Matthew,

We seem to be ships passing in the night, and I would not think that the
differences can be worked by email in this forum without greatly
overtaxing the patience of others.  Giving, conversing, representing--
these activities mentioned in my posting below seem to me to be such
obvious examples of relations that are not adequately accounted for with
two elements, or concatenations of them, that I am wondering what your
notion of an example can be (something that eludes the token-type
conjugacy?) that you ask for more.  Could it be that we are addressing
different problems or assuming different contexts of discourse? (a real
not a rhetorical question)

Similarly, your willingness to posit full-blown possible worlds for each
and every infinitesimal variation seems to me to violate so grossly the
conceptual parsimony and exactitude that you value elsewhere that I again
question whether we are in the same context of discourse.  Surely, you
would not permit this move if you saw it as exponentially exploding even
the merest flicker of a possibility well beyond what is needed to account
for it.

In an SUO forum, it would indeed make sense to tie this discussion to
methodology for construction of a reference object, with an attempt at
transparency in relating design choices to purposes to be served. Many
differences would then be cut away demands of a common project. This
is why I repeat at every opportunity the desirability of a working paper
on method and a review of relevant best/existing practices, as well as the
use of what I believe is a common standards' term-- 'reference object'--
for what we can hope to achieve.   

I am mindful that when the question was posed (under the topic that Jim
Schoening labeled Funding Realism) whether there was anyone not on a
short-term government contract who could commit to the 4-5 year period to
develop and maintain a standard, you were the only person to respond
affirmatively. So I do appreciate that your persistence in smoking out
deep differences is implicitly tied to the project at hand.  

A few interlinear responses below underline the points above.

Best,

Lee
