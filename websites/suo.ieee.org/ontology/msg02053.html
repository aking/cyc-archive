MW: Well certainly we are aware of the degree of normalisation our 
models achieve (5th normal form, rather than the more usual 3rd normal
form considered sufficient for simple applications). But equally this
was not a driving force. More, we found that taking account of things
more precisely had benefits of this kind that were encouraging side
effects. It was business considerations of being able to meet 
requirements for information and its management that have been our
principle drivers. We set out with very pragmatic ambitions, and have
found increasing benefit in theoretical considerations (with a pragmatic
test of utility applied).
