Comments : I will consider here the relation between disk and peg, for
example 'disk 1 on peg B'

One can notice several way to expresse disk and piton. Each linguistic
formulation gave a specific determination of the relation.
I interpreted that as giving a specific context for the move

The important effects are :
1) The situational level
- emphasis on the peg in 13 with the repetition 'sur le B' in 13 and 14

at the first level, i.e. 'disk vert on peg B' 
                          'disk rose on peg B'
the emphasis (or focus) on peg B give a first structuration of the
situation. The corresponding linguistic formalization suggests that every
disk susceptible to move on peg B are equivalent.

- emphasis on disk jaune in 5 and 6, since disk 1 is located to it. I
interpreted this as an attentional focusing on disk jaune since disk rose
was put in the background. This could be formalized as an inference drawn
from disk 3 in this context. ( S. thinks of 3 and disk 1 goes automatically)

 
These two effects show that organization in agregates is started at level of
disks and also at level of pegs. This organization depends on context
N.B. it is the same in 2 and 3 for disk vert

- Another important effect is the creation of new elements as 'disk b' in 1
and disk C in 18

2) At another level of analysis I took in account anaphoric relation 'le'.
In this example it appears for every disk (except disk C), but it is not
always the case.
I interpreted it as a selection of elements of the situation. Disks are
detached from situation. I called this level 'enuntiative level'
One can notice there is no direct relation between selected disks at this
level. But it could have, and it is the case at trial 2.

But there is connectives 'ensuite' and 'alors' that cut the protocol into
episods. So a set of independant disks - and moves - were agregated in a whole;
Furthermore the verb 'reprendre' in 4, different of verb 'prendre' suggests
that disk rose is put in the background, i.e. linked to others at
enunciative level

3) Situational level works as context for enunciative level. One can notice
that there is a link with emphasis on disk vert in 2 and 3 at situational
level since  at enunciative level there is the boundary between episods.

Later (in trial 3 and 4) with the presence of interjections there is the
construction of new operator of the problem space. The construction can be
explain with a parallel treatment at both level enunciative and situational.
