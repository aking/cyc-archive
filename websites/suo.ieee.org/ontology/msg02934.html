At 09:30 23.07.2001 -0400, Jon Awbrey wrote:
>The term "reification" is a venerable old term from philosophy,
>the subject whereof it speaks often going about under the more
>formidable names of "hypostasis" or "hypostatic abstraction",
>at least two of which can be found in an ordinary dictionary.

We were aware of that fact when we took the very conscious decision
at our Paris meeting to grasp the bull by the horns and use the term.

Words have a way of changing their meanings over time. (Look at
"ontology", a venerable old term from philosophy if ever there was
one!) Better to accept that fact and make use of it (though obviously
not unthinkingly or indiscriminately).

Reification is here to stay, I think. And I do like it better
than "thingification" or "topification", don't you?

Steve
