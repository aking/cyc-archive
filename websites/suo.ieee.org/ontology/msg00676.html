Yes, it does have two opposing meanings.

Jargon File, via http://www.dict.org :

showstopper n.  A hardware or (especially) software bug that
  makes an implementation effectively unusable; one that absolutely has
  to be fixed before development can go on.  Opposite in connotation from
  its original theatrical use, which refers to something stunningly
  _good_.

(The "show" being stopped by the bugs is the launch of the product.  In
the case of major OS releases by Microsoft or Apple, the launch really is
a theatrical production.)
