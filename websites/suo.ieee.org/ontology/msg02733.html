Hi All,

	 I just wanted to give everyone an update on the Merged Ontology.
In line with the suggestion from many sources that we should be guided from
below rather than above in our ontological engineering, I've stripped down
the top level once again.  I've tried to eliminate all concepts that are not
necessary to support any of the content of the middle-level ontologies that
have been incorporated thus far.  The resulting high-level structure can be
summarized as follows (where the indentations indicate subclass-of
relations).

Entity
	Physical
		Object
		Process
	Relation
	Abstract
		Set
		Proposition
		Quantity
		Attribute

I invite everyone to examine these concepts and the ones subsumed by them in
our ontology browser (http://ontology.teknowledge.com/rsigma/).  

Aside from paring down the high-level structure of the Merged Ontology, I've
added and revised many of the docu-strings in the ontology, formulated many
new axioms, and tried to achieve a tighter integration of all of the content
in the ontology.  One area that still needs further integration is
mereotopology.  Currently, definitions and axioms are scattered in three
different sections of the ontology:  the mereotopology proper, the section
on holes based on Varzi and Casati's work, and the section on Positions
(everything in this section is a subrelation-of the BinaryRelation
position).  I welcome any criticisms of or comments on anything in the
current version of the Merged Ontology. 

-Ian
