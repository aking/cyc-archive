Factorization SIG,

Now that the joke about the "phantom limb extension" (PLE)
of sign relations has been aired out in public for a while,
I am becoming increasingly aware of its more objectionable
connotations.  At first, it seemed aptly felicitous enough
to call up our basic and common experiences with many forms
of projective objects and prospective objectives, with all
of the shades and the shadows of abductive ghosts, abstract
hypostases, apparent objects, apparitional reities, virtual
images, and that whole realm of beings on the other side of
the mirror.  But the limitations of the term to organa that
were once and are never to be again makes it seem like there
is no hope that the hypostatic hypothesis has any chance of
grasping a reality, and is thus forever and day 'religated'
to a deceitful, deceptive, elusive, ephemeral, evanescent,
and just plain false existence.  But that is not how it
is, in fact, since there are hypostatic hypotheses that
do grasp, reach, or at least reach toward real objects.

So I am going to change the terminology, once again, and call this brand of
transformation an "objective extension" (OE) or an "outward extension" (OE)
of the underlying sign relation.

This naturally raises the question of whether there might be such a brand of
conversion as an "interpretive extension" (IE) or an "inward extension" (IE)
of the underlying sign relation, and this is the topic that I consider next.

Like, next week, maybe,

Jon Awbrey

Graphical Conventions:

By way of representing projective relationships in this
ASCII-affine space, where only certain angles of lines
are allowed, to wit, elements of {"-", "/", "|", "\"},
I am using "parallel lines of identity" (PLOI's) to
collapse, equate, equivalence, identify, quotient --
or select your own favorite term of identification --
ostensibly multiple nodes into one, depicted like this:

      @
      =
      @
     ...     or     @=@=...=@=@
      =
      @
      =
      @

Typographical Conventions:

I will use "sharp brackets" around terms, as #A#, for bold face type.
I will use "scrip brackets" around terms, as $A$, for curl face type.
