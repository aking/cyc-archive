I guess I will blame this one on weekend logic --
the "y = x" condition is obviously necessary,
or else the return arrow will just not fly.

Jon Awbrey wrote:
 
> 3.  The "change of variables" formula:
>     For all x in X,  L(x) <=> [L(y) for some y in X] --
>     the introduction of "y = x" is just another distraction --
>     is a true thing to say about the application of a relation L
>     to a tuple x in a cartesian product space X, but it contributes
>     nothing non-trivial to the articulation of L as a relation, per se.

Jon Awbrey should have written:

> 3.  The "change of variables" formula:
>     For all x in X,  L(x) <=> [L(y) for some y = x in X]
>     is a true thing to say about the application of a relation L
>     to a tuple x in a cartesian product space X, but it contributes
>     nothing non-trivial to the articulation of L as a relation, per se.
