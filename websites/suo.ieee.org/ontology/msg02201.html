Dear Matthew, 
I wonder if the following isn't yet closer to what you have in mind.

   (<=> (gives3 ?d ?r &g)
        (and (gives2 ?x)
             (giver ?x ?d)
             (receiver ?x ?r)
             (given ?x ?g)
         )
    )     
             
Here 
?d suggests any "donor" (not just the donor John"), 
?r suggests "recipient", and
?g suggests "gift", and
?x suggests an act of giving.


(I assume that the "<=>" above is exemplary of
what the [irreducible] triadicity disagreement is about.)


From the above axiom, 
I think I can infer both equivalences below.


Relations contain tuples as follows:

gives2:
[X0]
[X1]

giver:
[X0 John]
[X1 John]

receiver:
[X0 Mary]
[X1 Ileana]

and given:
[X0 Book1]
[X1 Book1]


<=>


Relation (gives3) contains the tuples:

[John Mary Book1]
[John Ileana Book1]


<=>


Relations contain tuples as follows:

gives2:
[X0]

giver:
[X0 John]

receiver:
[X0 Mary]
[X0 Ileana]

and given:
[X0 Book1]





The gives3 situation clearly suggests that there were
two acts of giving.

The one reduction to a gives2 situation 
suggests that there were two acts of giving.
But a different reduction to a gives2 situation 
suggests that 
there was one act of giving with two recipients.

I would say that 
[John Mary Book1]
[John Ileana Book1]
is irreducibly triadic in the sense that it necessarily will have
more than one logically equivalent representation after being reduced.

More generally, from one diadic knowledge base and a
triadic reducibility axiom,
I can infer another diadic knowledge base.
Now I have to wonder if these two (and any others I can infer)
are logically equivalent.
(Intuitively, they aren't.)

But from the one triadic knowledge base, I only know what I know.


I propose that we not have any triadic reducibility axioms 
such as the following in our SUO.


(<=> (g3 ?d ?r &g)
        (and (g2 ?x)
             (gr ?x ?d)
             (rr ?x ?r)
             (gn ?x ?g)
         )
    ) 


If this is agreed, then
maybe we can stop talking about irreducible triadicity.

   -Fred Chase
