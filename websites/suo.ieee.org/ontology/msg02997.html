Bernard Vatant wrote (BV):
David Whitten wrote (DW):
Jon Awbrey wrote (JA):

BV: This method would be as rigourous as people using it :o)
    Seriously.  It's still an open field.  A successful work
    has been done to implement the Topic Maps concepts into
    XML DTD, which nobody here I suppose will consider like
    an "axiomatization" or a "formal theory".  In fact, the
    formal model -- or the conceptual model -- for Topic Maps
    is still in debate.

BV: A graph model is IMO the best candidate at the moment.
    But there are several pending points concerning the
    distinction between theory primitives and meta-level,
    recursivity and such.  There are not that many people
    from the "pure" formal logic community or other
    formalists in the TM community right now, and
    we struggle as best we can :)

DW: What kinds of inference would it support?

BV: To bring the TM concepts at the inference level needs I think
    a clarification of the above points.  OTOH, trying to express
    inference rules would maybe help the clarification process.
    As far as I know, nobody tried so far to express any formal
    set of axioms and inference rules in TM syntax, but I really
    think it's possible.  IMO propositions could be expressed as
    associations between primitive objects and inference rules as
    association types linking (properly reified) propositions.
    As I understand it, RDF folks are ploughing that field too;
    and there are many people eager to see convergences of all
    those efforts.

JA: I will focus on the points that perk up my ears.
    Permit me also to speak off-the-cuff, and allow
    you to address whatever hasty first impressions
    that I made have formed in surfing past some of
    the Topic Map shores.

JA: On the negative side of the balance beam, I get the impression of
    "yet another representation without a reasoning system" (YARWARS).
    We have already got plenty of those, and I despair at the thought
    of one more being added to the fray.  I earnestly await any datum,
    raw not meta, that you might bring to the table against this idea.

JA: On the positive side of the picture, I have just spent thirty-plus
    years working out how to "do logic" in graph-theoretical structure.
    If you are not the sort of person who thinks that you could likely
    re-create all of that work in a spare fortnight or two, then it is
    possible that we may find a problem or twelve to work out together.

JA: You might avail yourself of an introduction this work
    by tracing a few of the clues along the "PERS" thread:
