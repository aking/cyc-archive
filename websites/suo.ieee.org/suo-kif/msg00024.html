John-

Several times, I have pointed out your inaccurate statements regarding the status of the CG and KIF standards activities.  Again, I will remind you:

        - These documents had never achieve "draft standard" status, they were merely working papers.
        - They are not ANSI standards, but NCITS standards (assuming the work proceeds in NCITS/L8).
        - While these documents may proceed towards NCITS (and ANSI later on) and ISO/IEC standards, there is still *significant* work to do.
        - Given the amount of technical work necessary for the standard, it will easily take 1-2 years before you actually achieve some sort of "draft standard" (or committee draft, etc.) status.
        - In both NCITS/L8 and within ISO/IEC JTC1 SC32 WG2, you should be aware that you will need to develop, explain, educate, convince, and build consensus (just like any other committee).  This will take time, not just a meeting or two.

I encourage you become more familiar with the particular details of the process ... otherwise, you are likely to be disappointed in the progress and the level of satisfaction of your (and others') volunteer efforts.  For example, it is *highly unlikely* that the documents can be finished by 2001-03 NCITS/L8 meeting and approved at the 2001-04 SC32/WG2 meeting.  I would be happy to explain further via a followup phone call.
