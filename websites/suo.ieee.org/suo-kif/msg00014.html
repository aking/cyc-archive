Hello Matthew,
you misunderstood Adam's message. He is talking about the BNF grammar
for KIF, so that `number' is a syntactic object, just like `word'.
He is NOT talking about a theory of numbers; KIF does not contain
any theory of numbers or any other ontology. Imaginary and rational numbers
belong more appropriately to an ontology that is written in KIF.

An analogy -- simply because the grammar allows the string `cat' does not
mean that KIF contains a theory of cats.

- michael
