All,

I see there is a small error in the FOL table: the "set inclusion" symbol
between the interpretation of a sentence and the truth set should be an
"element member-of" symbol. I have attached the corrected FOL table.

I will take this opportunity to make a few further comments on the symbology
in the FOL table.
    The set of tuples over a set A is defined to be the disjoint union
tuple(A) =def {A^n | n a natural number}.
    The unsorted 1st order model here can be consider a one-sorted model,
where the domain D = dom(M) = I[*] is the interpretation of a single sort,
say "*" -- I used this notation in the comment about SUO-KIF semantics
[http://grouper.ieee.org/groups/ltsc/logs/suo-kif/msg00000.html], where the
interpretation symbol was "SUO", not "I"].
    Since this model theory is part of set theory, I have used the terms
"element" and "member", not "individual", to denote the interpretation of
constants.
    I presume that John Sowa's term "relational structure" in the message on
basic terminology [http://ltsc.ieee.org/logs/suo/msg02398.html] refers to
the interpretation of predicate symbols. As such, the relations need to be
indexed by the predicate symbols, for otherwise there is no connection to
the logical language. In the FOL table this indexing is performed by the
interpretation.
    John does not discuss the important notion of a logical theory, but I
presume that the notion of an ontology is synonymous with this. I also
presume that John's statement "The ontology is completely determined by the
choice of D and R" refers to the unique theory determined by a model, the
set of all sentences that the model satisfies -- what is referred to as the
"theory of a model" th(M) in the FOL table.

Reference:
Enderton, Herbert B. "A Mathematical Introduction to Logic", Academic Press,
1972.
