> states, "Every draft version of a standard has to be labeled with the
> appropriate copyright notices."

I'm suggesting exactly that.  The first part of this proposed license is
just the IEEE copyright, which needs to be there.  The part I object to is
the material following the header "License for SUMO"

>
>         If you feel this copyright statement will place undesirable
> restrictions on anyone, could you give us some specific examples of what
> couldn't be done.

I believe I have already.  As I read this current proposal, it would
prevent me, or anyone else, from altering SUMO.  The phrase "but not alter"
is the primary problem.

As Matthew pointed out, the issue should be not whether one can alter SUMO,
but whether one could alter it and claim conformance.  The latter should
indeed be prohibited, but the former should not.  Note that as a draft, it
appears the IEEE policy is that drafts can't have conforming uses, only
actual approved standards can, if I'm understanding their text correctly.
