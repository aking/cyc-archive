We can't make progress unless we can work with the latest proposed draft.
    As I said, IEEE's license is what we currently have to work with but
is not completely clear .  If IEEE will accept a few clarifying tweaks,
I think we can all be happy.
    I suspect their intention was to allow working group members to
experiment, make suggestions to the technical editor, who then produces
the next proposed draft.  But is it definitely unclear what outsiders
can and cannot do with a downloaded copy.  That seems to largely revolve
around how such outsiders represent their derivative version--it should
not be confused with IEEE's SUMO.
