   I prefer that we retain it and solicit other resource pages to add to that section.  The Teknowledge page is where most of this material originated and that page will continue to evolve over time.  It also has material that does not appear on your site.  I see no harm in mentioning many SUMO pages.  That shows more depth for SUMO than if we point to only your page and no other.
   In line with the above, I have no issue with you adding your SUMO page to the resource section.  I suggest we adopt a length limit of a short paragraph. A resource listing should say what the site is about, and optionally mention/link what is most unique and interesting about the site.  Resource listings that go on for more than about 1/4 or 1/3 of a screen and dozens of links tend to overwhelm the reader and lose interest.  I suggest resource listings should serve to identify SUMO-related sites and pique the interest sufficiently to induce the reader to go and explore. Your translation templates have some interest, for example, but listing each and every ontology on your site does not seem productive.  Could you just say you have a number of SUMO-based ontologies and leave it at that?
