Dear Graham,

I think there are two options to be considered:

1. A minimal grammar and language that is iso-morphic to SUO-KIF, but is
aimed at being human interpretable, along the lines of some of my
translations of KIF into basic English. This would use variables and
probably namespaces much as KIF does/ might do, and would not appear very NL
like, but would be relatively easy to understand.

2. Something ACE like, that aims to maximise the closeness to full natural
language.

I think the former can be done in a relatively straight forward way. You
really just need a translater to and from KIF. I could probably undertake to
prepare a draft of the ebnf (but I'm not sure what the time-scale would be).

For the latter, I see no point in doing something more or less than simply
using ACE. ACE must have an internal format, and this will support FOL.
Again, the interesting thing is whether this can be translated to KIF. My
guess is that some of it can but not all. You could identify a subset of ACE
that does translate to KIF, and make that a conformance class of ACE.

We can pursue either or both of these. My personal view is that we get 80%
of the benefit for 20% of the effort if we follow option 1, and I can see
quite some challenges even there. For example, it seems to me that how you
choose to name relations is important.

Equally I see value in the latter approach too, and whilst I do not have the
energy for it, I would not wish to discourage others.

Regards  
      Matthew
