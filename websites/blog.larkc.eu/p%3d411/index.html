European Semantic Technologies Conference

(by Frank)

http://blogs.zdnet.com/semantic-web/?p=199 Focussing on the final panel. Interesting quotes:

  • “hide your technology” (by multiple speakers on the panel). 
  • A very LarKC specific comment by Mark Greaves (Vulcan, on our Advisory Board): “The cost of semantic data creation is tending toward zero. The quantityof semantic data is going toward infinity. The amount of ‘bad data’ is growing. Better publishing and inferencing tools, he suggested, would make it more straightforward for the data being created to be ‘good.’ “
  • “startups in the US stand on the shoulders of the generosity of European project funding.” (again, Mark Greaves)
  • The panel’s closing comments were well in line with some of the dominant sounds at SemTech last May in San Jose: “In closing remarks, many on the panel stressed the key importance of building a compelling (and complexity-disguising) user experience”.
