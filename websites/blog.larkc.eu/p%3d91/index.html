Where does the business value of Semantic Technologies come from?

(by Frank)

Leo Sauermann from DFKI took a close look at all the seventeen case studies of applied Semantic Web in industry settings that were collected by the W3C Semantic Web Education and Outreach SWEO Interest Group, and tabulated them on where their value-contribution was coming from.

An interesting exercise.
Details at http://www.dfki.uni-kl.de/~sauermann/2008/04/benefits,
Executive summary from Leo below

Many benefits were classified by me into “profit” - hence people use semantic web to improve their business.

  • The first five benefits of the technology are crucial: data integration accross sources, expressive queries, taxonomy/multilingual, accessible for users, navigation and serendipity. If you start a project, you should try to get benefits in these areas.
  • Inferencing was not mentioned very often - although this can be of any reason (i.e. people forgot to mention it) it can also show that inference is important, but the web-based data integration features of RDF are also worth a lot. A little amount of semantics can go a long way.
  • The last two points, the web of data, and globally unique identifiers, are going to be much more important in the future and are a growing area.
  • Focussing on the last point, I want to stress that given the Linking Open Data project (also kind-of-initiated by SWEO), you now have a very good overview of the publicly available Semantic Web data. People want automated features and to reduce workload on users, you must reuse the data that is out there and I would give the prophecy that your business value will increase if you reuse this data.
