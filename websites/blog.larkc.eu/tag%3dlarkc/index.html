LarKC in the World

Thursday, March 31st, 2011

Although LarKC is based in Europe, the project of building, and applying, web-scale reasoning is world wide. One of the most exciting things about living in a connected world, and a world of abundant, location independent computational resources, is that people anywhere in the world can do world class AI research, and develop applications based on that research. The recent, and very rapid, increase in internet bandwidth going into Africa means that one can now use Shazam to get impromptu karaoke lyrics for the Texas country-and-western playing in a hotel bar in Accra. It also means that previously isolated African researchers can make a full contribution to the advance of semantic technology.  In February, partially supported by the FP7 Active project, we had the opportunity to present LarKC, and the potential benefits of AI and human-computer collaboration, to students and researchers at the Ghana-India Kofi Annan Centre of Excellence in ICT in Ghana. Discussion following the talks was lively, with great local ideas for the application of AI in knowledge capture from small farmers, and resource allocation for rural health care. Video from some talks is being made available on VideoLectures.net, there was good coverage from the local media, and we look forward to building a collaboration with our new colleagues.

3rd LarKC Early Adopters Tutorial at ESWC 2010

Wednesday, January 27th, 2010

(by Mick Kerrigan, STI Innsbruck)

The 3rd edition of the LarKC Early Adopters Tutorial has been accepted at the 7th Extended Semantic Web Conference (ESWC10). The tutorial as always will be a full day event that provides participants with early access to the results of the LarKC project. Having completed the tutorial, participants will be able to build plug-ins to the LarKC platform, create new workflows by combining existing LarKC plug-ins together, and will have a thorough overview of the LarKC approach. The tutorial will take place on the 30th of May 2010 on the island of Crete in Greece and registration for the tutorial can be made through the ESWC10 website. We have learned a lot about how to present the LarKC content at the previous tutorials at ESWC09 and ISWC09, so if you have not been able to attend a tutorial so far we hope you will be able to join us in Crete! More information on the agenda for the tutorial can be found on the early adopters section of the LarKC website.

LarKC meets the largest: LarKC Dissemination in China 2009

Thursday, October 1st, 2009

(by Zhisheng Huang)

My China journey started on August 15 and ended on Sept 15, 2009. Because of the H1N1 flu control in China, I was requested to stay at home for one week and not allowed to enter any university campus after my arrival in China. Thus, I took the flight to the city where my parents are living after I arrived in Beijing on August 15.

My dissemination activities started on August 23, 2009 actually. From August 23 till Sept 15, I gave eighteen lectures and invited talks in China within the three weeks. Through the journey, I was surprised by the phenomenon that Chinese researchers and students are so eager to learn logics and the semantic technology. I was really touched by the younger generation of the Chinese, who are keen to learn the knowledge of modern science and technology.

My first activity was to give a series of lectures in the Chinese Semantic Web Summer School in Nanjing from August 24 till August 28. More than 110 researchers and students who are from about 50 China universities attended the summer school. Most of them are PhD students. Many students had to pay the accommodation and the travel expenses by themselves, without any financial support from their own universities. My eight lectures in the summer school cover the topics of i) Introduction to modern logics, ii) Introduction to Description Logics, iii) Decidability and complexity of Description Logics, iv) Knowledge representation and reasoning, v) Introduction to the Semantic Web, vi) Ontology reasoning and management, vii) LarKC and Web scale reasoning, and viii) Research topics in the Semantic Web.

From August 29 till August 31 I co-chaired the Chinese Semantic Web Symposium (CSWS09), which was co-located with the Chinese Semantic Web Summer School 2009. CSWS09 was held in the beautiful campus of the Southeast University, Nanjing. About 100 Chinese researchers/scientists attended the conference. 18 papers out of 95 submissions were accepted for the paper presentation in CSWS09. That made CSWS one of the highly competitive conferences in the Semantic Web. The scalability and Web scale reasoning were frequently discussed in the talks and the panel of CSWS09.

After my visit in Nanjing, I went to visit Jiangsu University of Science and Technology in Zhenjiang, a city where it is not very far away from Nanjing. In Zhenjiang I gave three lectures and an invited talk to talk about LarKC and its relevant technologies (i.e. the Semantic Web and ontology engineering) from Sept 1 till Sept 8.

My visit in Beijing (from Sept 9 till Sept 15) was a very busy week in my China journey.
That includes an invited talk in the Institute of Scientific and Technical Information of China in the afternoon of Sept 10, an invited talk in Beijing young logician seminar in the afternoon of Sept 12, an invited talk in Tsinghua University, one of the top universities in China in the afternoon of Sept 14, and a series of lectures in the International Web Intelligence Institute (WICI), Beijing University of Technology, one of the partners in the LarKC Consortium.

Colliding with your bootstraps

Wednesday, April 29th, 2009

(by Michael Witbrock)

Cycorp Europe’s sister organisation in the US has been participating in Bootstrapped Learning, a project to enable AI systems to be instructed using lessons that build on one another over time. Now the program is starting a competition to enable AI researchers around the world to drive this sort of learning forward.  This sort of research is one of the reasons we’re building the LarKC platform - to allow people to work on interesting, difficult problems in AI without having to build all the infrastructure themselves. It’d be great to see researchers using LarKC to build entries for the BL Cup, or for  TPTP. If you’d like to get started, perhaps you’re a  LarKC early adopter.

Real Time Cities

Sunday, July 13th, 2008
Image via Wikipedia

It seems that many cities may be starting to generate data suitable for LarKC scale processing. This autumn, San Francisco is apparently installing sensors on about a quarter of its parking spaces, with the aim of maintaining 85% occupation and dropping driving by about 30%. As someone who has often tried to find a parking space in S.F. this seems like a very good idea.
