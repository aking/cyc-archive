SPARQL benchmark and SW statistics

Friday, October 17th, 2008

(by Eyal)

During the LarKC meeting, I mentioned the recently published Berlin benchmark of RDF stores and RDBs on SPARQL queries, a good overview of some of the current triple stores. Note again that the results for the relational mappings (eg MySQL, D2R) are not directly comparable, since these use a fixed representation of the domain (they cannot store arbitrary triples). That may be enough functionality in many cases, but it’s not the same as a generic triple store.

Also interesting, a recent paper on Semantic Web statistics, in a history of other papers to investigate properties of decentralised SW data and to learn how and what to count. I believe analysing datasets is growing into a really challenging and interesting task.

Semantic mashup in the life sciences

Friday, October 3rd, 2008

by Angus

Journal of Biomedical Informatics

Volume 41, Issue 5,  Pages 683-860 (October 2008)

Semantic Mashup of Biomedical Data

This will be of interest to LarKC-ers, given our use cases - a JBI special issue on the Semantic Mashup of Biomedical Data. There is a huge number of biomed data sources (Vassil gave a count of > 1000 at the Amsterdam meeting), and a constant need to integrate and work across several at once. For our GWA use case, for example, we will need to integrate and query over half a dozen data sources, together with text annotated against half a dozen others.

The special issue editorial talks about how the usual integration “by hand” (i.e. ad-hoc perl scripts) is now moving towards the use and integration of data sources with defined semantics, in rdf and owl, queried using sparql etc. The various articles give examples of such mashups - at first sight, using varying degrees of semantic technology.

Perhaps I’ll get round to reading some of the papers and posting a bit more…
