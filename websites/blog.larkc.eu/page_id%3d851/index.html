Semantic Bookmarks

Internet users can store, organize, search, and manage bookmarks of web pages on the Internet with the help of metadata using semantic bookmarks, typically in the form of tags that collectively and/or collaboratively become a folksonomy. A semantic social bookmarking provides every social bookmarks the means to share and communicate based on semantically interoperable representative formalism. These bookmarks are usually public, and can be saved privately, shared only with specified people or groups, shared only inside certain networks, or another combination of public and private domains based on semantic technology. Some semantic social bookmarking services also draw inferences from the relationship of tags to create clusters of tags or bookmarks. In a semantic bookmarking system, users save links to web pages that they want to remember and/or share and can exchange the meaning thru semantically structured tags.Also, semantic social bookmarking can be useful as a way to access a consolidated set of bookmarks from various computers, organize large numbers of bookmarks, and share bookmarks with contacts beyond just one site. Libraries have found semantic social bookmarking to be useful as an easy way to provide lists of informative links to patrons.
