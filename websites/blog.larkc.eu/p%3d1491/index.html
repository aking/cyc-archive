Squared: Google does structured data, in spite of being openly against it previously

(by Jose Quesada)

Well, this is a surprise. Google opens a new service from Google labs that does look like structured data… but really strange. I have no idea how they decided that the best way to present structured data was in a spreadsheet format. Example:

Berlin - Google Squared

Not very impressive, but intriguing. One can add more columns, or more items. The 10th item on this Berlin Square happens to be about tropical Islands. How?

It seems that the assumption here is that people would want some kind of spreadsheet full of data.

This looks more like a reaction to Microsoft Bing and Wolfran Alpha.

Reviews are not very positive:

The information is simply inexplicable. Apart from the newspapers image and description Google Squared also came up with their Justice, Pakistan, and Games.

On the other hand, it does really well on more specific searches:

Haruki Murakami

The results are the most interesting books of this author. For books, they seem to be scraping and parsing Amazon, since the extra columns are really familiar (ISBN, star ratings). Now things become interesting. This has high remixability potential. Fortunately, Google gets the web and doesn’t try to hide these results from spiders by offering results as .gif files, like Alpha.

Digging up a bit more:

East Europe countries

Well, these are good results. Mashable quickly finds a good niche for it:

Based on our initial use, we see Google Squared being a potentially huge time saver for professionals and consumers who need to do quick comparisons. It’s incredibly useful if you know what you’re looking for.

So, with an API, this could be a great addition to the open linked data initiative. SemWeb detractors can no longer say that there’s not enough structured data out there to be of any relevance. Structured data is now mainstream. Thanks, Google.
