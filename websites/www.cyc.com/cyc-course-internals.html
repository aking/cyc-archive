The Cyc Canonicalizer: System Internals & Finding Assertions

Thursday 9:40-10:10 -- Lecture

This talk will introduce the audience to the Cyc canonicalizer. The canonicalizer is one of the subsystems that comprise the Cyc inference engine. Its mission is to translate formulas from the conventional notation of first-order logic into canonical forms that are used by the Cyc inference engine. This target audience of this talk includes all potential users of, and contributors to, the Cyc knowledge base. The goals of this talk include:

  • explaining why and when and how formulas are canonicalized
  • identifying how the representation of formulas may be changed by the canonicalizer, and explainng how users can find formulas they have just entered in spite of these changes
  • emphasizing the dependence of canonicalization on the Cyc knowledge base

Beginning Inferencing

Thursday 1:00-2:15 -- Lecture

The purpose of this section is to explain how the assertions in Cyc's Knowledge Base can be accessed and used in order to reason about queries that cannot be directly answered by looking up existing assertions. This process is commonly known as inference and the mechanism that performs these operations within Cyc is called the `inference engine'.

Cyc's inference engine treats inference as a search problem in the space of possible proofs over canonicalized Knowledge Base assertions and their possible instantiations. It tries to establish whether what Cyc already knows supports the assertion that is entered as a query or whether there are any possible instantiations of the variables in the query that would turn it into an assertions that Cyc can conclude from what it already knows. The inference engine relies on an implementation of the method of resolution to decide which instantiations are acceptable.

Human users can control the behavior of the inference engine's search in proof space by setting several parameters that determine, among other things, how `deep' or `shallow' the search can be, how much time at most it can take, and so on. Several heuristics are used to guide the search of the inference engine and make recommendations as to which branches of the search are most likely to lead up to an answer. In addition, certain special cases of inference (for example inferences having to do with closure properties of the #$genls and #$isa predicates) are delegated by the inference engine to specialized module that can handle such inferences much more efficiently than the inference engine itself.

HL Modules & KB Indexing for Programmers

Thursday 1:30-2:15 -- Lecture -- Programmer Track

Cyc is a large knowledge base with tens of thousands of constants interrelated by hundreds of thousands of assertions and logical deductions. The purpose of this talk is elaborate on two important mechanisms used to manage this complexity efficiently -- HL modules and KB indexing.

HL modules are the hundreds of independent code modules which perform inference in Cyc. Each module is designed to perform one specialized form of inferencing as efficiently as possible. The talk will introduce the user to HL modules, explain the motivation for them, and describe how they interact with inference and truth maintenance in Cyc.

KB indexing allows for subsets of the knowledge base with an important common property to be efficiently identified and accessed. The talk will describe the kinds of indexing currently used in Cyc and the interaction of indexing with knowledge representation and inference.
