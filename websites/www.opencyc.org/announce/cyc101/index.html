Cyc 101 Training
February 24-26, 2002

Cyc 101: Introducing Cyc and OE

"OE" stands for "Ontological Engineering", a term coined at Cycorp which basically means representing the world in a form that Cyc can use. This introductory Cyc course will introduce the tools used in Ontological Engineering and provide ample opportunity to use those tools to begin entering knowledge into Cyc.

Cyc 101 will be a three-day hands-on workshop with minimal time devoted to lecture. The instructor will give a brief explanation of some aspect of Cyc and then the students will be given time to apply that knowledge. Our aim is to have students spend as much time as possible interacting with Cyc. When students leave the class, they will feel comfortable with how to find and use the tools in Cyc/OpenCyc, they will have a strong basic understanding of how to find and make use of relevant KB content, and they will be able to enter simple facts and rules without assistance.

The second and third days will include an hour after lunch called "Lab Time". This is an hour which is devoted to giving you the opportunity to meet one-on-one with Cycorp employees to work through challenges or to meet with other Cyc 101 participants to share ideas and get an idea of how you might apply what you've learned when you get back to the office.

The morning of the third day will optionally consist of a lengthy assignment to ontologize a paragraph of text or it will serve as a chance to meet in small groups with various Cycorp employees for in-depth question-and-answer sessions.

Cycorp plans to offer Cyc 102: Advanced OE and Programming in the Spring. You can request to be notified about specific Cyc 102 plans by contacting training@cyc.com.
